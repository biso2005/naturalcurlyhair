### Gap 3 — Humectant Behaviour in Cold/Low-Humidity Conditions Is Ignored
Multiple results recommend glycerin-heavy products with no seasonal caveat. In UK winters (indoor RH can drop to 30–40%), glycerin can draw moisture *out* of the hair shaft rather than into it. No current result explains this mechanism or tells readers how to adjust by season.

**Sourced:** Chemists Corner (Perry Romanowski, cosmetic chemist) — glycerin absorbs 25% of its weight in water at 50% RH; below that threshold the direction reverses and humectants pull moisture out of the hair rather than in. UK indoor winter context: centrally heated homes frequently drop to 30–35% RH in January. A reader using glycerin-heavy leave-ins in a centrally heated flat in winter may be actively drying their hair with a product that works fine in summer. The product didn't change — the air did. Cite Chemists Corner for the hygroscopicity figure; cite UK indoor humidity data for seasonal context.
