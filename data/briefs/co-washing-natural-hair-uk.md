## 4. Gaps in Current Top Results to Exploit

### Gap 1 — No one addresses UK hard water
Every top result treats co-washing as a universal method. Hard water (prevalent across London, the South East, and the Midlands) causes mineral deposits that interact directly with the conditioning agents in co-washes, worsening buildup. This is testable, documentable, and entirely absent from the top 10.

### Gap 2 — The "for or against" debate lacks evidence
The top results split into pro-co-washing and anti-co-washing camps (Afrocenchix is a clear outlier). Neither side presents structured test data. A documented test — same tester, same regimen, tracked across four weeks — fills this gap with something no current result offers.

### Gap 3 — No practical failure-state guidance
Existing guides explain how to co-wash but not how to recognise when it has stopped working for your hair. No top result gives readers a symptom checklist (dullness, hygral fatigue, persistent itching, product smell retention) or a decision tree for switching methods. This keeps readers dependent on guesswork rather than building genuine hair literacy.
