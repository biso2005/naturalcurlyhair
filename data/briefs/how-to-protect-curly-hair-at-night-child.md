## 4. Gaps in Current Top Results to Exploit

### Gap 1 – No original evidence or testing
Every top result gives opinion-based advice. None document a test, comparison or result. Publishing a structured comparison (fabric type × protective method × curl outcome) with photos or a results table creates a durable differentiator and satisfies the site's testing brief.

### Gap 2 – Age and compliance are almost entirely ignored
Results treat "child" as one audience. The practical challenge for a parent of a wriggling two-year-old is completely different from that of a nine-year-old who can be taught a routine. No result segments advice by age or tackles the behavioural dimension of getting a young child to cooperate. This is a real parent pain point visible in the Reddit and Facebook threads but unaddressed in editorial content.

### Gap 3 – Pineappling is recommended without qualification
Multiple results recommend pineappling as a near-universal solution. It is ineffective on short hair, fine hair, or looser curl patterns. Acknowledging this honestly – and offering the right alternative for each case – builds trust and improves practical usefulness. It also targets the longtail queries from parents whose child's hair is "too short to pineapple."
