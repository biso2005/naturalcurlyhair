## 3. Products to Consider (Generic)

> **Do not make claims about specific brands. Writer must test and verify.**

- **Chelating shampoos** — look for EDTA (disodium or tetrasodium EDTA) or phytic acid in the ingredient list; these bind calcium and magnesium ions
- **Clarifying shampoos** — surfactant-heavy formulas that remove product buildup but *not* mineral ions; note the difference in use case
- **Shower filters** — inline filters claiming to reduce limescale; writer must test water hardness before and after with a calibrated TDS meter or British Water test strips
- **Citric acid powder** — widely available, used as a DIY chelating rinse; cheap to test
- **Apple cider vinegar** — common recommendation; writer to test pH effect and curl result vs. citric acid
- **Leave-in conditioners with humectants** — relevant because hard water inhibits moisture absorption; not a fix but a compensatory step worth testing
- **Deep conditioners** — hard water reduces conditioner efficacy; writer to test same product in hard vs. filtered water
