## 4. Gaps in Current Top Results to Exploit

**Gap 1 — No original data anywhere**
Every ranking result offers opinions or general advice. None presents measured breakage evidence. A piece with even a basic structured test (e.g. shed count across two style methods over four weeks on the same hair type) will be genuinely unique and citable.

**Gap 2 — The "how long you wear it" variable is ignored**
Results focus on style type but nobody addresses duration and maintenance frequency as independent breakage variables. A tight style worn for three days behaves differently to the same style worn for three weeks with no re-moisturising. This is a clear content angle with practical value.

**Gap 3 — Hairline and edges are underserved**
Most articles treat hair breakage as uniform across the head. The edges and nape are disproportionately vulnerable due to finer hair texture and repeated tension points. No top-10 result dedicates meaningful space to this. A focused H3 or callout box here addresses a real concern frequently raised in community forums.
