## 4. Gaps in Current Top Results to Exploit

**Gap 1 — No plain-English legal analysis specific to locs**
Results either quote the Equality Act broadly (GOV.UK, ACAS) or discuss dreadlocks anecdotally (Reddit, Neathouse). No result walks through *exactly* how s.19 indirect discrimination applies to a locs policy, what the employer must justify, and what the proportionality test looks like in practice. Own this.

**Gap 2 — Halo Code is presented as stronger than it is**
Multiple results list the Halo Code as though signing it creates legal obligation. It does not. A frank, accurate breakdown of what the Code does and does not protect — backed by a quote from an employment solicitor — would stand apart immediately.

**Gap 3 — Zero original testimony or documented experience**
Every top result is either a legal directory, a government page, or a Reddit thread. Nobody has spoken to UK workers who have actually raised a grievance over locs and documented the outcome. A short, verified case study (even one person's account, with their permission) creates content no competitor can copy without doing the same work.
