## 4. Gaps in Current Top Results to Exploit

### Gap 1 — No Original Testing Evidence
Every top result gives prescriptive steps with zero stated evidence. Nobody documents curl types tested, number of children, what failed, or how long they followed up. A single article with honest, methodical testing notes — even on two or three children across different curl patterns — immediately outranks on trust and E-E-A-T signals.

### Gap 2 — No UK-Specific Environmental Context
All ranking content is US-authored. No result addresses hard water (prevalent across much of England), central heating dryness in autumn/winter, or the fact that UK ambient humidity behaves differently across seasons. A section grounded in British conditions is both accurate and a genuine differentiator.

### Gap 3 — No Age or Developmental Nuance
Every result treats "child" as one category. Toddler scalps produce less sebum; pre-teens may begin producing more. Fine toddler hair behaves differently from a seven-year-old's more established curl pattern. No competitor draws this distinction. Writer must research or note clearly where guidance is pattern-based versus age-based, and flag what is anecdotal.
