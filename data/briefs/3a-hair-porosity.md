## 4. Gaps in Current Top Results to Exploit

**Gap 1: No result addresses why 3A specifically behaves differently under porosity rules**
Most results treat curl type and porosity as entirely separate topics and never connect them. 3A strands — often fine, with a loose spiral — absorb and release moisture differently than tighter curl types at the same porosity level. This intersection is unaddressed and is where our original testing adds direct value.

**Gap 2: No one critiques the float test for fine 3A strands**
Every result either endorses the float test uncritically or ignores tests altogether. Fine 3A hair is light enough to float regardless of porosity, making this test unreliable for this curl type. A tested, honest breakdown of which methods are accurate for 3A specifically would own this angle.

**Gap 3: Routines are generic, not porosity-and-curl-type specific**
The Curlsmith and HeyCurls guides give solid general porosity advice but do not tailor product layering, application technique, or frequency to 3A curl structure. A routine section built specifically around 3A porosity combinations — tested by real 3A-haired people — fills a clear, demonstrable gap.
