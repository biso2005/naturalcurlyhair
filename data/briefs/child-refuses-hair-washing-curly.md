## 4. Gaps in Current Top Results to Exploit

### Gap 1 — Pain, not psychology, is usually the root cause
Current results (Reddit, Mumsnet, Peaceful Parent) frame refusal almost entirely as emotional or behavioural. None explicitly address that **tangled curly hair causes real physical pain during washing**, which means distraction tactics alone will fail. A brief section on *removing the pain stimulus first* is absent everywhere.

### Gap 2 — No curl-type-specific guidance
Every result gives generic toddler-hair advice. The FroBabies post comes closest but is shallow. There is a clear opening for advice that distinguishes **wavy (2c), curly (3a–3c), and coily (4a–4c)** hair needs — different detangling tools, different water-to-conditioner ratios, different sectioning methods.

### Gap 3 — Sensory processing is mentioned nowhere in practical terms
The PDF result flags sensory issues but gives no actionable next steps. No result tells parents **what signs distinguish ordinary dislike from sensory sensitivity**, or when to seek an occupational therapist referral. This is both a content gap and a trust-building opportunity.
