- **High porosity:** protein treatments (hydrolysed keratin, rice protein — note protein sensitivity risk), heavier butters and oils as sealants, low-sulphate or sulphate-free cleansers, acidic rinses (ACV) to smooth the cuticle temporarily
- **Both types:** microfibre towels or cotton T-shirt drying, wide-tooth combs for wet detangling

## 4. Gaps in Current Top Results to Exploit

1. **No original testing data exists in any top-10 result.**
Every ranking page is advisory opinion. A page showing documented at-home porosity tests run on real 3C testers — with honest outcomes and failure notes — is immediately differentiated and builds genuine E-E-A-T.

2. **Mixed porosity is absent.**
Not one top result addresses the common reality that 3C hair frequently shows low porosity at the root and high porosity at the ends (particularly with colour-treated or heat-styled hair). This is a concrete topical gap with clear search intent behind it.

3. **No one distinguishes damage-induced from genetic high porosity.**
All current high-porosity content treats it as a single condition. The distinction changes the entire routine recommendation — protein treatments help chemically damaged high-porosity hair but may be unnecessary or counterproductive for naturally porous hair. Covering this with a sourced explanation is a clear quality signal.
