## 4. Gaps in Current Top Results to Exploit

### Gap 1 — No differentiation by loc age or maturity
Every top result treats "removing locs" as a single scenario. A loc that is six months old behaves completely differently from a five-year mature loc. **Exploit this** by building a clear decision framework around loc age, with verified observations on how the internal structure differs (loose coils still present vs. fully fused cortex). Writer must verify or source this from a loctician or hair scientist.

### Gap 2 — Zero honest data on hair loss
Results either ignore hair loss or offer false reassurance ("you'll keep all your hair!"). This is the primary fear of the searcher and no result addresses it with any measured, tested evidence. **Exploit this** with an honest tested account: photograph or document shed counts per loc, distinguish between normal telogen shed hairs (white bulb visible) and mid-shaft breakage, and give a frank verdict on acceptable vs. alarming loss rates. This is the clearest trust-building opportunity in the SERP.

### Gap 3 — No aftercare depth
Every result ends at "wash your hair." Newly unlocked natural hair — especially 4b/4c — is typically severely dehydrated, has compressed curl patterns, and may show signs of hygral fatigue from prolonged moisture-lock inside the loc. **Exploit this** by covering a proper aftercare protocol: moisture restoration timeline, whether a protein treatment is appropriate (writer must verify — timing matters), and what to expect from the curl pattern in weeks one through four.
