## 4. Gaps in Current Top Results to Exploit

**Gap 1 — No tested frequency guidance**
All top results focus on the single detangling session. None answer "how often should I do this?" Toddlers who are detangled too rarely develop matting; too often risks damage. Writer must research and state a defensible frequency based on curl type.

**Gap 2 — Distraction tactics treated as throwaway lines**
Competing content mentions distraction (tablet, snack) in one sentence. No one ranks tactics by time bought or age-appropriateness. A short, honest ranking (e.g. "new episode of favourite show = 12 minutes; snack = 4 minutes") would be genuinely useful and unique.

**Gap 3 — No distinction between curl types in the method**
Results treat all toddler curls as identical. A 2B wave and a 4C coil require meaningfully different product amounts, tool choices, and section sizes. Writer should address at minimum two distinct curl ranges with adapted advice.
