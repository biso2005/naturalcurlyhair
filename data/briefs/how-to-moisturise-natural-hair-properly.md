## 3. Products to Consider (Generic)

> **Writer instruction:** Do not name specific brands or make efficacy claims. List ingredient categories and formats only.

- **Water-based leave-in conditioners** — confirm water (aqua) is first ingredient; note aloe vera juice is also an acceptable humectant base
- **Lightweight oils for sealing** — e.g. grapeseed, sweet almond, argan; suitable for low-porosity or fine strands
- **Heavier oils and butters** — e.g. castor oil, shea butter, mango butter; suitable for high-porosity or coarse strands
- **Deep conditioners with humectants** — look for glycerin, honey, panthenol in ingredients
- **Protein-free options** — relevant for readers already in moisture overload; explain what to look for on label
- **Hard water filter or chelating shampoo** — relevant to troubleshooting section only; do not position as essential

Results reference porosity in passing but then give a single generic routine that ignores it. There is no article in the top 10 that gives genuinely differentiated instructions for low vs. high porosity hair throughout the *entire* routine — not just at the oil-selection step. We can own this by threading porosity context through every step.

### Gap 3 — The "Still Dry" Problem Is Not Solved
Two top-10 titles acknowledge persistent dryness but neither provides a structured diagnostic. Readers who have already tried standard advice and failed are underserved. This captures high-intent, frustrated readers and positions the site as the place to come after generic advice has failed.
