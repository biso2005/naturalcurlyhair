## Wash day basics

**How often should I wash natural hair?**
Most people with natural hair do well washing once a week or once a fortnight. Washing too frequently with a stripping shampoo causes dryness; not washing frequently enough causes product and sebum build-up. There's no universal rule — let your scalp and hair tell you.

**Do I need to shampoo every wash day?**
No. Co-washing (washing with conditioner only, no shampoo) is suitable for in-between washes when your scalp isn't oily and you haven't used heavy products. Use shampoo when you need to clear genuine build-up, and use a chelating shampoo monthly if you're in a hard water area.

**Should I wash my hair in sections?**
For 4B and 4C hair particularly, yes. Washing in sections keeps the hair from tangling and matting during the process. Clip or twist each section after washing to keep it separated.

**How long should I leave conditioner on?**
Longer than the bottle says. For rinse-out conditioner used as a deep conditioner, 20–30 minutes under a plastic cap (with or without additional heat) makes a meaningful difference. A two-minute rinse does very little for dense coily hair.

**What's the difference between a deep conditioner and a regular conditioner?**
A deep conditioner is formulated to penetrate more deeply and deliver more intensive moisture or protein. You leave it on longer and usually use heat. A rinse-out conditioner is lighter and designed for regular use. Both have a place in a natural hair routine.

## Products

**Do I need to buy products specifically marketed for natural hair?**
No. The label is marketing. Read the ingredients list. Many affordable general-market conditioners (Tresemmé, Aussie, Cantu) work well on natural hair. → [Can I use regular conditioner on 4C hair?](/hair-care/can-i-use-regular-conditioner-on-4c-hair-uk/)

**Is coconut oil good for natural hair?**
Useful as a sealant and pre-shampoo treatment, not as a moisturiser. Apply to damp hair, not dry. Protein-sensitive hair may react to it with stiffness. → [Full article: Is coconut oil bad for natural hair?](/hair-care/is-coconut-oil-bad-for-natural-hair/)

**What does glycerin do?**
It's a humectant — it draws moisture from the air into your hair. Useful in moderate humidity. In high humidity (UK summers) it can make hair sticky. In very low humidity (cold, dry winter air) it can draw moisture out of your hair instead of into it. Many UK naturals reduce glycerin-heavy products in winter.

**What's the difference between sealing and moisturising?**
Moisture is water or a water-based product. Sealing is applying an oil or butter on top to slow evaporation. The sequence matters: water or water-based product first, then seal. An oil applied to dry hair seals in the dryness.

**Is Vaseline okay to use on natural hair?**
As a sealant on damp hair, yes — in small amounts. Not on the scalp regularly, not as a moisturiser, not before conditioning. → [Full article: Is Vaseline good for natural hair?](/hair-care/is-vaseline-good-for-natural-hair-uk/)

## Growth and length

**Why isn't my hair growing?**
Hair grows continuously — around 1–1.5cm per month for most people. If you're not seeing length, the hair is leaving at the ends (breakage) faster than it's arriving. Focus on reducing breakage: gentler detangling, protective styles, moisture balance. → [Full article on length retention.](/hair-care/4c-hair-not-growing-uk/)

**Does trimming make hair grow faster?**
No. Trimming has no effect on the follicle or growth rate. It prevents split ends from travelling up the shaft, which preserves the length you've already grown. → [Full article.](/hair-care/does-trimming-natural-hair-make-it-grow-faster/)

**Does castor oil grow hair?**
The clinical evidence is weak. Castor oil is a good sealant and provides a useful medium for scalp massage, which has slightly better evidence for hair thickness. → [Full evidence review.](/products/castor-oil-hair-growth-evidence-uk/)

## Common problems

**Why does my hair feel dry even after conditioning?**
Most likely causes: hard water mineral build-up (chelating shampoo monthly helps), conditioner applied for too short a time, conditioner too light for your hair's density. → [Full article.](/hair-care/why-does-hair-feel-dry-right-after-washing-natural/)

**Why does natural hair shrink so much?**
The curl coils back on itself as it dries — 4C hair can shrink to 20% of stretched length. Shrinkage is a sign of healthy elasticity. → [Full article.](/hair-care/why-does-natural-hair-shrink-so-much/)

**Why is my hair sticky?**
Usually glycerin in high humidity, or product build-up. → [Full article.](/hair-care/why-is-my-natural-hair-sticky/)

**Why does my scalp itch after washing?**
Usually incomplete rinsing, an ingredient reaction, or hard water residue. Persistent itching with flaking may be seborrheic dermatitis. → [Full article.](/hair-care/scalp-itchy-after-washing-natural-hair/)

**Can I over-moisturise natural hair?**
Yes. Too much moisture without enough protein leaves hair feeling mushy and limp with excessive stretch. The fix is one protein treatment, then reassess. → [Full article.](/hair-care/can-you-over-moisturise-natural-hair/)

## Hard water (UK-specific)

**How do I know if hard water is affecting my hair?**
Products that used to work stop working, hair feels coated despite conditioning, curl definition is inconsistent wash to wash. Hard water is particularly relevant if you've moved to London, the South East, or the East Midlands. [Use the water hardness checker.](/tools/water-hardness/)

**What's the difference between a clarifying shampoo and a chelating shampoo?**
A clarifying shampoo removes product build-up. A chelating shampoo removes mineral deposits from hard water — it uses different chemistry (typically EDTA or phytic acid) that binds to calcium and magnesium. In hard water areas, you need both in your rotation. Clarifying won't remove mineral deposits.
