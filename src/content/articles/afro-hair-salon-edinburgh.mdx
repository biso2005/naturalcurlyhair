Edinburgh has a smaller Afro hair salon scene than Glasgow, but the city's growing international student population and expanding Black British community have supported a real, if limited, range of specialist options. Mobile stylists are particularly important in Edinburgh, where fixed Afro hair salon provision is thinner than in most comparable UK cities.

**Edinburgh water:** very soft. Scottish Water serves Edinburgh with water predominantly from Loch Lomond and Talla/Megget reservoirs — among the softest water in the UK. Mineral buildup is not a significant concern for natural hair in Edinburgh. A basic clarifying shampoo rather than a chelating shampoo is sufficient for routine scalp care.

---

## How to Find an Edinburgh Afro Hair Salon

**Instagram.** Search "#EdinburghNaturalHair", "#EdinburghBraids", "#AfroHairEdinburgh", "#ScottishNaturalHair". Some stylists list both Edinburgh and Glasgow as their service area. Mobile stylists covering Central Scotland are the most reliably active accounts.

**Facebook groups.** Edinburgh Black community groups, Edinburgh student community groups (the university population contributes to the natural hair community), and Scottish natural hair groups. Word of mouth in these communities is the most effective route in a smaller salon market.

**Google Maps.** "Afro hair salon Edinburgh" or "natural hair Edinburgh". The results set is limited compared to most of this guide's cities — read every listing carefully.

---

## Glasgow as an Alternative

Glasgow is approximately 45 minutes from Edinburgh by train, and has a larger Afro hair salon offering. For major services (locs installation, complex braiding), some Edinburgh residents find that the Glasgow options justify occasional travel.
