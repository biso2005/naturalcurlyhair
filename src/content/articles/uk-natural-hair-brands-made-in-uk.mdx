Most natural hair brands in the UK are US imports. That's not a criticism — As I Am, Cantu, and SheaMoisture all produce genuinely good products. But there is a growing set of UK-made, UK-based natural hair brands, and for readers who want to support domestic independent businesses, knowing which ones to buy from and which products to choose matters.

This is the honest shortlist: brands genuinely based and making products in the UK, with verdicts on what's worth buying.

---

## Afrocenchix (London)

Founded in London in 2014 by Rachael Corson and Joycelyn Mate. Products are manufactured in the UK to a clean formulation standard — free from sulphates, silicones, parabens, mineral oil, and artificial fragrance.

**Best products:** Swish Moisturising Shampoo, Whipped Moisturising Hair Cream, Soar Scalp Oil.

**Honest note:** the price point is higher than US imports at equivalent volume, reflecting UK manufacturing costs and small-scale independent production. The quality justifies the shampoo price; the styling products face tougher competition from larger US brands at lower prices.

Available at Boots and afrocenchix.com. → [Full Afrocenchix review](/products/afrocenchix-products-review-uk/)

## Bouclème (London)

A London-based brand founded in 2014, formulated specifically for curly and coily hair. Products are made in the UK and the brand was created by a founder frustrated by the lack of products designed for natural European curly hair, though the formulations work across all curl types.

**Best products:** Curl Cleanser (a co-wash that cleanses more thoroughly than most), Curl Defining Gel (works well for type 3B–4A hair, provides definition without heavy crunch).

**Honest note:** Bouclème skews toward type 2–3 hair in its formulation and marketing, though the gel and cleanser work on 4A. For 4B and 4C, the brand is less well matched than Afrocenchix. Widely available at Boots and ASOS.

## Mielle (note: US brand, not UK despite wide availability)

Mielle Organics is a US brand (founded in Illinois). It's widely stocked in UK Boots and Superdrug and is sometimes listed in "UK natural hair brands" roundups because of its UK retail presence. It is not UK-made. → [Mielle Organics UK review](/products/mielle-organics-review-uk/)

## Grace & Stella (UK distribution, formulated in Canada)

Sometimes listed as a UK brand due to their UK marketing presence. Formulated in Canada. Included here for clarity — it's not UK-made despite significant UK retail presence.

## Rëzo (UK-based, small batch)

A smaller UK-based salon-brand that produces haircare products alongside their Rëzo cut method training. Small distribution, primarily available through their own channels. The cleansing conditioner and the Rëzo Melt Leave-In are the most accessible products. Worth knowing if you're in the market for salon-origin UK natural hair products.

## Umberto Giannini (London)

A London-based haircare brand with wider supermarket distribution than most UK natural hair brands — available at Boots, Superdrug, and some supermarkets. The Curl Jelly and the Curl Friends range are the most relevant for natural hair.

**Best products:** Curl Jelly Conditioning Leave-In (the best UK-native leave-in for type 3 hair), Curl Friends Scrunch Activating Jelly (a curl activator gel that works well for 2C–3C hair).

**Honest note:** Umberto Giannini performs better for type 2–3 than for type 4. For looser curls, it's the strongest UK-native option. For type 4, Afrocenchix or US brands are better matched.

Available at Boots and Superdrug. Budget-to-mid pricing.

## How to Find UK-Made Natural Hair Brands

The most reliable verification method:
- Check the product label for a UK address in the "manufactured by" or "responsible person" field
- Look for the "Made in UK" or "Made in Great Britain" stamp
- The brand's website should state UK manufacturing explicitly under "About" or "Our products"

"UK-based" and "UK-made" are different. A brand can be headquartered in London and manufacture in Poland. The product label is the most reliable source.

## FAQs

**Is there a UK-made alternative to the SheaMoisture Manuka Honey masque?**

Not at the same price point and performance level yet. The Afrocenchix Whipped cream comes closest as a UK-made moisturising product, but it's a styling cream rather than a deep conditioner. The UK-native deep conditioner gap in the market is real.

**Why are UK natural hair brands more expensive than US imports?**

UK manufacturing costs are higher than US or overseas manufacturing at the same scale. Import duties make US brands somewhat more expensive to ship, but the volumes involved in US brands' UK distribution make the per-unit cost lower than a small UK independent manufacturing locally. As UK brands grow, their per-unit manufacturing cost should decrease.

**Are UK natural hair brands better for the environment?**

Shorter supply chains reduce shipping emissions compared to transatlantic imports. UK manufacturing is subject to UK environmental regulations. Beyond that, "better for the environment" depends on specific practices that vary brand by brand — look for specific certifications rather than assuming UK = greener.

**What UK-based retailers specialise in natural hair brands?**

Roots UK (online and some physical locations) stocks a wider range of Black-founded and natural hair brands than most mainstream retailers. Afro Hair & Skin Co. (online) similarly specialises. Both carry brands not available at Boots or Superdrug.
