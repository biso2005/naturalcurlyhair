Porosity on 3C hair matters less dramatically than on type 4 hair — 3C coils are generally more accessible to products than 4B or 4C — but it still shapes which products work, how much moisture your hair holds between washes, and whether you need heat during conditioning.

If your 3C routine produces inconsistent results — good on wash day but quickly dry, or consistently weighed down and buildup-prone — porosity is likely the variable you haven't accounted for yet.

## What Porosity Means for 3C Hair

**Low porosity 3C:**
- Hair takes a long time to fully wet in the shower
- Products tend to ball up or sit on top rather than absorbing
- Buildup accumulates quickly even with relatively light products
- Heat makes a significant difference during conditioning
- Hair can feel moisturised right after washing then surprisingly dry again quickly

**High porosity 3C:**
- Absorbs moisture fast — hair gets wet immediately
- Loses it just as fast — dryness returns within hours
- More frizz in humidity (the open cuticle catches atmospheric moisture unevenly)
- Ends feel dry or rough even after conditioning
- Colour-treated or chemically processed hair is almost always high porosity

**Medium porosity 3C:** Most common in this type. Products absorb and hold reasonably well. Medium-porosity 3C hair tends to be more straightforwardly product-responsive than type 4 hair with the same porosity.

---

## Why 3C Low Porosity Shows Up Differently Than Type 4

Low-porosity 4B or 4C hair builds up readily and products seem to achieve nothing. Low-porosity 3C is subtler — the coil is open enough that products do something, but not as much as they should. You might notice that your coils feel soft on wash day but lose moisture and definition faster than you'd expect, or that products pile up after a few days rather than continuing to work.

The distinction matters because low-porosity type 4 advice (very heavy products to push through the cuticle) is counterproductive on low-porosity 3C. The 3C cuticle is more accessible; the fix is heat and product weight, not brute force.

---

## Skip the Float Test

3C coils trap air within the spiral, creating false floats regardless of cuticle state. The test doesn't reliably distinguish low from high porosity on this curl type.

---

## Two Tests That Work

### The spray bottle test

Spritz a small section of clean, dry hair with plain water. Watch for 30 seconds.

- Water **beads on the surface** or takes a while to absorb — low porosity
- Water **absorbs quickly** and the section darkens fast — high porosity
- Water **sinks in but slowly** — medium porosity

Do this after a clarifying wash for accuracy. Product residue can make low-porosity hair appear to absorb faster than it actually does.

### The feel test

Run a single clean, product-free strand between your fingers from tip to root.

- **Smooth, almost slippery** — flat cuticles, likely low porosity
- **Rough or bumpy, snags slightly** — raised cuticles, likely high porosity
- **Very rough or breaks mid-stroke** — high porosity with damage

## Roots vs Ends on 3C Hair

If your 3C hair is any real length, your roots are almost certainly lower porosity than your ends. New growth hasn't been through years of washing, UV, and detangling; ends have.

This shows up practically: roots resist moisture a little more, ends absorb quickly but lose moisture fast. Treating the whole length identically means you're always slightly over-moisturising the roots and under-moisturising the ends.

The spray bottle test on roots vs ends separately will tell you if this applies to your hair.

## What to Do Based on Your Result

### Low porosity 3C

- **Use heat when deep conditioning.** Without it, conditioner on low-porosity 3C sits on the surface and rinses off without much impact. A heat cap or warm towel over a shower cap for 20–30 minutes makes a measurable difference.
- **Choose lighter, water-based products.** Heavy creams and butters can build up on low-porosity 3C rather than absorbing. A lightweight leave-in often works better than a thick cream.
- **Apply to the wettest possible hair.** Don't let the hair dry between conditioning and product application.
- **Clarify regularly.** Buildup happens faster on low-porosity hair because products pile up. Monthly clarifying keeps the surface clear.

### High porosity 3C

- **Seal immediately after leave-in.** High-porosity 3C loses moisture quickly. An oil or cream applied while the hair is still wet slows evaporation.
- **Mid-weight products hold better than light ones.** High-porosity 3C often needs a bit more weight in the product stack to maintain moisture through the day.
- **Occasional protein.** Protein helps high-porosity hair hold moisture more consistently. Every six to eight weeks or when the hair starts feeling mushy or overly stretchy. Don't overdo it — too much protein makes 3C coils stiff.
- **Satin bonnet at night.** High-porosity hair loses moisture to cotton pillowcases faster. Worth doing for both types but more critical for high porosity.

### Medium porosity 3C

You have the most flexibility. Most curl-specific product lines are designed with medium-porosity hair in mind, which means mainstream options work well. Adjust weight and sealant based on how your hair responds rather than following a strict protocol.

## Hard Water and Porosity: The Confounding Variable

Hard water can make low-porosity hair behave like high-porosity hair — mineral film on the cuticle blocks absorption regardless of the cuticle's natural state. If you're in a hard water area (most of England) and your porosity test gives confusing results, do a chelating wash first and test again. The result after chelating will be more accurate.

## FAQs

**My 3C hair absorbs product fine but dries out fast. Is that high porosity?**

Yes — absorbs quickly, loses quickly is the high-porosity profile. The fix is better sealing rather than more product. An oil layer immediately after leave-in, while hair is still wet, is the key step.

**I switched products three times and nothing works consistently. Could it be porosity?**

Possibly — but check hard water first if you're in England. Mineral buildup is the most common reason 3C routines stop working, and no product change fixes it. Chelate, then assess porosity.

**Low porosity sounds bad. Is it?**

No. Low-porosity hair holds moisture well once it gets in — that's the payoff. The challenge is getting past the cuticle, which heat solves reliably. Low-porosity hair often looks healthier and shinier than high-porosity hair once the routine is right.

**How often should I retest porosity?**

After any chemical treatment, significant heat styling, or if your routine stops working for no obvious reason. Porosity can shift, and what worked last year may not match your current hair.
