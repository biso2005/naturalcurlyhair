Porosity matters for 2C hair because the wave/curl border is exactly where product weight decisions get most consequential. Low-porosity 2C needs lightweight products plus heat to absorb — too-heavy products sit on the wave and flatten it. High-porosity 2C needs slightly more weight to retain moisture — too-light products evaporate before the wave sets. Getting this wrong by one step in either direction is the difference between defined waves and flat or frizzy ones.

## What Porosity Means for 2C Hair

**Low porosity 2C:**
- Hair takes a long time to fully saturate under running water
- Products tend to sit on the wave surface rather than absorbing
- Buildup appears quickly — waves feel coated within a few washes
- Heat during deep conditioning makes a noticeable difference
- Hair feels moisturised right after washing then dry again within hours
- Frizz that looks product-related may actually be from products not absorbing

**High porosity 2C:**
- Hair wets almost instantly
- Products absorb but moisture doesn't stay — dry and frizzy by mid-afternoon
- Ends are consistently rough or dry even with conditioner
- More common in colour-treated, bleached, or heat-styled 2C hair
- Open cuticle catches atmospheric moisture unevenly, accelerating frizz

**Medium porosity 2C:** Most common. The widest range of products works. Most mainstream wavy and light-curl products are designed with this in mind, which is one reason product selection is more straightforward at 2C than at type 4.

## The Float Test and 2C: Not Worth Doing

2C hair traps air in the S-wave curl. That air keeps the strand afloat even if the cuticle is fully open — so a floating strand doesn't tell you anything reliable about porosity. Skip it.

## Tests That Work

### The spray bottle test

Spritz a small section of clean, dry 2C hair. Watch for 30 seconds:

- Water **beads and resists** absorbing — low porosity
- Water **absorbs quickly**, section darkens — high porosity
- Water **absorbs but slowly** — medium porosity

Do this after a chelating or clarifying wash. Mineral deposits from hard water can mimic low porosity on what is actually medium-porosity hair — the mineral layer absorbs first and makes the hair look like it's resisting water.

### The feel test

Run a single clean, dry strand between fingers from tip to root:

- **Smooth, almost slippery** — flat cuticle, likely low porosity
- **Slightly rough or catches** — raised cuticle, likely high porosity
- **Very rough, crinkly, or breaks** — high porosity with damage

## Mixed Porosity on 2C Hair

Roots and ends behave differently on most 2C hair. New growth at the roots is lower porosity; ends that have been through months or years of washing, UV, and manipulation are higher porosity.

If your roots feel moisturised but your ends are dry and frizzy — especially ends that start frizzing before the roots do — this is why.

The adjustment: concentrate conditioner and any sealant on the ends. Apply leave-in more heavily at the ends than the roots. This is also the section where using a slightly heavier oil (still lightweight — argan or jojoba) makes sense.

## What to Do Based on Your Result

### Low porosity 2C

Getting moisture into the hair is the challenge without adding weight that flattens the wave.

- **Heat during conditioning.** A warm towel around a shower cap for 20 minutes while deep conditioning. The most effective single change for low-porosity 2C — without heat, conditioner sits on the cuticle rather than penetrating. With heat, it absorbs and the wave feels genuinely moisturised.
- **Lightweight liquid leave-in only.** Cream leave-ins or anything thick sits on low-porosity 2C waves and makes them look coated without moisturising them.
- **Apply to soaking-wet hair.** Water opens the cuticle slightly; applying leave-in while the hair is very wet means products go in during the brief window when the cuticle is more receptive.
- **Monthly clarifying wash.** Low-porosity hair accumulates product buildup faster than higher-porosity hair — products pile on the surface because they don't penetrate. A monthly clarify keeps the surface clear.
- **Skip heavy sealants.** A couple of drops of lightweight oil is enough. Heavy oils on low-porosity 2C sit on top and flatten the wave.

### High porosity 2C

Keeping moisture in is the challenge; the wide-open cuticle loses it quickly in UK conditions.

- **Seal while hair is wet.** Apply leave-in, then immediately seal with a small amount of lightweight oil before the leave-in dries. The oil layer slows moisture evaporation from the wave.
- **Slightly more weight than medium porosity.** High-porosity 2C can handle a slightly heavier leave-in than medium-porosity 2C — not cream-heavy, but a richer liquid rather than the thinnest possible mist.
- **Protein occasionally.** High-porosity 2C hair that's been colour-treated or heat-styled can benefit from a protein treatment every six to eight weeks. The signal: hair that feels mushy or stretches excessively when wet. Don't use protein on a schedule — use it when the hair shows those specific signs.
- **Satin at night.** High-porosity hair loses moisture through cotton pillowcases faster than lower-porosity hair. Satin bonnet or pillowcase, every night.

### Medium porosity 2C

Most mainstream products work. Focus on the UK seasonal adjustment — lighter and lower glycerin in winter — rather than a strict protocol. Adjust based on how the waves respond from one wash to the next.

## Hard Water and Porosity Testing

In hard water areas, mineral deposits on the 2C wave can make the spray bottle test read incorrectly — the mineral layer absorbs water before the hair does, mimicking low porosity. Chelate or clarify first, then test on clean hair.

## FAQs

**My 2C waves work on wash day but frizz on day two. Is that porosity?**

Could be high porosity, could be night routine, could be both. Check: are you sealing with oil after leave-in? Are you using a satin bonnet? Is your refresh spritz tap water in a hard water area? High porosity + hard water + cotton pillowcase = classic day-two frizz for 2C.

**I've been using heavy products thinking my 2C hair needed moisture. Now it's flat and frizzy. What happened?**

Low porosity 2C — the heavy products couldn't absorb and just flattened the wave. Do a clarifying wash to reset, test porosity on clean hair, switch to lightweight products plus heat during conditioning.

**Does porosity change?**

Yes, gradually. Chemical treatments, heat styling, and UV exposure all raise porosity over time. Hair that was medium-porosity three years ago may be high-porosity now. Re-test if your routine stops working for no clear reason.

**My 2C hair absorbs products fine but my waves still aren't defined. What else could cause it?**

Porosity is probably fine then. Check technique (touching while drying), product amount (too light a gel for UK humidity), and hard water (chelating).
