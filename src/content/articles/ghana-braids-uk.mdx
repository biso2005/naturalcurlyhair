Ghana braids — also called banana braids or straight-backs with extensions — are cornrows that use the feed-in technique: starting with natural hair only at the root and gradually incorporating extension hair along the braid, so each braid starts thin at the scalp and gradually thickens toward the end. The visual result is a tapered root with more volume mid-length, different from traditional cornrows that start and stay the same width.

---

## Ghana Braids vs Regular Cornrows

The technical difference is the feed-in:

**Regular cornrows:** begin with all three sections (including extension hair) at the root, creating uniform width from root to end.

**Ghana braids:** begin with natural hair only, feeding in extension hair gradually. Less tension at the root because there's less hair being pulled from the scalp at the start. The taper creates a different aesthetic.

The result looks cleaner at the root and puts less initial strain on the hairline than standard cornrows with extension hair.

---

## Styles

Ghana braids are versatile in the patterns they can be installed in:

**Straight back:** the most common — five to eight rows running back from the hairline. Clean, classic.

**Side part:** rows arranged with a side part, some rows crossing over.

**Freestyle:** curved rows, diagonal patterns, geometric designs. More installation time and skill; higher cost.

**Pop smoke braids:** a popular variant — Ghana braids with visible, defined zigzag or angled parts, worn with the ends loose (usually with kanekalon curled at the tip). Common in UK Afro hair salons.

---

## Cost in the UK

**Typical range: £50–£150.** Variation by pattern complexity, length, and whether the stylist provides hair or you bring your own.

A standard straight-back Ghana braid appointment takes two to four hours. More intricate designs or longer hair add time.

---

## How Long They Last

**Four to six weeks** for most people. The feed-in technique means less initial root bulk, so the root area looks neater for slightly longer than traditional cornrows with extension hair. New growth shows at approximately the same rate.

---

## Care

**Same as cornrows:**

- Scalp oil or spray every two to three days to the scalp between rows
- Satin bonnet or pillowcase at night
- Scalp wash every two weeks with diluted shampoo, rinsed thoroughly, dried fully
- Light edge control along the rows in the morning if needed

**Hard water note:** if you're in London or another hard water area, an ACV rinse after washing reduces mineral buildup on the extension hair. The extension hair in Ghana braids is more visible than in box braids, so dullness from mineral deposits is more noticeable.

---

## Tension at the Edges

Like all cornrow styles, Ghana braids begin at the hairline. The feed-in technique reduces tension compared to standard extension cornrows, but the edges are still the most vulnerable area. If you feel significant pulling at the temples or nape during installation, say so.

Visibly stretched skin at the hairline, bumps along the hairline a few days after installation, or persistent soreness at the edge are all signs of too much tension. If the stylist is known for very neat, tight edges, ask explicitly for the hairline to be looser.

## FAQs

**What's the difference between Ghana braids and box braids?**

Ghana braids are cornrows — they lie flat against the scalp in rows. Box braids are individual braids that hang free from a parted section. Ghana braids create a flatter, more structured look. Box braids create a fuller, more voluminous result. Both are protective styles.

**Do I need to bring my own hair?**

Many salons provide the kanekalon hair. Ask when booking — some include it in the price; others ask you to buy and bring it. For Ghana braids, one to two packs of kanekalon (depending on density and desired thickness) is typically sufficient.

**Can I swim in Ghana braids?**

Yes, with a swimming cap over the braids. Ghana braids with exposed extension hair at the ends absorb water quickly; the extension hair can feel rough or frizzy after chlorine exposure. Rinse thoroughly after swimming and dry fully.

**Are Ghana braids suitable for children?**

Yes, particularly because of the lower tension at the root compared to standard cornrows with extensions. Straight-back Ghana braids are a practical school hairstyle — they lie flat, survive PE, and can last several weeks. For children, ask specifically for loose installation at the hairline.
