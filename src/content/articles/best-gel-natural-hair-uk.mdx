The best gel for natural hair in the UK is Eco Style Olive Oil Gel for type 4 wash-and-gos and slicked styles. Aunt Jackie's Don't Shrink Flaxseed Elongating Curling Gel is the pick for type 3 hair needing definition without stiffness. LA Looks Extreme Sport is the high-hold budget option that outperforms products three times its price, if you can find it in the UK.

## The Picks

### For Type 4 (4A, 4B, 4C) — Definition and Hold

**The pick: Eco Style Olive Oil Gel** — the most widely used gel in the natural hair community for a reason. Strong hold without flaking on type 4 hair. Works for wash-and-gos, braid-outs (applied before braiding), and edge laying. The green formula (regular) is the standard pick; the blue (sport) is higher hold for edges and slicked styles. Available at Boots, Superdrug, Sainsbury's, and most BSS. Around £5 for 236ml.

**Runner-up: TGIN Triple Moisture Replenishing Conditioner used as a sty—** no. Wrong direction. **Runner-up: Mielle Organics Honey & Ginger Styling Gel** — more moisturising than Eco Style, slightly less hold, better for 4A hair that finds Eco Style drying on its own. Available at Boots. Around £12 for 227ml.

**Skip: Garnier Fructis Curl Sculpting Gel** — formulated for Caucasian curl types. Flakes on type 4 and doesn't provide enough hold for coily hair. The ingredient list isn't matched to the moisture needs of 4A–4C.

### For Type 3 (3A, 3B, 3C) — Definition Without Crunch

**The pick: Aunt Jackie's Don't Shrink Flaxseed Elongating Curling Gel** — holds curl definition without making the hair stiff or crunchy when dry. The flaxseed base is gentler on lighter curl patterns than acrylic polymer-heavy gels. Available at Boots and online. Around £10 for 227g.

**Runner-up: Cantu Wave Whip Curling Mousse** — technically a mousse but functions similarly to a light gel for type 3 hair. Lighter hold, more movement, less definition. Better for 3A–3B hair that doesn't need strong hold. Available at Boots and Superdrug. Around £8 for 240ml.

**Skip: TRESemmé Curl Hydration Gel** — fine for occasional use on 3A but builds up quickly and produces frizz rather than reducing it on 3B and 3C.

### For Edges and Slicked Styles

**The pick: Got2B Glued Blasting Freeze Spray or Styling Gel (Schwarzkopf)** — the strongest-hold product available at UK supermarkets and widely used for laying edges and slicked styles that need to last. Available at Boots and Superdrug. Around £6 for 240ml.

**Runner-up: Eco Style Sport Gel (blue)** — the high-hold version of the Eco Style range. Lower risk of flaking than Got2B on natural coily hair.

**Skip: Murray's Beeswax** — used for edges in some communities but is oil-based wax rather than a water-based gel, which means buildup that doesn't wash out cleanly. Difficult to remove and can cause scalp buildup over time.

### Budget Pick

**LA Looks Extreme Sport Gel** — the open secret of the natural hair community. Extremely strong hold, no flaking on type 4 hair, costs under £4 for a large tub. The caveat is availability in the UK: it's a US product available through Amazon UK and some BSS, not at Boots or Superdrug. Worth ordering online. Around £3–4 for 680g, considerably cheaper per gram than any comparable product.

## How to Apply Gel on Natural Hair

**Wash-and-go method:**
1. Leave-in on soaking-wet hair, section by section
2. Optional: curl cream on top
3. Gel applied on top of still-wet hair, using the praying hands method (press the section between palms and smooth downward) or finger coiling individual sections
4. Do not touch the hair while it dries — touching the wet gel breaks the cast
5. Once fully dry (this is important: fully dry, not damp), scrunch out the cast with hands or a microfibre towel

Touching the hair before the cast is fully dry is the most common cause of frizz.

**The gel cast:** gel on natural hair dries into a hard cast. This is correct. The cast is scrunched out when dry to reveal soft, defined curls underneath. If the hair is still crunchy after scrunching, either the cast isn't fully dry, the gel was applied too thickly, or a few drops of oil on the hands during the scrunch softens it.

## FAQs

**My gel flakes. What's causing it?**

Either the gel is reacting with other products in the routine (some gels react with certain silicones or oils, causing white flaking), or it's applied too thickly. Try applying gel alone with no other products and see if the flaking continues — if not, there's a product interaction to diagnose. Eco Style and LA Looks are two gels with low flaking rates on type 4.

**Can I use gel on a twist-out or braid-out?**

Yes — applied before twisting or braiding, gel defines the braid-out or twist-out pattern as the hair dries. This produces a more defined result than cream alone. Apply gel on top of leave-in and cream before twisting, leave overnight, and unravel the next day.

**Does gel cause breakage?**

Gel itself doesn't cause breakage. Scrunching out the cast on damp hair rather than fully dry hair does — the coil structure is fragile while wet, and mechanical manipulation while damp breaks more than when the hair is fully set. Let the hair dry completely before touching it.

**My gel works in summer but not in UK winter. Why?**

Gel needs moisture in the air to set correctly. In UK winter, central heating drops indoor humidity to 30–40%, and outdoor cold air is also dry. The gel may not be setting into the same defined cast it does in more humid conditions. Try applying to wetter hair (not just damp) in winter, or add a humectant product (glycerin-containing leave-in) before the gel.
