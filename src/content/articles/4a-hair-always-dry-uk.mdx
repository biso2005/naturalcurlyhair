Persistent dryness on 4A hair is frustrating because it often doesn't respond to the obvious fix — more conditioner, more product — the way you'd expect. That's usually because the cause isn't what you think it is.

There are four things that commonly produce 4A dryness that products alone can't fix. This article helps you work out which one you're dealing with.

---

## Cause 1: Hard Water

If you're in London, the South East, East Anglia, or the Midlands, start here.

Hard water contains dissolved minerals — calcium and magnesium — that settle on your hair shaft with every wash. These minerals coat the cuticle and physically block your conditioner from reaching the hair. You can apply the best deep conditioner available and get almost nothing from it if a mineral layer is sitting in the way.

The symptom profile: hair that feels rough or stiff after washing, conditioner that seems to do nothing, a product routine that used to work and now doesn't, or a greying/ashy tone to the hair that conditioning doesn't resolve.

**The fix:** a chelating shampoo used once a month. Not a clarifying shampoo — that removes product buildup, not minerals. A chelating shampoo (check for EDTA or phytic acid in the ingredients) removes the mineral film and resets the hair. After the first proper chelating wash, most people notice their conditioner working differently.

→ [4A hair and hard water UK](/4a/4a-hair-hard-water-uk/)

**How to confirm this is your problem:** buy aquarium water hardness test strips from a pet shop or Amazon. Fill a glass from your bathroom tap, dip the strip, check the result. Above 200 mg/L means you're in a hard water area.

## Cause 2: Missing or Wrong Sealant

4A hair absorbs moisture well — the S-coil has more surface contact with conditioner and leave-in than the Z-angles of 4B or 4C. But absorbing moisture is only half the job. If there's nothing on top of the leave-in to slow moisture evaporating out, your hair will feel great immediately after wash day and dry again within hours.

**Signs this is the issue:** hair feels moisturised right after washing but is dry by the next morning. Products seem to work but don't last.

**The fix:** apply a sealant layer — oil, butter, or cream — immediately after your leave-in, while the hair is still wet. The sealant sits on top of the moisture layer and slows evaporation.

For 4A hair specifically: lighter sealants usually work better than heavy ones like castor oil or thick shea butter. Jojoba, argan, or a light curl cream typically give better definition without weighing the coil down. If a heavy sealant is making your 4A coils look weighed down rather than defined, switch to something lighter.

## Cause 3: Products Sitting on Top Rather Than Absorbing

This is usually a porosity issue — specifically low porosity, where the cuticle resists moisture regardless of what you apply.

**Signs this is the issue:** products ball up on the hair as you apply them, the hair feels greasy but not moisturised, buildup accumulates quickly, and the hair still feels dry despite using a lot of product.

**The fix:** two things work for low-porosity 4A hair.

First, heat during conditioning. A heat cap, steamer, or warm damp towel over a shower cap helps moisture penetrate. Without it, conditioner on low-porosity hair stays on the surface and rinses off without much effect.

Second, switch to lighter, water-based products. Heavy butters and thick creams are designed to penetrate, but on low-porosity hair they just add to the surface layer. A lightweight liquid leave-in applied to soaking-wet hair often absorbs better than a thick cream.

## Cause 4: Not Enough Water at the Application Step

This is the most common and easiest to fix. If your hair is damp — rather than soaking wet — when you apply leave-in, you're starting with less moisture than your hair needs, and no product will compensate for that.

**Signs this is the issue:** hair feels moisturised immediately but not deeply; definition after wash day is good but fades within 24 hours.

**The fix:** apply everything while the hair is dripping wet. Not towel-dried damp. Not air-dried for five minutes damp. Soaking, water running down your arms damp. Water is your primary moisturiser; the leave-in and sealant preserve it. Starting with less water means finishing with less moisture regardless of what you layer on top.

## UK-Specific: Indoor Winter Dryness

There's a dryness cause that's specific to living in the UK and worth mentioning separately: indoor heated air in winter.

The UK's central heating creates low-humidity indoor environments in November–March, even while outdoors it's damp. Humectant-heavy products — particularly leave-ins and gels with high glycerin content — work by pulling moisture from the surrounding air. In dry indoor air, they can pull moisture out of the hair instead, making dryness worse rather than better.

If your 4A routine works well from April to October and fails every winter, this is likely the cause. Reduce glycerin-heavy products and increase sealants in the colder months.

## Working Out Which Cause Applies to You

Start with these in order — they're ranked from most common to least:

1. **Check your water hardness first.** If you're in a hard water area, do a chelating wash before changing anything else. You may not need to change your products at all.

2. **Check your application method.** Is your hair soaking wet when you apply leave-in? Try the next wash day with noticeably wetter hair and compare.

3. **Check your sealant.** Are you using one? Is it the right weight for your hair? Try adding or changing your oil/cream layer.

4. **Check your porosity.** Spray bottle test after a clarifying wash gives you the most accurate read. If water beads on the surface rather than absorbing, you have low porosity — and heat during conditioning is the fix.

---

## What Doesn't Help

**Using more product.** If moisture isn't absorbing, more of the same product just creates buildup. The problem is the barrier — hard water, wrong sealant timing, or low porosity — not the quantity.

**Switching products repeatedly.** If you've gone through ten different leave-ins looking for one that "works" and none have, the issue is upstream of the product itself. Diagnose first.

**Skipping the sealant because the leave-in already has oil in it.** A leave-in with a small amount of oil in the formula is not the same as a separate sealant layer. Both can be useful, but the dedicated sealing step makes a measurable difference in how long moisture stays.

## FAQs

**My 4A hair feels moisturised right after wash day but is dry by the next morning. What's happening?**

Classic missing-or-insufficient sealant. Apply oil or cream immediately after your leave-in while the hair is still wet, then don't touch it while it dries. If you're already doing this, try a heavier sealant or increase the amount.

**I've tried everything and nothing works. Where do I start?**

Hard water. It's the cause most often missed because it's invisible and not a product problem. Check your postcode and water hardness, do a chelating wash, then reassess.

**Could my diet affect 4A dryness?**

Scalp health can affect moisture production but it's rarely the explanation for persistent dryness on the length. Focus on the water and application method first — those are overwhelmingly more common causes.

**Why does my 4A hair feel different after I've been somewhere with softer water?**

That's hard water confirmed. Your hair responds to the absence of mineral buildup. The fix at home is the monthly chelating wash.
