Micro braids are very small individual braids — comparable to the diameter of a pencil or smaller — installed across the entire head. They cost more, take longer, and have a longer lifespan than medium or large box braids. Whether they're worth it depends on what you want.

---

## The Honest Numbers

**Cost in the UK: £180–£350.** The wide range reflects stylist experience, hair length, density, and whether extension hair is included. In London, expect the higher end. Outside London, £150–£250 is more typical.

**Installation time: 10–16 hours.** This is a one-day or sometimes two-session appointment. Factor this into your decision — sixteen hours is a significant commitment.

**Longevity: 6–8 weeks.** Longer than medium box braids because the smaller size and tighter sections unravel more slowly. Some people with very tight natural coils and good maintenance get ten weeks.

**Removal time: 3–5 hours.** Longer than larger braids because there are more of them and each one is smaller.

---

## What Micro Braids Give You

**Versatility:** the most compelling reason to choose micro braids. Because there are so many of them and they're fine, they move and style almost like natural loose hair. You can wear them in a bun, a high ponytail, curled on rollers, half-up half-down. This is not achievable with medium or large box braids.

**Longevity:** the additional weeks they stay in compared to medium braids can justify the cost for people who value the lower frequency of salon visits.

**Definition:** each braid is smaller and finer, giving a more detailed pattern.

---

## What Micro Braids Cost You

**Time:** the installation is significantly longer than any other braid style. A full day in a salon chair is a real commitment.

**Money:** among the most expensive protective style options in the UK.

**Root tension risk:** hundreds of small braids means hundreds of small traction points. The risk of traction alopecia is present with any braid style, but the multiplication of small sections means more stress distributed across more of the scalp. Good stylist selection and loose installation at the hairline matter more here than with larger braids.

**Scalp access:** very small braids in high density make accessing the scalp for moisture and washing more difficult than medium braids. Maintenance is more involved.

---

## Who Micro Braids Are Right For

- People who want to style the braids as if they were hair — ponytails, buns, roller sets
- People willing to invest significantly in an install they won't redo for six to eight weeks
- People with a reliable, experienced stylist they trust with a long, delicate installation
- People whose scalp tolerates extended protective styles well

---

## Who Should Consider Something Else

- Anyone with a sensitive scalp or history of traction alopecia — the density of small sections increases risk
- Anyone with a limited budget — medium box braids give most of the protective benefit at a third of the cost
- Anyone who can't spare the installation time — ten to sixteen hours is a real barrier
- Anyone in a hurry — there is no shortcut with micro braids

## FAQs

**Can I sleep in micro braids without damage?**

Yes, with a satin bonnet large enough to cover all the braids, or a satin/silk pillowcase. The fine braids are more prone to frizzing at the root from cotton friction than larger braids.

**How do I find a good micro braid stylist in the UK?**

Portfolio review is essential — ask specifically for photos of micro braids, not just small braids. Ask how long a full install takes. Ask about their hairline approach. Micro braids that aren't installed correctly tangle together over the weeks and cause significant breakage on removal.

**I had micro braids removed badly before and my hair broke significantly. Was that normal?**

Heavy breakage on micro braid removal usually comes from removal that was rushed, done dry without conditioner, or done by pulling rather than carefully unravelling. With good removal technique — conditioner, patience, unravelling from the bottom up — breakage should be minimal. If you're having professional removal, verify they take the time required.

**Can I get micro braids without extension hair?**

Yes. Natural-hair-only micro braids are smaller but still achievable on sufficient hair length. They won't reach below the shoulders without extension hair and the natural hair needs to be at least three to four centimetres long to braid without slipping.
