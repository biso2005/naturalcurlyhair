Hard water does not cause hair loss in the clinical sense — your follicles are unaffected by calcium and magnesium minerals, and hair that falls from the root in hard water areas is not falling because of the water.

What hard water does cause is **breakage** — and breakage that happens close to the scalp can look like hair loss. If you're finding short snapped pieces of hair in the bath rather than long strands with a white root bulb attached, that's breakage, not loss.

## Why hard water causes breakage

Calcium and magnesium deposits coat the hair shaft, blocking moisture from getting in and making the strand rough and brittle. Dry, brittle hair snaps during detangling, washing, and styling. In a very hard water area — London, East Midlands, Brighton, Hull, Luton — the mineral build-up is continuous and significant.

The breakage tends to happen at the most fragile points: the ends, areas subject to friction (edges, nape, the section where a headband sits), and points where the hair has been chemically processed.

## How to tell breakage from hair loss

- **Breakage:** Short pieces, varied lengths, no root bulb at the end, finds in the bath and on your pillow after styling
- **True hair loss:** Long strands, white or translucent root bulb attached to one end, thinning at the scalp that doesn't reverse

If you're seeing thinning at the scalp — particularly at the edges, crown, or temples — that's a different matter and worth seeing a GP or trichologist about. Hard water is unlikely to be the direct cause.

## What hard water does to natural hair specifically

Natural hair is already prone to dryness because the curl pattern makes it harder for sebum to travel from scalp to tip. Mineral deposits make this worse: they sit on the shaft and actively prevent moisture from getting in, even when you're applying conditioner correctly.

The result is a routine that stops working. Products that were fine in your previous address (particularly if you moved to London or the Home Counties from a soft water area) suddenly seem ineffective. Your hair feels perpetually dry and brittle despite conditioning.

## What to do

**Chelating shampoo monthly.** A chelating shampoo contains ingredients (usually EDTA or phytic acid) designed specifically to bond with mineral deposits and remove them. Regular clarifying shampoo does not do this.

**Deep condition immediately after chelating.** Chelating strips both minerals and some of the hair's natural moisture. Always follow with a deep conditioner.

**Shower filter as a first line of defence.** A filter fitted to your shower head reduces the mineral content of the water before it reaches your hair, reducing accumulation between chelating sessions.

If the breakage continues after three months of consistent chelating, or if you're seeing actual scalp thinning, see a trichologist. The [Institute of Trichologists directory](https://trichologists.org.uk) lists practitioners with experience in Afro and natural hair.
