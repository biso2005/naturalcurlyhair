## The basics

**What is 4C hair?**
4C is the tightest curl type in the Andre Walker typing system — a very tight, densely packed coil pattern with no defined curl shape when dry. The coils are so tight that individual strands often look like they kink in multiple directions rather than forming a consistent spiral. It's the most common hair type among people of sub-Saharan African descent.

**Is 4C hair the same as 4B?**
Related but different. 4B hair forms tight Z-shaped bends; 4C is even tighter and usually shows less definition. Many people have a mix of both across different areas of their scalp. The practical care requirements are similar — the key differences are in shrinkage (4C is usually more extreme) and detangling difficulty.

**Why does 4C hair feel dry all the time?**
The tight coil pattern makes it difficult for sebum — the scalp's natural oil — to travel from root to tip. Most of the length of the hair strand gets none of the scalp's natural conditioning. Deep conditioning, sealing with oil after moisturising, and protective styles that reduce daily manipulation all help.

**Is 4C hair fragile?**
More vulnerable to breakage than looser curl types, but not inherently fragile. The curl pattern means each strand bends sharply many times along its length, which creates points of stress. Rough handling, insufficient moisture, and mechanical tension are the main causes of breakage — not the hair type itself.

## Wash day

**How often should I wash 4C hair?**
Once a week to once a fortnight for most people. Less than weekly is fine if your scalp isn't oily and you're in a low-manipulation protective style. More than weekly with a sulphate shampoo will cause dryness in most cases.

**How do I detangle 4C hair without breakage?**
Detangle in small sections on wet hair with a slippery conditioner, starting from the ends and working up. A wide-tooth comb or fingers. Never start from the roots. Never dry-detangle. Long-contact conditioner makes a significant difference — apply it and leave for ten minutes before attempting to detangle.

**Should I deep condition every wash day?**
For most 4C hair, yes — deep conditioning once a week is appropriate. The standard two-minute rinse-out conditioner isn't sufficient for the moisture levels dense, coily hair needs. A dedicated deep conditioner with heat for 20–30 minutes changes outcomes noticeably.

**What's the LOC method and does it work for 4C hair?**
LOC stands for Leave-in / Oil / Cream. The idea is to layer moisture (leave-in), then seal it with oil, then lock it in with a heavier cream. For 4C hair with normal to high porosity, it works well. For low-porosity 4C hair, the LCO order (leave-in / cream / oil last) or a lighter version of LOC may absorb better.

## Shrinkage and length

**Why does my 4C hair shrink so much?**
4C hair can shrink to 20% of its actual length when dry. The coils compress on themselves as they dry — it's the same mechanism as a spring contracting. Shrinkage is a sign of elasticity and moisture. → [Full article on shrinkage.](/hair-care/why-does-natural-hair-shrink-so-much/)

**How do I stretch 4C hair without heat?**
Banding (placing hair ties at intervals on damp hair before drying), African threading, braids or twists to dry. These give elongation without risking heat damage to the curl pattern.

**Why isn't my 4C hair growing?**
Almost always a retention problem, not a growth rate problem. 4C hair grows the same rate as any other hair — breakage at the ends removes it at roughly the same pace. Gentle detangling, protective styles, and moisture balance retain more of what grows.

## Products

**What products work for 4C hair?**
Heavier, richer conditioners (Cantu, Shea Moisture Manuka Honey, SheaMoisture Raw Shea Butter), leave-ins with good slip, thicker sealant oils (castor oil, shea butter). 4C hair generally needs more product than looser curl types and doesn't do well with very light, watery formulas.

**Do I need a denman brush for 4C hair?**
Not necessarily. A denman brush is useful for defining and elongating the curl pattern, but not everyone's 4C hair responds well to it. If brushing causes more breakage than benefit, wide-tooth comb or fingers-only is fine.

**Is the LOC method better than the LCO method for 4C hair?**
Depends on porosity. High-porosity 4C hair absorbs moisture quickly and benefits from oil before cream to slow absorption and prevent frizz. Low-porosity 4C hair benefits from oil last, after the leave-in and cream, so the lighter products can absorb first.

## Common problems

**Why does 4C hair tangle so badly?**
Partly structural — the tight coil creates more opportunity for strands to wrap around each other. Single-strand knots (tiny knots that form when one strand coils back on itself) are particularly common with 4C hair. → [Full article on tangling.](/hair-care/why-does-natural-hair-tangle-so-much-uk/)

**My 4C hair is always breaking. What's wrong?**
Most likely: insufficient moisture, detangling too roughly, or hard water mineral build-up. Work through them in that order. → [Check your water hardness.](/tools/water-hardness/)

**Can hard water make 4C hair worse?**
Yes. The mineral deposits that hard water leaves on the hair shaft are particularly disruptive for 4C hair because they roughen the cuticle and block moisture entry — making the dryness that 4C hair already experiences significantly worse. Monthly chelating shampoo is especially important for 4C hair in London and the South East.

**Is 4C hair unsuitable for certain products?**
4C hair tends to struggle with very light, watery products that don't provide enough moisture or film weight for dense coils. Thin spritz-style leave-ins, very lightweight conditioners, and non-occlusive oils used as the only sealant often underdeliver. Beyond that, most products can work depending on your porosity and density.
