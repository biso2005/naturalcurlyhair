The best leave-in conditioner for natural hair in the UK is As I Am Leave-In Conditioner for most type 4 hair. Cantu Shea Butter Leave-In Conditioning Repair Cream is the pick for very dry or high porosity 4B and 4C. For low porosity hair that struggles with heavy products, Kinky Curly Knot Today Leave-In Detangler works without buildup.

Everything else in the routine — cream, gel, oil — layers on top of the leave-in. If the leave-in is wrong, the rest of the routine doesn't work.

## The Picks

### For Most Type 4 Hair (4A, 4B, 4C — normal to high porosity)

**The pick: As I Am Leave-In Conditioner** — water as the first ingredient, which matters: the hair needs water as the first layer of moisture, not a cream or butter. Light enough to apply without weighing down the coil but conditioning enough to work as a real base for the routine. Detangles as it conditions. Available at Boots and Superdrug. Around £11 for 237ml.

**Runner-up: SheaMoisture Jamaican Black Castor Oil Strengthen & Restore Leave-In Conditioner** — heavier than the As I Am, appropriate for hair that needs more conditioning before layering products. Also works as a detangling aid. Available at Boots, Superdrug, and Sainsbury's. Around £10 for 325ml.

**Skip: Cantu Shea Butter Natural Hair Coconut Curling Cream** — marketed as a styling product and sometimes used as a leave-in, but it's heavy enough to weigh down the curl if used as the base layer. Better as a curl cream on top of a proper leave-in.

### For Very Dry or High Porosity Hair (4B and 4C specifically)

**The pick: Cantu Shea Butter Leave-In Conditioning Repair Cream** — the heavier formulation suits hair that loses moisture rapidly. The shea butter creates a surface coat that slows moisture evaporation. Available at Boots, Superdrug, and most supermarkets. Around £8 for 453g.

**Runner-up: Mielle Organics Babassu Oil & Mint Deep Conditioner used as a leave-in** — some 4C hair finds that a standard rinse-out conditioner used as a leave-in provides more conditioning than a dedicated leave-in product. Mielle's formulation works this way on very dry hair. Available at Boots and online. Around £14 for 227g.

**Skip: Giovanni Direct Leave-In** — a popular US recommendation but too lightweight for 4B and 4C high porosity hair. Works well for type 3 and fine-stranded hair; doesn't provide enough slip or moisture for dense 4C.

### For Low Porosity Hair

**The pick: Kinky Curly Knot Today Leave-In Detangler** — one of the few genuinely lightweight leave-ins that doesn't build up on low porosity hair. Water first in the ingredients, light enough to penetrate a flat cuticle with heat, clarifies cleanly. Available on Amazon UK and LookFantastic. Around £14 for 236ml.

**Runner-up: As I Am Leave-In Conditioner** (listed above) — works for low porosity provided the hair is properly clarified first. The key for low porosity is application: apply to very wet hair section by section, and use a hooded dryer or sit under heat for ten minutes to open the cuticle before sealing.

**Skip: Any leave-in that lists butters or oils early in the ingredients.** These coat the hair surface and are appropriate as sealants after the leave-in, not as the leave-in itself on low porosity hair. Shea butter as a third or fourth ingredient in a leave-in is a problem for low porosity.

## Application

The leave-in goes on wet or very damp hair — not dry hair. On soaking-wet hair direct from the shower, section by section, smoothed through from root to tip. The water in the hair activates the leave-in; applying it dry is wasted product.

For type 4, apply in four to six sections. For looser curl types, the whole head in two to three sections.

The next product (cream or gel, depending on the routine) goes on top immediately — before the hair begins to dry.

## FAQs

**Can I use a rinse-out conditioner as a leave-in?**

Some rinse-out conditioners work as leave-ins — particularly for type 4 high porosity hair that needs more moisture than a standard leave-in provides. The risk is product buildup on the scalp. If you're using a rinse-out as a leave-in, clarify more frequently.

**My leave-in makes the hair crunchy when it dries. Why?**

Either the leave-in contains protein (some do) and the hair is protein-sensitive, or it's being applied to hair that still has product or mineral buildup underneath. Clarify, then try again. If the crunchiness persists, check the ingredient list for hydrolysed protein — that may be the cause.

**Is a spray leave-in or a cream leave-in better?**

Cream leave-ins provide more conditioning and are better for type 4 hair. Spray leave-ins are more convenient for daily refreshing and work better for looser curl types. Many people use a cream leave-in on wash day and a spray leave-in for midweek refreshing.

**My leave-in stops working after a few months. What's happening?**

Either the hair has changed (seasonal, hormonal, or product accumulation changes the hair's response to the same product) or buildup from the leave-in itself is reducing its effectiveness. A clarifying wash and fresh start usually confirms which it is.
