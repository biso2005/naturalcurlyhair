Cantu is one of the most widely distributed natural hair brands in the UK — available at Boots, Superdrug, and most supermarkets — which makes it the default entry point for a lot of people starting a natural hair routine. Some of the range is genuinely good value. Some of it is carried by brand recognition rather than formulation quality. Here's the honest breakdown by product.

---

## Worth Buying

**Cantu Shea Butter Leave-In Conditioning Repair Cream** — the strongest product in the range. Dense enough to work as a real moisture base for 4B and 4C hair, provides genuine slip for detangling, and is widely available for around £7–8 for a large tub. This is the product that built Cantu's reputation in the natural hair community and it earns it. Available at Boots, Superdrug, Sainsbury's, Asda.

**Cantu Moisturizing Curl Activator Cream** — works well as a curl cream for type 4C hair that needs heavy moisture. Not as refined as some higher-price curl creams but does the job at the price point. Around £8 for 355ml.

**Cantu Wave Whip Curling Mousse** — the best-performing product for type 3 hair in the Cantu range. Lighter hold than a gel, more movement, works well on 3A–3C wash-and-gos. Around £8 for 240ml.

**Cantu Coconut Curling Cream** — solid second layer in a leave-in + cream routine for 4A and 4B hair. Not as strong as the Leave-In Repair Cream but appropriate for hair that finds the repair cream too heavy. Around £8 for 340g.

**Cantu Clarifying Shampoo** — a more thorough cleanse than their moisturising shampoo range. Good quarterly clarifying option at a budget price point. Available at Boots.

---

## Skip These

**Cantu Growth Oil** — the growth claim is not supported by evidence. This is a blend of castor oil and oils with no meaningful evidence for hair growth. The oils are fine as sealants (the castor oil base works for sealing moisture), but you're paying for a growth product that doesn't grow hair. A bottle of plain castor oil from Holland & Barrett achieves the same sealant effect for less money and without the marketing claim. → [Castor oil and hair growth: what the evidence actually says](/products/castor-oil-hair-growth-evidence-uk/)

**Cantu Complete Conditioning Co-Wash** — underperforms the As I Am Coconut CoWash at a similar price point. Doesn't have enough slip for type 4 detangling and the cleansing effect is minimal.

**Cantu Moisturizing Shampoo (regular range)** — too mild to do a proper cleansing job on hair with regular product use. Works only if hair has very low product buildup. For most type 4 routines, the Curl Clarity or Clarifying Shampoo is more useful.

**Cantu Jamaican Black Castor Oil Strengthen & Restore Shampoo** — the JBCO addition is a marketing feature, not a functional one. The shampoo itself is fine for monthly use but is not significantly better than comparable options from competitors at the same price.

## The Range's Strengths and Weaknesses

**Strengths:**
- Widely available in UK supermarkets and pharmacies — the easiest range to access without ordering online
- Generally lower glycerin content than SheaMoisture equivalents, which makes Cantu products more consistent in UK winter conditions
- Budget-friendly for a dedicated natural hair brand
- Silicone-free across most of the styling range

**Weaknesses:**
- Some products lean heavily on the Cantu name rather than formulation differentiation — the Growth Oil and JBCO range are the clearest examples
- The shampoo range is less developed than the styling and conditioning range
- Not all products are labelled with hub: parents clarity around ingredients for those managing sensitive scalps or children's hair

## FAQs

**Is Cantu good for 4C hair?**

The Leave-In Conditioning Repair Cream and the Curl Activator Cream are both appropriate for 4C hair. The brand overall skews toward the heavier moisture needs of type 4 hair and performs better there than on looser curl types.

**Is Cantu good for kids?**

The Leave-In and the moisturising shampoo are the products most commonly used on children's natural hair. The range is generally free of the harsh sulphates and silicones that are most problematic for developing hair. The Cantu Kids range (separate packaging) is specifically formulated for children's hair and is a good starting point for parents.

**Has Cantu changed its formula recently?**

There have been periodic complaints in the natural hair community about formulation changes to Cantu products. The Leave-In in particular has been subject to these complaints. If you've used a Cantu product before and it's not performing the same, a formulation change is possible. The core Shea Butter Leave-In and Coconut Curling Cream have remained closest to their original formulation.

**Cantu vs SheaMoisture — which brand is better?**

For different things. Cantu: better availability, lower price point, lower glycerin (more consistent in winter). SheaMoisture: wider range, generally more refined formulations, better deep conditioning options. The best routine often combines products from both rather than committing to one brand entirely.
