The greenhouse effect for natural hair means covering the hair with a plastic cap or bag to trap heat and moisture close to the scalp, usually overnight or for a few hours. The warmth generated by your scalp opens the cuticle and allows moisture to penetrate more deeply. It works.

The name is a loose analogy — it's not literally the same mechanism as atmospheric greenhouse warming, but the principle is accurate enough: trapped warmth + moisture = better absorption.

## When it's useful

- Chronically dry hair that doesn't respond to regular conditioning
- Low-porosity hair, where the tightly closed cuticle resists moisture entry at room temperature
- Restoring moisture mid-week without a full wash
- In winter when central heating creates a particularly dry environment

## How to do it

1. Apply a water-based leave-in conditioner or a light moisturiser to damp or lightly misted hair
2. Add a small amount of oil to seal (optional, but helps the moisture stay in once the cap comes off)
3. Cover with a plastic cap or a plastic bag — any will do
4. Leave for 30 minutes to overnight. Your scalp generates its own heat
5. Remove the cap and allow hair to finish drying as normal, or apply your styler

You don't need external heat. Your scalp is warm enough. Adding a heated cap or hooded dryer on top is sometimes called a "supercharged" greenhouse method — it works, but plain scalp heat is sufficient for most.

## What can go wrong

**Doing it too often.** Trapping moisture repeatedly without protein can tip the moisture-protein balance and leave hair mushy and over-moisturised. Once or twice a week is enough for most people.

**Covering hair that's too wet.** Hair that's wringing wet, not just damp, under a cap for hours can create conditions that cause scalp odour (a sealed, warm, wet environment is ideal for bacterial growth). Hair should feel damp, not soaking.

**Expecting it to work on hair that needs protein, not moisture.** The greenhouse effect adds moisture. If your hair is breaking because of protein deficiency, adding moisture won't fix it. Check: does your hair stretch a long way before breaking (needs protein) or snap immediately with no stretch (needs moisture)?

## Oil-only version

Some people do a greenhouse treatment with oil only — no water-based product, just oil. This works as a pre-shampoo treatment to reduce protein loss during washing, not as a moisture treatment. Oil does not add moisture; it seals and protects. If you want moisture penetration, you need water in the product underneath.

## How often

Once a week is fine for dry hair. Fortnightly or on an as-needed basis once your moisture levels stabilise.
