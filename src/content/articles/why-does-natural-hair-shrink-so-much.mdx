Natural hair shrinks because the curl pattern coils back on itself when dry — and the tighter the curl, the more the strand shortens. 4C hair can shrink to 20% of its actual length. 3C hair typically shows 50–70% shrinkage. This is normal. It means your hair is moisturised and elasticated correctly.

## Why shrinkage is a good sign

Hair that doesn't shrink is usually dry, damaged, or has been heat-trained into a looser pattern. The spring that pulls your hair upward when it dries is the same elasticity that allows your curl pattern to bounce back after manipulation. Hair without that spring tends to break more easily.

If you're losing shrinkage — hair that used to coil tightly now hangs loosely when wet — that's something worth paying attention to. It can indicate heat damage, chemical damage, or loss of moisture-protein balance.

## If shrinkage bothers you

Shrinkage isn't a problem to solve — it's a structural feature of tightly coiled hair. But there are ways to wear hair in a more elongated state without heat:

**Banding.** After washing, divide damp hair into sections and place hair ties at intervals from root to tip. As it dries, the weight and tension stretches the curl pattern. Remove the bands when dry.

**Braids or twists to dry.** Letting hair dry in braids or twists gives some elongation without the curl definition of a wash-and-go. Works well for protective styles.

**African threading.** Thread wound tightly around sections stretches the hair without heat and is one of the most effective methods for significant elongation, particularly on 4B and 4C hair.

**Stretching before protective styles.** If you're putting your hair into box braids, faux locs, or other added-hair styles, stretching beforehand makes the install easier and reduces tension.

## Heat as a stretching method

Blow-drying on low heat with a tension method (holding the hair taut while moving the dryer from roots to ends) elongates the curl temporarily and with less risk of heat damage than high-heat direct tools. Direct heat tools — flat irons, hot combs — straighten hair but at higher risk of permanent curl pattern change if used frequently.

## What to say when someone asks why your hair looks shorter

Nothing. But if you want an explanation: it's the same length it was last week. It's just coiled.
