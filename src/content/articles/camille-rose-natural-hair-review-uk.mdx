Camille Rose is a premium-positioned natural hair brand with a strong following in the UK community. The range is genuinely well-formulated across its core products — better than most brands at this price point — but it's not a brand where everything in the range earns its price. The flagship deep conditioner and the curl-defining gel are genuinely excellent. Some of the styling range doesn't justify the premium over better-value competitors.

Available in the UK primarily through Boots, LookFantastic, and Amazon. Price point is mid-to-high: £14–20 for most products.

---

## Worth Buying

**Camille Rose Algae Renew Deep Conditioning Mask** — one of the best deep conditioners in the UK natural hair market. The algae extract provides genuine slip and the formulation penetrates well on type 3 and 4A hair. Lighter than the SheaMoisture Manuka Honey masque, which makes it better suited to fine-stranded natural hair or hair that finds heavier masques weighty. Not the pick for very dry 4C (the SheaMoisture masque edges it there), but for most type 3 and 4A hair this is the best in class. Around £18 for 240g from LookFantastic.

**Camille Rose Cocoa Nibs & Honey Ultimate Moisturizing Hair Elixir** — the strongest styling product in the range. A liquid leave-in that works well as an additional moisture layer under a cream or gel. The honey is a humectant that works in the same way as glycerin but with slightly less humidity-sensitivity in winter. Around £16 for 120ml.

**Camille Rose Sweet Ginger Cleansing Rinse** — a light cleansing conditioner that works as a co-wash for type 3 and fine-stranded 4A hair. Rinses cleaner than most co-washes in this price range. Not heavy enough for 4B and 4C. Around £15 for 266ml.

**Camille Rose Moroccan Pear Conditioning Custard** — a medium-weight curl cream that produces good definition on 3C and 4A hair. The texture is lighter than most custards, which suits finer curl types that find heavy creams weighting them down. Around £16 for 240g.

---

## Skip These

**Camille Rose Curl Love Moisture Milk Strengthening Hair Milk** — the "strengthening" claim needs protein in the formulation to be real; this is primarily a light moisturising leave-in. It's a fine product but at £16 for 240ml it's priced against the As I Am Leave-In (£11 for 237ml) and loses. No formulation advantage justifies the premium.

**Camille Rose Curlaide Moisture Butter** — too heavy for most hair types outside of very dense 4C, and for 4C there are better-value options. The butter sits on the hair surface rather than absorbing. Works as a sealant but you're paying a premium for a product that does what a cheaper butter would do.

**Camille Rose Honey Hydrate Luxe Masque** — a lighter version of the Algae Renew, positioned as a weekly mask. The Algae Renew is better and there's no reason to have both. Buy the Algae Renew; use it on a schedule that works for your hair.

## The Glycerin Note

Camille Rose is high-glycerin across most of the range — glycerin appears as ingredient two or three in several products. The same winter performance issue that affects SheaMoisture applies here. → [Glycerin in natural hair products: summer vs winter UK](/products/glycerin-natural-hair-winter-summer-uk/)

In UK winter, seal over Camille Rose styling products with a light oil to slow overnight moisture evaporation.

## FAQs

**Is Camille Rose worth the price over SheaMoisture?**

For the Algae Renew masque: yes — it's the better deep conditioner for fine-stranded type 3 and 4A hair. For styling products: the SheaMoisture Coconut & Hibiscus Smoothie is comparable to the Camille Rose custard at a slightly lower price point. Choose Camille Rose for the deep conditioning; don't feel obliged to use them for everything.

**Is Camille Rose good for 4C hair?**

The Algae Renew works on 4C but the SheaMoisture Manuka Honey masque provides more conditioning for very dense, dry 4C. The Cocoa Nibs elixir works for 4C as a supplemental moisture layer. The brand generally skews toward fine-to-medium stranded hair rather than the heaviest moisture needs of dense 4C.

**Is Camille Rose available in UK stores?**

More limited than Cantu and SheaMoisture. Boots stocks some of the range in larger stores and online. LookFantastic is the most complete UK online source. It's not consistently in Superdrug or major supermarkets, which is a practical consideration if you prefer to buy in person.

**Does Camille Rose test on animals?**

Camille Rose is cruelty-free. The brand was founded by Janell Stephens as an independent natural hair brand and has maintained its cruelty-free position through its growth.
