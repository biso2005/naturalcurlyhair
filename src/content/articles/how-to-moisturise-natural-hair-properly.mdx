Natural hair that stays dry despite regular moisturising has a method problem, not a product problem. The three most common causes: applying products to dry rather than damp hair, not sealing after the leave-in, and hard water mineral buildup preventing any of it from working. Fix the method before changing the products.

---

## The Core Principle: Water In, Seal Closed

Natural coily and curly hair doesn't receive moisture from the scalp the way straight hair does. The bends and coils in the strand interrupt the flow of scalp oils down the shaft. That moisture has to come from water, applied externally.

The sequence:
1. **Water** — the actual moisture source. Always the first step.
2. **Leave-in conditioner** — provides some moisture plus helps retain it.
3. **Sealant (oil or cream)** — locks the moisture in so it doesn't evaporate.

Skipping step 3 means the moisture from step 1 and 2 evaporates within hours. Hair feels moisturised on wash day and dry by the next morning.

---

## On Wash Day

This is when the deepest moisture work happens. The hair is fully wet — this is the moment to saturate it with products before sealing.

**After rinsing conditioner:**
1. Don't towel-dry. The hair should be soaking wet.
2. Apply leave-in section by section while the hair is wet — not damp, wet. The water already in the hair is part of the moisture you're sealing in.
3. Apply oil (a few drops per section) over the leave-in, worked through with fingers.
4. Apply curl cream or butter over the oil, scrunched in.
5. Dry without touching. Touching while drying disrupts the seal and the curl pattern.

---

## Between Washes: The Daily or Every-Other-Day Refresh

Hair loses moisture continuously. Between wash days — especially in centrally-heated rooms in winter, or in London's hard water — the refresh is what keeps the routine working.

**The refresh:**
1. Lightly spritz the hair with water (a spray bottle). Enough to make it slightly damp, not soaking.
2. Apply a very small amount of leave-in or a light refreshing spray, scrunched through.
3. Seal with a small amount of the same oil or cream used on wash day.

The mistake is applying products to dry hair without water first. Products applied to dry hair sit on the surface rather than penetrating. Water opens the door; products seal it.

## Deep Conditioning

A deep conditioner is a concentrated treatment left on for 20–30 minutes, typically under heat (a warm towel, a shower cap in the shower, or a hooded dryer). It restores moisture more deeply than a rinse-out conditioner.

**When to use it:**
- Weekly for type 4 high-porosity hair
- Every two weeks for type 3 and medium porosity
- As needed for low-porosity hair (less frequent, but with heat to open the cuticle)

Deep conditioning without heat is significantly less effective on medium and low porosity hair. The heat lifts the cuticle and allows the conditioner to penetrate further.

## Why Moisturising Isn't Working

### The hair is dry when you apply products

Products applied to dry hair don't penetrate. They coat the outside of an already-dry strand. Water opens the cuticle slightly and allows product to work alongside it rather than just sitting on top.

**Fix:** always dampen the hair before applying any leave-in or refresh product. On wash day, apply to soaking-wet hair.

### No sealant

Leave-in and conditioner provide moisture. Without a sealant, that moisture evaporates. A product sequence that ends at leave-in will feel moisturised immediately and dry within a few hours.

**Fix:** oil then cream (LOC) or cream then oil (LCO) after every leave-in application.

→ [LOC vs LCO method](/hair-care/loc-vs-lco-method-natural-hair/)

### Hard water mineral buildup

Calcium and magnesium in hard water deposit on the hair shaft over months. These deposits sit between the hair and any product applied to it, preventing absorption. The routine seems to stop working gradually rather than all at once.

**Fix:** monthly chelating with a product containing EDTA or phytic acid. This removes mineral deposits and restores the hair shaft's ability to absorb products. Clarifying shampoo is not the same — it removes product buildup, not minerals.

If you're in London, the South East, East Anglia, or the Midlands and haven't chelated recently, this is probably the issue.

→ [Hard water and curly hair UK](/hair-care/hard-water-curly-hair-uk/)

### Too much product or the wrong weight

More product is not more moisture. A heavy cream applied in large quantities to fine-stranded hair creates buildup that blocks moisture from reaching the strand. The hair feels coated rather than moisturised.

**Fix:** use products appropriate for your hair's weight and porosity. High porosity type 4 hair can handle heavier products. Fine wavy hair needs much lighter products. When in doubt, use less and add if needed.

### Protein imbalance

Over-proteined hair feels dry and stiff despite moisture application. The protein coating prevents water from penetrating.

**Fix:** run the stretch test. If the hair barely stretches and snaps immediately, protein overload is the issue, not moisture method.

## FAQs

**How often should I moisturise between wash days?**

For type 4 hair: every one to two days. For type 3: every two to three days. For type 2: every three to four days, or when the hair feels dry. UK central heating dries hair faster in winter — increase frequency between October and March.

**My leave-in conditioner makes my hair feel sticky. What's wrong?**

Either the leave-in contains too much hold ingredient for your hair type, or you're applying too much. Try a lighter leave-in or use a smaller amount. The hair should feel soft and moisturised, not coated.

**I moisturise every day and the hair is still dry. What haven't I tried?**

Chelating. If you're in a hard water area and haven't chelated in the last six weeks, do that before anything else. Mineral deposits are the most common missed cause of persistent dryness despite a correct routine.

**Should I use water or a leave-in spray for the daily refresh?**

Either. Plain water from a spray bottle is free and works. A water-based refreshing spray adds a small amount of leave-in and a light hold, which can extend definition. Both need a sealant applied over them.
