Bristol has a well-established Afro-Caribbean community, particularly in St Pauls, Easton, and Barton Hill, and an Afro hair salon scene that's proportionate to that community. For a city of its size, Bristol has a reasonable range of options — though outside these areas and the city centre, the salon desert problem applies.

**Bristol water:** moderately hard. Bristol Water data shows moderate hardness — harder than the north of England and Wales but softer than London. A chelating shampoo monthly or bi-monthly is worthwhile for natural hair in Bristol.

## How to Find a Bristol Afro Hair Salon

**Instagram.** Search "#BristolNaturalHair", "#BristolBraids", "#AfroHairBristol". Bristol has a visible natural hair community on Instagram; area tags surface both salon and mobile stylists.

**Facebook groups.** Bristol-specific Black community and natural hair Facebook groups. Recommendations from members whose hair you can see are the most reliable route.

**Google Maps.** "Afro hair salon Bristol" or "natural hair Bristol". Read reviews for mentions of natural hair types specifically.

**St Pauls.** The highest concentration of Afro hair businesses in Bristol. The St Pauls Carnival community network is also a useful informal referral source.
