Mielle Organics gained a significant UK following after the Rosemary Oil went viral. The rosemary oil is genuinely a good scalp oil — not because rosemary grows hair (the evidence is weak at the growth claim) but because it works well as a light scalp treatment for scalp massage. The brand's broader range is more variable: the curl smoothie is one of the better styling products at its price point; some of the newer range extensions don't match the original products' quality.

Available at Boots, Superdrug, and online. Mid-range pricing: £11–16 for most products.

---

## Worth Buying

**Mielle Organics Pomegranate & Honey Curl Smoothie** — the strongest styling product in the range. A light-to-medium curl cream that defines the coil without weighing it down, works well as the second step in a leave-in + cream routine for 4A and 4B hair, and produces good definition on twist-outs and braid-outs. Available at Boots. Around £11 for 240ml.

**Mielle Organics Rosemary Mint Scalp & Hair Strengthening Oil** — the viral scalp oil. Works well as a lightweight scalp treatment that absorbs without leaving the scalp greasy or the hair heavy. The rosemary essential oil provides a cooling sensation that makes scalp massage more pleasant, which increases how often you do it — and scalp massage has weak-but-better-than-castor-oil evidence for improving hair density. Available at Boots and Superdrug. Around £10 for 59ml.

**What the rosemary oil doesn't do:** grow hair significantly. The brand's claims around the rosemary oil are calibrated carefully; the community's claims about it are sometimes less so. Use it because it's a good scalp oil and because regular scalp massage is worth doing. Don't use it expecting the hairline recession to reverse in three months.

**Mielle Organics Honey & Ginger Styling Gel** — a more moisturising gel than Eco Style, slightly less hold. Better for 4A hair that finds Eco Style drying even with products layered underneath. Available at Boots. Around £12 for 227ml.

**Mielle Organics White Peony Leave-In Conditioner** — a lighter leave-in than most of the range. Works well for fine-stranded 4A or type 3 hair that finds the brand's other leave-ins heavy. Around £12 for 240ml.

## Skip These

**Mielle Organics Babassu Oil & Mint Deep Conditioner** — frequently mentioned as a deep conditioner by the natural hair community, and it's a genuinely good conditioning product. But at £14 for 227ml it's more expensive than the SheaMoisture Manuka Honey masque (£12 for 326g) and the Camille Rose Algae Renew (£18 for 240g), and it delivers less conditioning effect than either per gram of product. It works better as a leave-in on very dry 4C hair than as a standard deep conditioner.

**Mielle Organics Mongongo Oil Protein-Free Moisturizing Conditioner** — fine but priced above its performance. A rinse-out conditioner at £13 for 355ml that doesn't outperform the Cantu Leave-In used similarly, which costs considerably less.

**Mielle Organics Rice Water & Aloe Vera Collection** — the rice water marketing wave has produced another sub-range across multiple brands. The Mielle rice water range is not better than the brand's original products and at higher price points.

## The Rosemary Oil FAQ Addendum

Because the Mielle Rosemary Oil is the product most readers arrive here asking about:

**Does the rosemary oil grow edges back?** Not reliably. Removing the source of traction and resting the area is what recovers edges. The rosemary oil scalp massage may marginally help with blood flow. It's not the primary treatment. → [Thinning edges on natural hair UK](/hair-care/thinning-edges-natural-hair-uk/)

**Can I use the rosemary oil directly on my scalp?** Yes — it's already diluted in a carrier oil base. Do not use undiluted rosemary essential oil directly on the scalp; the Mielle product is formulated for direct scalp application.

## FAQs

**Is Mielle good for type 4C hair?**

The Pomegranate & Honey Curl Smoothie works on 4C for twist-outs and braid-outs. For wash-and-gos on dense 4C, the hold isn't enough without layering a gel over the top. The brand skews toward 4A and 4B and performs best there.

**Is Mielle Organics a Black-owned brand?**

Yes. Mielle was founded by Monique Rodriguez in 2014 as an independent Black-owned natural hair brand. In 2023, Procter & Gamble acquired a majority stake. The founder retained a leadership role and the brand's positioning has been maintained post-acquisition, though the acquisition is notable for the community.

**Is there a cheaper alternative to the Mielle Rosemary Oil?**

Yes. The rosemary oil scalp treatment can be replicated with a jojoba or grapeseed carrier oil and a few drops of rosemary essential oil (not applied undiluted). Boots and Holland & Barrett sell rosemary essential oil. The DIY version costs a fraction of the Mielle product and does the same thing.

**Does Mielle still use organic ingredients?**

The "Organics" in the name refers to the brand's positioning at founding. Not all products contain USDA-certified organic ingredients. Check individual product labels if organic certification matters to you.
