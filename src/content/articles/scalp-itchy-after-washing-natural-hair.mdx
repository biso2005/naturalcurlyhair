An itchy scalp immediately or shortly after washing is almost always caused by one of four things: product residue that wasn't fully rinsed, a reaction to an ingredient in your shampoo or conditioner, scalp dryness from over-cleansing, or the start of a scalp condition that needs a different approach.

## Product that wasn't fully rinsed

The most common cause. Natural hair is dense and takes a long time to rinse thoroughly, especially at the roots. Conditioner left on the scalp — or shampoo residue — creates a film that irritates as it dries.

**Fix:** Rinse longer than you think is necessary, especially at the scalp. In sections. Tilt your head so the water runs directly from roots to tips rather than across the scalp.

## Ingredient reaction

Some people react to specific ingredients in cleansing and conditioning products: sulphates (particularly sodium lauryl sulphate), fragrances, certain preservatives, or essential oils used as scalp treatments. The reaction tends to show up consistently with one particular product and clear up when you stop using it.

**Fix:** Isolate which product causes the reaction by switching one product at a time. If you've recently changed your shampoo and the itching started at the same time, start there.

## Over-cleansing

Washing natural hair with a strong sulphate shampoo every week, or more often, can strip the scalp's natural oils and cause reactive itching. The scalp becomes dry and irritated, and may also begin overproducing oil to compensate — which creates a cycle of itching and needing to wash more often.

**Fix:** Reduce wash frequency, or switch to a gentler sulphate-free shampoo for weekly washing and reserve a clarifying wash for once or twice a month. Natural hair generally does well with one to two washes per week.

## Hard water mineral residue

In hard water areas, minerals in the tap water combine with shampoo and form a soap scum that deposits on the scalp. This residue doesn't rinse out cleanly and can cause itching that regular washing doesn't resolve.

**Fix:** A chelating shampoo once a month removes the mineral layer. If you're in London, the Home Counties, or the East Midlands, this is worth trying before concluding you have a scalp condition. [Check your water hardness.](/tools/water-hardness/)

## Scalp conditions

If the itching is accompanied by visible flaking, redness, or patches, or if it persists despite changing products and improving rinsing, the most likely culprits are:

- **Seborrheic dermatitis** — a fungal condition causing itching and flaking, common and treatable. A shampoo containing zinc pyrithione (Head & Shoulders, or prescription-strength equivalents) or ketoconazole is first-line treatment.
- **Scalp psoriasis** — causes thicker, silvery plaques rather than fine dandruff flakes. Needs GP assessment.
- **Contact dermatitis** — a reaction to something touching the scalp (new product, hair dye, a fabric). Usually clears when the irritant is removed.

If over-the-counter dandruff shampoo doesn't resolve the itching within four to six weeks, see your GP. Don't manage an undiagnosed scalp condition with home remedies indefinitely.

## Order to try

1. Rinse more thoroughly
2. Remove the most recently added new product
3. Reduce wash frequency or switch to a gentler shampoo
4. If in a hard water area, try a chelating shampoo
5. If flaking or redness is present, try a zinc pyrithione shampoo for four weeks
6. If nothing works, see a GP or dermatologist
