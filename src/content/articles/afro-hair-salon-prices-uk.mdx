Afro hair salon pricing in the UK is widely variable and rarely published clearly — which means first-time clients often don't know what they're walking into. This is the honest pricing guide: real ranges for common services, what drives the variation, and what to do when a salon won't tell you the price before you book.

---

## What Drives the Variation

**Location.** London salon prices are typically 30–50% higher than equivalent services in regional cities, which are in turn higher than suburban and home-based stylists. The rent differential is real and reflected in service pricing.

**Stylist experience and reputation.** A senior stylist with fifteen years of experience and a booked-out appointment diary charges more than a recently qualified stylist. The premium is usually worth it for major services.

**Style complexity and time.** Box braids at shoulder length take 4–6 hours; at waist length they take 6–10. Time is the primary driver of braiding service prices. If a quote seems low, ask how long the service will take — speed and cost are related.

**Hair length and density.** Many salons price by service type at a base rate, then add for length and density. Ask specifically whether the quoted price applies to your hair length and density, not just the style.

**Extensions included or separate.** Some salons include extensions in the braiding price; others charge separately. This dramatically changes the apparent price comparison between salons. Ask explicitly: "Does that include extensions, and what type?"

---

## Red Flags on Pricing

**No price quoted until after the service is complete.** Ask for a firm price or firm range before booking any service. A salon that won't give you a price before you sit in the chair is one where the final bill may surprise you.

**"Depends on your hair" without further detail.** "Your hair type, length, and density will affect the price" is a legitimate answer when followed by specific ranges. "It depends" on its own is not.

**Significantly below-market prices for time-intensive styles.** Box braids quoted at £40 in London in 2024 should prompt questions — either the service will be rushed, the quality will be lower than expected, or there will be significant add-ons at the end.

---

## Tipping

Tipping in UK salons is not as expected as in the US, but a tip for good service is appreciated. £5–10 for standard services; £15–20 for long or complex services where you were particularly happy with the result. There is no standard percentage expectation in the UK hair industry.

## FAQs

**I was charged significantly more than the quoted price. What can I do?**

Ask for an itemised breakdown at the time of payment. If a salon has added charges not disclosed before the service, query them on the spot — it is much harder to resolve after you've left. **The stylist quoted £80 but added £30 for extensions at the end. Is that normal?**

If extensions were not disclosed as a separate cost when you booked, it's not a fair charge to add at the end. Some salons do charge separately and should say so when you book. The fix is to ask explicitly when booking: "Is that price inclusive of extensions?"

**Are these prices for natural hair or can I get the same style done more cheaply as a general salon?**

General (non-specialist) salons may charge less but frequently lack the experience with natural Afro-textured hair to produce equivalent results. A cheaper quote from a stylist without natural hair experience is not necessarily a saving — the cost of a correction at a specialist can exceed the original price difference.

**Why do Sisterlocks cost so much?**

Sisterlocks installation is a very long service (8–16+ hours), requires a certified Sisterlocks consultant trained by Sisterlocks Inc., uses a proprietary tool and technique, and is followed by a required three-month retightening session.
