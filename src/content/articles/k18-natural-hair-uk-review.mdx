K18 works on natural hair with chemical or heat damage, and for some use cases it works better than Olaplex on coily hair specifically. The key differences: K18 is a leave-in (no rinse required), acts faster (four minutes), and its mechanism is slightly different from Olaplex's. For coily natural hair, the leave-in format and shorter treatment time are practical advantages.

---

## What K18 Does

K18's active ingredient is a synthetic peptide (K18PEPTIDE) that mimics a segment of the hair's own keratin chain. The peptide attaches to broken keratin chains in the hair cortex and reconnects them — similar in effect to Olaplex's disulphide bond relinking, but through a different molecular pathway.

The result: improved hair strength, elasticity, and smoothness in hair where the keratin chain has been damaged. K18 also partially repairs disulphide bonds in addition to the keratin chain reconnection, which is why the two products can be used together.

## K18 vs Olaplex on Coily Natural Hair

**K18 advantages for coily hair:**
- Leave-in format: K18 is applied to washed hair, left for four minutes, and not rinsed. Subsequent styling products are applied over it. This removes one step from the process and reduces mechanical manipulation during application and rinse.
- Speed: four minutes vs Olaplex No. 3's thirty minutes to overnight. For coily hair where minimising time spent manipulating is a consideration, this matters.
- Surface smoothing: K18 has a noticeable smoothing effect on the hair cuticle that Olaplex doesn't provide to the same degree. This produces better slip and manageability in the week following treatment.

**Olaplex advantages:**
- Longer track record and more peer-reviewed evidence
- No. 0 + No. 3 protocol is more intensive for severe bond damage
- Price per treatment is somewhat lower

**For most natural hair use cases:** K18 and Olaplex address the same damage type. If you have chemical or heat damage, either works. K18's leave-in format makes it more practical in a coily hair routine. Olaplex's No. 0 + No. 3 protocol may be more effective for severe damage.

## When K18 Is Appropriate for Natural Hair

**Yes — for this:**
- Bleached or coloured natural hair
- Moderate heat damage (flat iron, excessive blow drying)
- Transitioning from relaxed to natural — improving the integrity of the remaining relaxed portions
- Natural hair that's been frequently in tight protective styles with significant mechanical stress over time

**No — not appropriate for this:**
- General moisturising or conditioning — K18 is not a moisture product
- Unprocessed natural hair with no heat or chemical damage history — nothing to repair
- Breakage from dryness alone — that needs moisture, not structural repair
- As a protein treatment substitute — K18 addresses a different mechanism than hydrolysed protein

## How to Use K18 on Natural Hair

1. Shampoo as normal (K18 works on clean hair, not over product buildup)
2. Do not condition before K18 — the peptide needs to reach the hair shaft without a conditioner barrier
3. Apply K18 Leave-In Molecular Repair Hair Mask to damp hair, section by section
4. Wait four minutes — do not rinse
5. Apply styling products over the K18 as normal
6. Deep condition on the next wash day as usual — the K18 does not replace moisture treatments

**Frequency:** monthly for actively damaged hair; every six to eight weeks for maintenance. Daily use is not needed and excessive use on undamaged hair provides no additional benefit.

## FAQs

**Can I use K18 and a protein treatment in the same routine?**

Not in the same session. Both address structural issues in the hair, and using both together risks protein overload. Alternate: K18 one month, hydrolysed protein treatment the next, if both are needed. In practice, most hair with chemical damage benefits more from K18 than from a standard protein treatment — they're addressing similar (not identical) damage.

**Can I use K18 and Olaplex?**

Yes — they work through different pathways and can complement each other. The community-established protocol is to use Olaplex first (No. 0 + No. 3 for thirty minutes to overnight), then shampoo, then apply K18 (four minutes, no rinse). Don't do both in the same session more than monthly.

**My natural hair isn't chemically processed but I've had a lot of tight braids and mechanical stress. Will K18 help?**

Possibly. Severe mechanical stress — years of tight braids, heat manipulation, aggressive detangling — can cause cumulative cortex damage. K18 may improve the hair's behaviour. It's not guaranteed to address purely mechanical damage the way it addresses chemical or heat damage, but it's not harmful to try.

**Is K18 better than a bond-builder on a budget?**

If budget is the constraint, a consistent protein treatment (ORS Hair Mayo, Aphogee Two-Minute) addresses some of the same structural issues K18 addresses, at much lower cost. The mechanism is different — protein fills cuticle gaps rather than repairing the cortex — but for mild damage in an unprocessed natural hair routine, the protein treatment is the better value. K18 is the right tool for significant chemical or heat damage, not for general maintenance.
