Faux locs are synthetic or semi-synthetic loc extensions attached to natural hair — they look like mature locs without the commitment of actually starting them. They're a significant time and money investment: expect 5–10 hours in the salon and £100–£280 depending on length, size, and method. The result is a style that typically lasts six to eight weeks.

---

## Types of Faux Locs in the UK

### Wrapped faux locs

A braided base (usually box braids or two-strand twists) over which extension hair is wrapped — either synthetic hair or distressed kanekalon — to create the loc appearance. The most common method. The wrapping is done by the stylist and takes the most time.

### Crochet faux locs

Pre-made loc extensions crocheted onto a cornrow base. Faster to install than wrapped faux locs — two to four hours rather than six to ten — and therefore cheaper. The result looks similar but the locs may move slightly differently and the weight distribution is different.

### Goddess faux locs (boho locs)

Wrapped faux locs with sections of curly extension hair left free at intervals, creating a more textured, less uniform look. More styling time; higher cost.

### Butterfly locs / distressed locs

A looser wrapping technique that creates a more textured, unravelled-looking loc. Popular in the UK. Uses distressed wavy hair that gives a different texture from the standard smooth wrapped loc.

## What to Expect at the Appointment

A wrapped faux locs appointment on waist-length is a full-day commitment — bring food, entertainment, and comfortable clothes. Crochet faux locs are significantly faster.

**The base:** most stylists braid or twist the natural hair into a base first, then attach the extension hair. This takes one to two hours before the actual loc installation begins.

**Weight:** faux locs are heavy, particularly long faux locs. The weight is distributed across the full head rather than concentrated at the root (unlike box braids), which makes them more comfortable over time — but the initial heaviness of long faux locs can cause tension headaches in the first 48 hours, particularly at the nape.

**Tension:** the attachment point — where the extension loc connects to the natural hair — carries all the tension. The hairline and nape are most vulnerable. Say something if you feel significant pulling during installation. A tight attachment that causes pain during installation will cause traction over the weeks the style is in.

---

## Weight and the Nape

This is the thing most faux locs guides skip. Long faux locs — waist-length or longer — accumulate significant weight at the nape when the hair falls backward (sleeping, leaning). This weight is different from box braid weight because the locs are thicker and stiffer.

**What this means:**
- The nape hairline is more vulnerable to traction with very long faux locs
- Sleeping without supporting the weight can pull the nape hair over months
- A satin pillowcase and gathering the locs loosely before sleep reduces this

If your nape is already fragile or you have history of nape thinning, consider medium-length rather than waist-length faux locs.

## Care While In

**Scalp moisture every two to three days:** the same as any protective style — lightweight spray or oil applied directly to the scalp between the attachment points.

**Night protection:** a very large satin bonnet if the locs are short enough, or gathering into a loose low puff with a satin scrunchie for longer styles. The satin surface reduces friction on the loc exterior, which would otherwise frizz and distort.

**Scalp washing:** every two weeks. Diluted shampoo applied to the scalp through the style, rinsed thoroughly, dried fully. The interior of the faux loc can trap water — take extra time to dry the base of each loc where the natural hair and extension hair join.

**Hard water note:** the extension hair on faux locs becomes rough and dull faster in hard water areas than soft water. An ACV rinse after scalp washing helps. The loc exterior is the most visible surface in faux locs (unlike box braids where the kanekalon is somewhat hidden inside the braid) so mineral dullness shows more.

## Removal

Budget two to three hours. The extension hair is wrapped or crocheted over the natural hair — it needs to be carefully unwrapped or cut away before the natural hair can be released.

**Method:**
1. Cut or unpick the loc at the end to free the extension hair
2. Carefully unwrap the extension hair from the top down, holding the natural hair base
3. When the natural hair is exposed, gently unravel the base twist or braid
4. Apply conditioner generously and detangle each section before moving to the next

After removal: wash and deep condition before any next style. Give the hairline and nape a specific rest period without tension.

## FAQs

**Can I get faux locs on short hair?**

Short natural hair needs a minimum length for the base braiding or twisting — approximately three to four centimetres. Very short hair may not hold the attachment point securely. Ask the stylist about minimum length for the method they use.

**Will faux locs damage my natural hair?**

Done correctly, with appropriate tension and proper care, they shouldn't. Done with excessive tension, left in too long, or removed incorrectly, they cause the same traction issues as any extension style. The attachment point and the weight on the hairline and nape are the primary risk areas.

**Can I swim in faux locs?**

Yes, with a swim cap. Chlorine affects the extension hair quickly, making it rough and stiff. Rinse thoroughly after swimming and dry fully. Extended water contact makes the faux loc exterior distress faster.

**My faux locs are very heavy and causing headaches. What do I do?**

Give it 48 hours — the same tension headache pattern as any installation. If it doesn't ease, the attachment point may be too tight. Some stylists can loosen specific sections. If the weight is from the length (very long locs), wearing them up and piled on top of the head rather than hanging reduces nape strain.
