Porosity matters for 3A hair for a specific reason: the wide S-curl is easily weighed down. The wrong product weight for your porosity gives you either limp, heavy curls (if products are too thick for low porosity) or dry, frizzy curls that won't hold definition (if products are too light for high porosity). Getting porosity right is how you narrow down what actually works before spending money on products.

## What Porosity Means for 3A Hair

**Low porosity 3A:**
- Hair resists wetting — takes longer than expected to fully saturate under running water
- Products tend to ball up or sit on the curl surface rather than absorbing
- Buildup appears quickly, within a few wash days
- Heat during conditioning makes a noticeable difference — conditioner without heat barely absorbs
- Hair can feel moisturised right after washing then surprisingly dry a few hours later

**High porosity 3A:**
- Hair wets almost instantly
- Absorbs product readily but loses moisture just as fast
- Frizz in humidity is more pronounced — open cuticle catches atmospheric moisture unevenly
- Ends feel dry or rough even after conditioning
- Common in chemically treated, colour-treated, or heat-styled 3A hair

**Medium porosity 3A:** The most common category for 3A. Most mainstream curly and wavy hair products are designed with medium-porosity 3A–3B hair in mind, which is why product selection is often easier at this type than at type 4.

---

## Why Porosity Matters More on 3A Than 3B or 3C

3A's wide, loose curl is the most susceptible to weight. A high-porosity 3A routine that relies on heavy butters and thick creams (appropriate for some type 4 high-porosity hair) will flatten the S-loop before the day is over. A low-porosity routine that uses heavy products because "they didn't absorb otherwise" creates the same weight problem.

Getting porosity right means you can use the minimum product weight that actually works — which on 3A means the curl stays defined and bouncy rather than weighed down.

## The Float Test: Not Reliable for 3A

The wide S-curl in 3A hair traps air inside the loop. That air keeps the strand afloat regardless of cuticle state, making it indistinguishable from a genuinely low-porosity strand. Skip it.

---

## Two Tests That Work

### The spray bottle test

Spritz a small section of clean, dry 3A hair with plain water. Watch for 30 seconds.

- Water **beads on the surface** and takes time to absorb — low porosity
- Water **absorbs quickly** and the section darkens — high porosity
- Water **sinks in but slowly** — medium porosity

Do this after a clarifying or chelating wash. Product residue and mineral deposits (from hard water) can make low-porosity hair appear to absorb faster than it does naturally.

### The feel test

Run a single clean, product-free strand between fingers from tip to root.

- **Smooth, almost slippery** — flat cuticle, likely low porosity
- **Slightly rough or snags** — raised cuticle, likely high porosity
- **Very rough or breaks** — high porosity with damage

---

## Roots vs Ends: Why 3A Often Has Mixed Porosity

Roots are recent growth — lower porosity. Ends have been through years of washing, UV, and manipulation — higher porosity. If your 3A roots feel moisturised but your ends are consistently dry and frizzy, this is why.

The practical adjustment: concentrate conditioner and sealant on the ends, use lighter products at the roots. The spray bottle test on roots and ends separately confirms whether this applies.

## What to Do Based on Your Result

### Low porosity 3A

The challenge is getting moisture past the cuticle without weighing the curl down.

- **Heat during conditioning.** A warm damp towel over a shower cap for 20 minutes makes conditioner actually absorb rather than sitting on the surface. This is the most effective single change for low-porosity 3A.
- **Lightweight liquid leave-in only.** Thick creams sit on top of a low-porosity 3A curl rather than absorbing, and create the appearance of product working while adding zero moisture to the hair.
- **Apply to the wettest possible hair.** Don't let the hair partially air dry before applying leave-in.
- **Clarify monthly.** Buildup happens faster on low-porosity hair — products pile up because they don't penetrate. A monthly clarifying wash keeps the surface clear.
- **Skip heavy oils.** A few drops of a lightweight oil (jojoba, argan) is plenty. Castor oil and shea butter will weigh the S-loop down.

### High porosity 3A

The challenge is keeping moisture in the curl long enough to matter.

- **Seal after leave-in.** A light oil or cream applied while the hair is still wet acts as a barrier against moisture evaporation. Don't skip this step.
- **Medium-weight products rather than very light ones.** High-porosity 3A loses moisture faster than medium-porosity — a slightly heavier leave-in holds better. Not cream-heavy, but not a pure liquid mist either.
- **Occasional protein.** High-porosity 3A that's been heat-styled or colour-treated benefits from a protein treatment every six to eight weeks. When hair starts feeling mushy or over-stretchy when wet, that's the signal. Don't overdo it — too much protein makes 3A coils stiff.
- **Satin bonnet at night.** High-porosity hair loses moisture to cotton pillowcases faster. Worth doing for all curl types, but critical for high porosity.

### Medium porosity 3A

- Most flexibility — the widest range of products works
- Focus on the UK seasonal adjustment: lighter products in summer, slightly more sealing in winter
- Adjust based on how the hair responds, not a strict protocol

## Hard Water and Porosity Testing

In a hard water area, mineral deposits on the 3A curl can make the spray bottle test read incorrectly. Mineral film on a low-porosity curl makes it appear to absorb water faster (the mineral layer is absorbing, not the hair). Chelate first, then test.

## FAQs

**My 3A products work on wash day but the curl is dry and frizzy by day two. Is that porosity?**

High porosity or missing sealant. Check whether you're applying oil or cream after your leave-in, while the hair is still wet. If you are, try a slightly heavier sealant or a protein treatment and see if retention improves.

**I've been using creams and butters because my 3A hair felt dry. It still looks dry and now it's limp. What happened?**

You likely have low porosity 3A — the heavy products can't absorb and are just sitting on the curl surface adding weight. Do a clarifying wash, test your porosity, and switch to lightweight liquids plus heat during conditioning.

**Is there any porosity that doesn't suit 3A?**

All three work fine with the right products. Low porosity 3A + correct lightweight products + heat = healthy curl. High porosity 3A + correct sealing routine = healthy curl. The problems happen when the product weight mismatches the porosity.

**Do I need to test porosity more than once?**

Re-test if your routine stops working for no obvious reason, after any chemical treatment, or if your hair has changed significantly in texture. Porosity shifts over time and with damage.
