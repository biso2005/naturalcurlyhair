3C wash day is generally more forgiving than type 4 — the corkscrew coil distributes conditioner more readily, tangles are less structural, and the hair takes a wash-and-go reliably when the products are right. But there are two UK-specific factors that most 3C guides miss: hard water across most of England degrading coil definition, and the outdoor/indoor humidity swing that means a product routine working in summer can fail in winter.

This routine accounts for both.

---

## Before You Wash: Pre-Poo

Optional for most 3C hair, more useful after longer stretches between washes or in winter. Apply oil or conditioner to dry hair section by section before shampooing — it adds slip and softens the hair before the friction of washing.

For 3C hair, lighter oils work fine for pre-poo: coconut, jojoba, or even your usual conditioner. Leave 20 minutes, then wash.

---

## Shampoo

### Weekly or bi-weekly: gentle shampoo or co-wash

3C hair handles co-washing well between shampoo sessions — the corkscrew coil benefits from slip and the extra conditioning. A co-wash or gentle sulphate-free shampoo every week or two, with a proper shampoo every two to four weeks depending on how much product you use.

Signs you need a shampoo rather than a co-wash: scalp itching, hair feeling heavy or weighed down, definition that's gone waxy rather than soft.

### Monthly: chelating shampoo

If you're in a hard water area — London, South East, East Anglia, Midlands — add a chelating shampoo once a month. Hard water deposits minerals on the corkscrew coil with every wash, disrupting the spiral shape and blocking conditioner. Chelating removes them.

Check the label for EDTA or phytic acid. Not a clarifying shampoo — those remove product buildup but not minerals.

## Deep Conditioning

Apply to soaking-wet hair in sections. 3C tends toward moisture-first conditioning most of the time. A moisture conditioner weekly and a light protein treatment every six to eight weeks (or when the hair starts feeling mushy or stretchy) is a reasonable baseline.

**Use heat.** A heat cap, steamer, or warm damp towel over a shower cap improves results for all 3C hair, but especially if you have lower-porosity coils where the cuticle resists moisture. 20–30 minutes minimum.

**Detangle while the conditioner is in.** 3C tangles less severely than type 4, but detangling with conditioner in — before rinsing — is still easier and causes less breakage than detangling afterwards. Start at the ends, work upward in sections.

## Rinsing

Lukewarm or cool water. A cool final rinse helps coils snap back to definition and adds shine.

In hard water areas: consider a distilled water pour-over as your last rinse step. You've just chelated or clarified the mineral layer off the hair — a tap water final rinse re-deposits some. A jug of distilled water from the supermarket costs almost nothing per wash.

---

## Applying Products

Everything on soaking-wet hair. The wetter, the better — water is the primary moisturiser.

**Leave-in conditioner:** first layer, applied section by section. For 3C, mid-weight leave-ins work better than either very heavy butters (which can weigh down corkscrew coils) or very lightweight sprays (which often aren't enough on their own). Look for something with water as the first ingredient and some slip.

**Oil (optional):** a light sealing oil over the leave-in helps extend moisture retention. Jojoba, argan, and sweet almond are the right weight for most 3C hair. If your coils look weighed down rather than defined, you may not need oil in addition to a cream or gel.

**Styler:** either a curl cream for a softer result or a gel for stronger hold and frizz control in UK humidity. Apply and define using your preferred method:

- **Shingling/raking:** run fingers or a wide-tooth comb through each section to define individual coils
- **Scrunching:** press hair upward into the palm to encourage natural clumping — gives a less regimented result
- **Praying hands:** press the section between both palms and smooth downward, then scrunch — smooths the cuticle before coils form

**UK winter adjustment:** in cold months, reduce how much glycerin is in your styler. High-glycerin products in low-humidity indoor air can pull moisture out of 3C hair rather than keeping it in. Switch to a lower-glycerin hold product or increase your sealing step.

## Drying

**Don't use a rough towel.** A microfibre towel or old cotton t-shirt to scrunch out excess water — no rubbing, no wringing.

**Air dry where possible.** Diffusing works well on 3C but high heat disrupts the coil pattern as it forms. Low heat, diffuser cup held upward scrunching the hair in, until mostly dry — then air dry the rest.

**Don't touch until dry.** 3C hair touched while the coil is forming gives frizz. Leave it. When fully dry, scrunch out any hard gel cast with a small amount of oil on your palms.

---

## Night Routine

**Satin bonnet or pillowcase.** 3C coils lose moisture to cotton overnight and develop frizz from friction. Satin is the fix.

**Pineapple for loose styles.** Gather hair loosely at the very top of the head with a satin scrunchie. This protects the coil structure while sleeping without compressing it.

**Refresh the next morning.** A light spritz of water (distilled or filtered if you're in a hard water area) and a small amount of leave-in or gel scrunched in revives 3C coils for the next day without a full wash.

---

## Troubleshooting

**Coils fall apart by day two:** check your hold product and whether you're sealing properly. A stronger gel or adding an oil sealant before the gel can extend the style.

**Frizzy the moment you step outside:** UK humidity. A stronger-hold product as the top layer creates a better barrier. Avoid touching the hair once outside.

**Conditioner feels like it does nothing:** most likely hard water. Check your postcode, test your water, add a chelating wash.

**Wash day takes longer than expected:** often the detangling step. Move it to during conditioning (not after rinsing) and work in smaller sections.

## FAQs

**Can I co-wash 3C hair every week?**

Yes, if you're not using heavy products that need shampoo to remove. If you're using butters, heavy creams, or silicone serums, a gentle shampoo every two to three weeks stops buildup from making coils feel heavy.

**How long should 3C wash day take?**

One to two hours for a thorough session including conditioning time. Significantly less than type 4 because 3C tangles and distributes product more easily.

**Do I need a deep conditioner or can I use regular conditioner?**

A rinse-out conditioner left on for five minutes works for some 3C hair. If your coils feel dry or frizzy after conditioning, upgrade to a proper deep conditioner and add heat. The difference is usually significant.

**Why does my 3C hair feel great on wash day and then progressively worse?**

Usually moisture loss — either no sealant or insufficient sealant, letting moisture evaporate between wash days. Protect with a satin bonnet, refresh mid-week, and check that your sealing step is happening while the hair is still wet.
