3C hair sits in a genuinely confusing spot in the curl typing system. It's tight enough to shrink significantly and misbehave in humidity. It's defined enough to hold a wash-and-go that 4A or 4B hair struggles to maintain. And it overlaps enough with 4A that plenty of 3C naturals have spent years following type 4 advice that was never quite right for their hair.

This guide is specifically for 3C hair in the UK: what the corkscrew coil means in practice, how it differs from 4A, and a routine built around how 3C actually behaves — including the hard water and humidity factors that shift everything.

---

## What Is 3C Hair?

3C hair forms tight corkscrew coils — consistent spirals, roughly pencil-width to straw-width in diameter. Unlike 4A's very tight S-coil or 4B's angular Z-pattern, 3C coils have a recognisable, consistent loop. When dry, 3C hair shows visible curl definition without any product — the coils are tight but distinct.

Key characteristics:

- **Corkscrew spiral** — consistent loop, pencil to straw width
- **Visible definition when dry** — unlike 4A, 4B, 4C where definition is harder to see without product
- **Significant shrinkage** — typically 40–60% — less extreme than type 4 but still enough to frustrate length retention
- **Volume** — 3C hair has natural volume that can read as frizz in the wrong humidity
- **Prone to dryness** — not as acutely as type 4, but the coil still interrupts oil travel from the scalp

### The 3C/4A overlap — being honest about it

A lot of people with 3C hair have spent time in 4A communities, or vice versa. The practical distinction: 3C coils have a visible, consistent loop when dry. 4A coils are smaller, tighter, and may compress into a texture that looks less defined without product.

Many people have both on the same head — looser 3C at the crown or temples, tighter 4A at the back or nape. If that's you, your routine needs to account for both. The care principles overlap considerably; the main practical difference is product weight and how much hold you need.

### 3C vs 3B and 4A

| Type | Pattern | Diameter | Shrinkage |
|------|---------|----------|-----------|
| 3B | Springy ringlets | Marker-width | Moderate |
| 3C | Tight corkscrew coils | Pencil to straw width | High |
| 4A | Very tight S-coils | Crochet-needle width | Very high |

## Why the Corkscrew Changes How 3C Behaves

**In humidity:** The corkscrew coil catches atmospheric moisture from all sides. Outdoors in a UK drizzle, 3C hair can go from defined to frizzy and voluminous within minutes. The coil unravels as it absorbs moisture and then re-sets unevenly as it dries. This is the most common frustration for 3C readers in the UK — the weather undoes the work.

**In hard water:** Mineral deposits from hard water coat the corkscrew coil and disrupt its natural spiral shape. Instead of lying in a consistent loop, mineral-coated 3C coils feel rough, look dull, and lose definition faster than they should. The coil's all-round surface exposure means more mineral contact than a flatter, more linear strand.

**With product distribution:** 3C coils are self-organising in a way 4B and 4C hair isn't — the consistent spiral creates natural clumping. This means 3C can hold a wash-and-go more reliably, but it also means that product unevenness shows up as uneven definition rather than general frizz.

## A Routine for 3C Hair in the UK

### Hard water first

If you're in London, the South East, East Anglia, or the Midlands, hard water is actively working against your 3C definition. Minerals coat the coil, disrupt the spiral, and block conditioner from reaching the hair shaft. A chelating shampoo used monthly resets this.

→ [3C hair and hard water UK](/3c/3c-hair-hard-water-uk/)

### Wash day

1. **Pre-poo (optional):** Oil or conditioner on dry hair before washing — particularly useful in winter or after a long stretch between washes.
2. **Chelating shampoo** (monthly): Scalp-focused, rinse through length.
3. **Gentle shampoo or co-wash** (other weeks): 3C hair often does well with co-washing between shampoo weeks — the corkscrew benefits from slip.
4. **Deep conditioner:** Apply to soaking-wet hair in sections. 3C tends to need moisture-focused conditioning most of the time; protein treatments are a periodic check-in, not a weekly event.
5. **Detangle during conditioning:** 3C hair tangles less aggressively than type 4 but the corkscrew still wraps — always easier with conditioner in.
6. **Style immediately** while soaking wet.

### Moisturising, sealing, and styling

3C hair sits in a sweet spot where medium-weight products — not as heavy as what 4B or 4C needs, not as light as 3A or 3B — usually work best. Lighter oils (jojoba, sweet almond) for sealing; curl creams and gels for definition.

The defining step matters: how you apply your styler shapes what your coils look like. Scrunching upward encourages natural clumping. Raking through defines but can separate clumps into smaller coils. Try both and see which gives your 3C coils the result you prefer.

## Shrinkage on 3C Hair

3C shrinkage is high but the coils retain their shape — you can still see a curl, just a compressed one. This makes 3C shrinkage feel different from type 4 shrinkage, but it's still significant enough that your length looks shorter than it is.

The frustration specific to 3C: because you can see your coils, you can see your length diminishing in real time as hair dries. 4B or 4C naturals often can't track day-to-day length differences because shrinkage is so total. 3C naturals watch their curls spring up as they dry and it's more viscerally obvious.

## Handling UK Weather

### Outdoor humidity

UK outdoor humidity is high for most of the year. 3C coils exposed to rain or drizzle absorb moisture from the air, swell, and lose definition. The best defences:

- A strong-hold gel or cream applied over leave-in creates a film that slows moisture uptake
- Glycerin-based products react to atmospheric moisture — in high outdoor humidity, moderate glycerin is fine; heavy glycerin can make 3C frizz increase
- A satin bonnet or scarf for covering in rain is a practical choice rather than a style compromise

### Indoor winter dryness

UK central heating creates low-humidity indoor air in winter. This is the opposite problem — moisture leaves the hair rather than entering it. Glycerin-heavy products that work in summer can pull moisture out of 3C coils in dry indoor air.

If your 3C routine works April to October and fails every November, reduce glycerin in your leave-in and styler for the cold months.

## Common 3C Problems

### Definition that falls apart during the day

Usually one of: insufficient hold, product that can't handle UK humidity, or hair touched while drying. Try a stronger-hold gel, avoid touching while drying, and scrunch out the cast only once fully dry.

### Frizz that isn't frizz

3C hair's volume can look like frizz when it's actually just the coils expanding. Check whether the issue is actual frizzy strands (escaped from the curl pattern) or coils that have separated from each other and look larger. Different causes, different fixes.

### Conditioner that seems to do nothing

Hard water. If you're in England and haven't chelated recently, that's where to start.

## Where to Find Products in the UK

3C hair has more mainstream product options than type 4 hair — many brands designed for "curly" or "wavy" hair work reasonably well on 3C, depending on porosity. Boots and Superdrug both carry usable options. For more targeted type 3C products, specialist curly hair shops like Curl Warehouse (UK online) and Afro-Caribbean beauty supply shops in major cities have better ranges. ## FAQs

**Am I 3C or 4A?**

If your coils have a visible, consistent loop when dry without product, you're likely 3C. If your hair compresses into a texture where the curl shape isn't clearly visible when dry, you're likely 4A. Many people have both. The practical difference is small enough that routines overlap — the main adjustment is product weight and hold level.

**Why does my 3C hair look different in summer and winter?**

UK seasonal humidity swings. Summer outdoor humidity keeps coils moisturised and defined. Winter outdoor humidity combined with low indoor humidity (heating) dehydrates the hair and changes how products behave. Adjust product weight and glycerin content seasonally.

**Does 3C hair need protein treatments?**

Occasionally, not regularly. If the hair is feeling mushy, stretchy, or losing its spring when wet, a protein treatment helps. Monthly moisture and a protein check every six to eight weeks is a reasonable baseline.

**Why does my 3C hair frizz the moment I step outside?**

UK outdoor humidity — real. The fix is a strong hold product that acts as a barrier against moisture uptake. Apply gel or cream over leave-in, scrunch, and don't touch until dry. Don't skip the leave-in underneath; the gel on its own without a moisture base leads to crunch.
