The answer most people get is every four to six weeks. For some people it's every eight weeks. For some it's less. What determines it is the rate of new growth, how much the loose root bothers you, and your hair's response to retwisting — not a fixed schedule.

What's certain: retwisting more often than needed causes thinning at the root, not neater locs.

---

## What Retwisting Does

When new hair grows from the scalp, it emerges outside the existing loc — a section of loose, untwisted hair between the scalp and the base of the loc. Retwisting integrates this new growth back into the loc and maintains the neat, separate appearance at the root.

That's the only thing retwisting does. It does not cause the loc itself to loc faster. It does not make the loc thicker or stronger. It is root maintenance.

---

## How Often Is Right for You

The correct frequency is determined by two things:

**1. How fast your hair grows:** faster growth means more loose root accumulates faster. UK average hair growth is approximately 1–1.5cm per month. Two to three centimetres of loose root is typically when most people retwist.

**2. How much loose root you can tolerate:** some people retwist at the first sign of new growth; others are comfortable with significant length at the root. Neither is wrong.

For most people this works out to every four to eight weeks. Athletes and people who sweat heavily at the scalp often retwist more frequently to address sweat-related frizz at the root. People who find retwisting causes scalp sensitivity often stretch to eight to ten weeks.

---

## What Over-Retwisting Looks Like

Retwisting more often than necessary — every one to two weeks, or with excessive tension to create a very neat appearance — causes stress at the root of the loc.

Signs of over-retwisting:
- Thinning at the root where the loc meets the scalp
- A noticeably thinner section at the base compared to the rest of the loc
- Small bumps or folliculitis (inflamed follicles) at retwisted roots
- Hair loss at the root visible after retwisting

The solution is to extend the time between retwists and reduce the tension used. The root needs to rest between sessions. If thinning is already visible, stop all root manipulation and give the loc a complete rest for six to eight weeks.

## How to Retwist at Home

**Timing:** on freshly washed, fully dry locs. Retwisting on damp locs creates frizz as they dry. Retwisting on dirty locs uses product over buildup.

**Products:** a small amount of loc butter, wax, or gel at the root only. Less is better — heavy products accumulate inside the loc over time. Apply to fingers and work through the new growth section only.

**Method:**
1. Separate the locs cleanly — any two locs that have begun to grow together should be gently separated before they fully join
2. Hold the loc with one hand below the root
3. Twist the new growth in the same direction the loc was originally twisted
4. Only twist the new growth — don't twist the full loc length
5. Roll the root between thumb and forefinger until it sits neatly

**After retwisting:** if you use heat to set the retwist, clip the root lightly or roll onto small rods while drying. Remove clips before the locs are completely dry to avoid indentation marks.

---

## Retwisting at a Loctician vs at Home

Many people start with loctician retwists and transition to home maintenance once the locs are established and they're confident in the technique. The transition is usually possible by months six to twelve.

What a loctician adds: assessment of whether locs are merging, detection of thinning or weak spots before they become problems, and consistent tension across the full head. For the first year, having a professional check in occasionally is worth the cost even if you're doing most maintenance at home.

## FAQs

**My locs are new (two months old). How often should I retwist?**

Every six to eight weeks in the starter loc stage. In the early stages, retwisting is about maintaining the sections and integrating loose hair — less often is better. The locs need to be allowed to mat without constant manipulation.

**I retwisted and now the root looks thin. What do I do?**

Stop retwisting that loc for at least six to eight weeks. Apply no tension to the root. If the thinning is significant, leave the loc completely alone for longer and see if the root recovers. Persistent thinning after a rest period — see a trichologist.

**The locs at my hairline are always unravelling after I retwist them. Is this normal?**

Hairline locs are finer and shorter — they're more prone to unravelling throughout the first year and a half. Light retwisting with minimal product is the approach. Avoid retwisting the hairline more often to compensate — it worsens thinning. Some locticians use interlocking on the hairline specifically to give it more hold.

**I can only afford to go to a loctician every three months. Is that okay?**

Yes, and many people do exactly this. You can maintain the loose root between loctician appointments by very gently hand-rolling the new growth without product, which gives a neater appearance without adding stress. The loctician appointment then addresses anything that's needed more precisely.
