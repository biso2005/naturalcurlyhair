Birmingham has the second-largest concentration of Afro hair salons in the UK after London, reflecting a large and long-established Caribbean and West African community. Lozells, Handsworth, Aston, Erdington, and Moseley have historically had the highest concentration of Afro hair specialists, though salons are distributed across the city.

**Birmingham water:** moderately hard to hard. Severn Trent data shows Birmingham water at the harder end of the Midlands range, though significantly softer than London. A chelating shampoo quarterly to bi-monthly is worthwhile for natural hair in Birmingham.

## How to Find a Birmingham Afro Hair Salon

**Instagram.** Search "#BirminghamNaturalHair", "#BrumBraids", "#AfroHairBirmingham" plus the style you want. Birmingham has a substantial natural hair community on Instagram; area and neighbourhood tags surface both salons and mobile stylists across the West Midlands.

**Facebook groups.** Birmingham-based natural hair and Black community Facebook groups are active and well-populated. A recommendation from a group member whose hair you can see is the most reliable route to a good stylist.

**Google Maps.** "Afro hair salon Birmingham" or "natural hair Birmingham" returns a range of results. Read reviews for mentions of natural hair types specifically — a salon with strong reviews for relaxed hair may not have equivalent experience with natural type 4 styles.

**Lozells and Handsworth.** These areas have the highest density of Afro hair businesses in Birmingham, including both salons and Black-owned beauty supply stores (BSS) where you can ask for recommendations from other customers.
