The best shampoo for natural hair in the UK depends on what the hair needs: a regular cleanse, a hard water chelating wash, or a gentler co-wash between wash days. Different jobs need different products. Here are the picks for each.

---

## Best Clarifying Shampoo

**The pick: As I Am Curl Clarity Shampoo** — sulphate-free but genuinely cleansing. Removes product buildup without stripping the hair of all moisture, which most sulphate-containing clarifying shampoos do. Available at Boots and Superdrug. Around £10 for 237ml.

**Runner-up: Design Essentials Natural Honey & Shea Edge Tamer Shampoo** — slightly heavier cleanse. Better for hair that uses a lot of oil-based products and needs a more thorough reset. Available at BSS and online via Sally Beauty UK.

**Skip: Pantene Gold Series Shampoo** — formulated for natural hair but performs more like a general moisturising shampoo than a clarifying wash. If you need a real cleanse, this doesn't deliver it.

---

## Best Chelating Shampoo

A chelating shampoo removes mineral deposits from hard water — calcium and magnesium that build up on the hair shaft over months and cause brittleness, reduced moisture uptake, and a rough hair surface. A regular clarifying shampoo removes product buildup; a chelating shampoo removes minerals. These are different things, and in most of England, both are needed.

**The pick: Malibu C Hard Water Wellness Shampoo** — the most widely available chelating shampoo in the UK, with EDTA as the chelating agent. Effective at mineral removal in one use. Available at LookFantastic and Amazon. Around £15 for 266ml.

**Runner-up: Joico Clarifying Shampoo** — dual-function: chelates and clarifies in a single wash. Useful if you want to run one step rather than two. Available at LookFantastic and Superdrug salon range. Around £12 for 300ml.

**Skip: Any shampoo labelled "detoxifying" without EDTA or phytic acid in the ingredients.** "Detoxifying" is not a chelating claim. Check the ingredients — if there's no EDTA, citric acid in meaningful concentration, or phytic acid, it won't remove mineral deposits.

Use a chelating shampoo monthly in hard water areas (London, South East, East Anglia, East Midlands). Follow immediately with a deep conditioner — chelating removes minerals but also temporarily reduces moisture.

## Best Sulphate-Free Moisturising Shampoo

For the standard weekly or fortnightly wash that isn't a clarifying reset — just cleansing without the strip.

**The pick: SheaMoisture Manuka Honey & Mafura Oil Intensive Hydration Shampoo** — genuinely moisturising without leaving the hair coated or weighed down. Works on type 3 and 4 hair. Available at Boots, Superdrug, and Sainsbury's. Around £12 for 384ml.

**Runner-up: Camille Rose Sweet Ginger Cleansing Rinse** — lighter cleanse, works better for fine-stranded natural hair or loose curl types that find the SheaMoisture heavy. Available at Boots and online. Around £15 for 266ml.

**Skip: OGX Coconut Milk Shampoo** — widely available and heavily marketed for curly hair but contains sulphates and builds up quickly on coily hair types. Fine for looser curls, underwhelming for type 4.

## Best Co-Wash

A co-washing conditioner cleanses without shampoo — useful for refreshing between wash days or for very dry hair types that can't tolerate even a sulphate-free shampoo weekly. Not a substitute for a clarifying wash monthly.

**The pick: As I Am Coconut CoWash** — the original and still the benchmark. Rinses cleanly, doesn't leave the hair limp, and works on type 3 and 4 hair. Available at Boots and Superdrug. Around £9 for 454g.

**Runner-up: Cantu Cleansing Cream Conditioner** — slightly heavier, works better for very dry type 4 hair. Available at Boots, Superdrug, and most supermarkets. Around £7 for 400ml.

**Skip: Using a standard rinse-out conditioner as a co-wash.** Some conditioners work as co-washes in a pinch, but standard rinse-out conditioners aren't formulated for the scalp and tend to leave buildup there. A dedicated co-wash product is worth the distinction.

## How to Choose

**Hard water area (London, SE, East Anglia, East Midlands):**
Monthly chelating wash (Malibu C Hard Water) + fortnightly sulphate-free moisturising wash (SheaMoisture Manuka Honey) is the core rotation. Co-wash in between if needed.

**Soft water area (Wales, Scotland, NW England):**
Fortnightly sulphate-free wash is usually sufficient. A clarifying wash quarterly to remove product buildup.

**Low porosity hair:** co-washes can build up on low porosity hair faster than other types. Clarify more frequently — monthly rather than quarterly — even in soft water areas.

**High porosity hair:** needs the most moisture protection post-wash. Follow every shampoo with a deep conditioner before the leave-in.

## FAQs

**Can I use a normal supermarket shampoo on natural hair?**

Most supermarket shampoos contain sulphates (sodium lauryl sulphate or sodium laureth sulphate) that are too stripping for regular use on type 4 hair. Occasional use — as a quarterly clarifying wash — is fine. Weekly use on coily hair will strip moisture faster than it can be replaced.

**How often should I shampoo natural hair?**

Most type 4 hair does best with a full wash every one to two weeks, with co-washing in between if needed. Less frequent than that and product and scalp buildup accumulate. More frequent and the hair doesn't have time to retain moisture between washes.

**My shampoo leaves my hair feeling rough and stripped. What's happening?**

Either the shampoo has sulphates and is too stripping, or you're not deep conditioning immediately after washing, or both. Switch to a sulphate-free option and always deep condition post-shampoo — the shampoo and conditioner work as a system, not independently.

**Is there a UK dupe for the popular US chelating shampoos?**

Malibu C (widely available on Amazon UK and LookFantastic) and Joico Clarifying are both US-origin brands that are well-distributed in the UK. There isn't a UK-native chelating shampoo that matches them yet at this quality level.
