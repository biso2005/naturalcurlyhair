Porosity matters for 2A hair because 2A is the pattern most sensitive to product weight, and porosity determines what weight is actually appropriate. Low-porosity 2A and wrong-heavy products is the most common combination that makes 2A waves completely disappear. High-porosity 2A with too-light products means the wave forms and fades within hours.

## What Porosity Means for 2A Hair

**Low porosity 2A:**
- Hair resists wetting — takes a long time to fully saturate
- Products sit on the surface rather than absorbing
- Buildup accumulates quickly; the hair feels coated within a few washes
- Heat makes a noticeable difference during conditioning
- The wave can look flat because products are sitting on top, not in the hair

**High porosity 2A:**
- Hair wets almost instantly
- Products absorb but the wave still fades quickly — moisture in, moisture out
- Ends consistently feel dry
- More common after bleaching, chemical processing, or heat styling
- In UK winter, high-porosity 2A loses moisture to dry indoor air faster than any other type

**Medium porosity 2A:** Most common. The widest range of lightweight 2A-appropriate products works. Technique and drying conditions matter more than precise product formulation.

## Testing Porosity on 2A Hair

### Float test: skip it

2A waves trap air in the S-bend, which keeps the strand afloat regardless of cuticle state. The test gives a false low-porosity reading for almost all 2A hair.

### Spray bottle test

Spritz a small section of clean, dry 2A hair. Watch for 30 seconds:

- Water **beads and sits on the surface** — low porosity
- Water **absorbs quickly**, section darkens immediately — high porosity
- Water **absorbs slowly** — medium porosity

Run this after a chelating or clarifying wash on clean hair. Mineral deposits from hard water create a false low-porosity reading — the mineral layer absorbs the water first.

### Feel test

Run a single strand between fingers from tip to root:

- **Smooth, almost nothing to feel** — low porosity
- **Slightly rough or catches slightly** — high porosity
- **Very rough, crinkles, or breaks easily** — high porosity with damage

## Mixed Porosity: Common on 2A

Roots have newer growth and lower porosity; ends have higher porosity from years of handling and exposure. For 2A this often doesn't require much adjustment — the routine already focuses products on the mid-length and ends, which is where higher porosity tends to be.

## What to Do Based on Your Result

### Low porosity 2A

The challenge: getting moisture past the cuticle without adding weight.

- **Heat during deep conditioning.** A warm damp towel around a shower cap for 15 minutes. Without heat, conditioner on low-porosity 2A sits on the surface and adds weight without absorbing.
- **Mist leave-in only.** Not liquid, not cream. The absolute minimum to slightly open the cuticle and add slip.
- **Apply to soaking-wet hair.** The brief window when the cuticle is slightly more receptive from the water.
- **Clarify monthly.** Low-porosity hair accumulates buildup faster — products pile on the surface. Regular clarifying keeps the wave free to form.
- **Skip oil.** Already the rule for 2A, but especially for low porosity — it sits on top and contributes zero moisture while adding weight.

### High porosity 2A

The challenge: the wave forms but the moisture leaves quickly in UK conditions.

- **Seal while hair is wet.** One drop of lightweight oil at the ends while the hair is still wet, immediately after leave-in. This slows moisture loss from the ends during drying.
- **Mousse with some hold.** High-porosity 2A may need a slightly stronger flexible hold than medium porosity — enough that the wave coat lasts through the afternoon rather than fading by noon.
- **Diffuse in winter.** High-porosity 2A loses moisture to dry indoor air faster than any other wave type. Diffusing sets the wave before the centrally-heated air takes the moisture.
- **Protein when needed.** High-porosity 2A that's been colour-treated or heat-styled: a protein treatment every six to eight weeks when the hair feels mushy or stretches excessively when wet. Not on a schedule.
- **Satin bonnet every night.** High-porosity hair loses moisture through cotton faster.

### Medium porosity 2A

Most lightweight 2A products work. Focus on the seasonal adjustment (low-glycerin from October to March) and drying technique. Technique and environment are the main variables.

## Hard Water Complicates Porosity Testing

In hard water areas, minerals on the 2A hair shaft make the spray bottle test read incorrectly — the mineral layer absorbs first, mimicking low porosity. Always chelate before testing.

## FAQs

**My 2A wave looks fine right after washing but fades within a couple of hours. Porosity?**

Could be high porosity, could be drying environment, could be both. Check: are you air drying in a centrally-heated room in winter? If yes, try diffusing. If the wave still fades quickly even with diffusing, high porosity is a more likely factor — try sealing with one drop of oil at the ends after leave-in.

**I've been told to deep condition weekly for 2A. Is that right?**

Rarely necessary for healthy 2A hair. Over-conditioning 2A adds weight and can reduce wave definition. Condition the ends with rinse-out conditioner every wash, deep condition every four to six weeks or when the hair specifically feels rough or dry.

**Does porosity affect how often I need to chelate?**

Low-porosity 2A benefits from chelating monthly — mineral buildup accumulates faster on a flat cuticle. High-porosity 2A can chelate monthly too; it's less likely to feel stripped after, but always deep condition with heat immediately after regardless.
