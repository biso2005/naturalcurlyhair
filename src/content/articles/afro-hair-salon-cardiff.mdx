Cardiff has one of the oldest established Black communities in Wales, historically centred in Butetown (Tiger Bay), and an Afro hair salon community that reflects that history. The city has a proportionate range of options relative to its size, though the salon desert problem is acute in the rest of Wales outside Cardiff and Newport.

**Cardiff water:** soft to moderately soft. Dŵr Cymru (Welsh Water) serves Cardiff with water from upland Welsh reservoirs, which are naturally very soft. Mineral buildup is not a significant concern for natural hair in Cardiff. A quarterly clarifying shampoo is sufficient routine maintenance.

---

## How to Find a Cardiff Afro Hair Salon

**Instagram.** Search "#CardiffNaturalHair", "#CardiffBraids", "#AfroHairCardiff", "#WalesNaturalHair". Cardiff's natural hair stylist community is active on Instagram; some stylists serve both Cardiff and Newport.

**Facebook groups.** Cardiff Black community groups and Wales natural hair groups. Butetown community networks are a particularly useful route given the area's history as the centre of Cardiff's Black community.

**Google Maps.** "Afro hair salon Cardiff" or "natural hair Cardiff". Read reviews for natural hair mentions specifically.

**Butetown and Grangetown.** These areas have the highest concentration of Afro hair businesses in Cardiff.

---

## Outside Cardiff

Newport has some Afro hair provision. Swansea, Wrexham, and other Welsh towns outside Cardiff have very limited specialist options — mobile stylists and travel to Cardiff are the practical alternatives.
