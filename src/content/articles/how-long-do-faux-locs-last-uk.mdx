Faux locs in the UK last six to eight weeks for most people. The style doesn't end on a specific day — it deteriorates at the attachment point first, then at the loc exterior, then at the base as new growth increases. Understanding the pattern tells you when to plan removal.

---

## Week by Week

### Weeks 1–2: Optimal

New installation. The style looks its best. Some people experience tension at the attachment points in the first 48 hours, particularly at the nape with longer locs.

**UK factor:** UK autumn and winter outdoor damp may create slight frizzing at the loc exterior surface even at this stage. This is normal and doesn't indicate anything is wrong with the installation.

### Weeks 3–4: Peak period

The attachment points have settled. The style moves comfortably. The loc exterior still looks defined and clean. This is when faux locs photograph best and are most wearable.

**UK factor:** if it's winter, central heating indoors means the locs may feel slightly drier than they would in a non-heated environment. Light scalp oil every two to three days keeps the scalp comfortable; a very light oil over the loc exterior adds sheen.

### Weeks 5–6: Visible new growth

The natural hair at the base of each attachment point is growing out. The space between the scalp and the start of the faux loc becomes visible. With wrapped faux locs, the wrapped base may start to loosen at some attachment points.

The loc exterior on wrapped locs still looks good. Crochet faux locs typically show more distress at the attachment point by week five.

**UK factor:** if it's autumn or spring — higher outdoor humidity — the loc exterior may be frizzing slightly more than in summer or winter. An ACV rinse after scalp washing helps maintain the exterior.

### Weeks 7–8: End of style life

Most styles reach their natural end point here. The new growth at the root is significant. The scalp has accumulated enough sebum and buildup that washing is harder to do effectively. Some attachment points are loosening or the wrapping has distorted.

Still wearable, but the style is working against you rather than with you at this point.

**UK factor:** if you've been washing with hard water throughout without chelating, the loc exterior is noticeably duller and rougher than it was at installation. This is mineral accumulation on the extension hair.

### Beyond week 8

Not recommended for most people. The natural hair at the attachment base begins to mat with the new growth. Removal becomes significantly harder and more damaging.

## What Shortens Faux Locs Duration

**Cotton pillowcase:** frizzes the loc exterior and distorts the wrapping at the surface faster than any other single factor.

**Frequent washing without thorough drying:** water trapped in the loc base causes mildew. Not drying fully means the attachment point stays damp, which softens the wrap and causes it to loosen faster.

**Hard water without chelating:** mineral buildup on the extension hair makes the exterior rough and dull by weeks four to five. Prevention: monthly ACV rinse, or a chelating shampoo wash.

**Humidity in UK autumn and spring:** outdoor humidity frizzes the loc exterior. An anti-humidity product at installation and periodically throughout helps.

**Too much product on the loc exterior:** accumulates, makes the loc stiff or sticky, distorts the texture.

---

## What Extends Faux Locs Duration

- Satin bonnet or pillowcase every night
- Scalp moisture every two to three days
- Scalp washing every two weeks and drying fully
- ACV rinse periodically on the loc exterior
- Not applying products to the loc length (just the scalp)

---

## Crochet vs Wrapped: Lifespan Difference

Crochet faux locs attach at a single point rather than wrapping — the attachment can loosen faster. In practice, crochet faux locs typically look their best for four to six weeks and begin showing wear at the attachment point earlier than wrapped locs.

Wrapped faux locs — where the extension hair is wrapped continuously around the natural hair — hold at the attachment point longer and typically maintain their look until week six to eight.

## FAQs

**My faux locs look perfect but the scalp is unbearably itchy at week five. Do I have to take them down?**

Try a thorough scalp wash first — diluted shampoo, very thorough rinse, full dry. If that resolves the itching for several days, the style can continue. If the itching returns within a day or two despite washing, the scalp buildup is beyond what in-style washing can address and the faux locs need to come down.

**The extension hair at the attachment point is separating from the natural hair. Is that a sign of damage?**

It may be — if the natural hair is pulling away from the attachment rather than growing past it, the attachment was too tight or the natural hair at the base is weakening. Don't add tension to those specific locs. If the natural hair at the base looks significantly thinner than it did at installation, take those locs down sooner.

**It's been eight weeks and mine still look great. Can I leave them in?**

If the scalp is healthy, the attachment points are secure, and there's no significant matting at the base, you can extend by a week or two while maintaining the same scalp routine. The risk increases steadily beyond eight weeks regardless of how the exterior looks.

**Can I get a salon refresh rather than full removal?**

Some salons offer attachment-point refreshes — removing and re-attaching specific locs that have loosened while leaving the rest in. Ask when booking the original appointment whether this is something the stylist offers. It can extend the style by two to three weeks without full removal.
