**K18 for most natural hair with chemical or heat damage — the leave-in format and four-minute treatment time fit a coily hair routine more naturally than Olaplex's rinse-out protocol. Olaplex No. 0 + No. 3 for severe bond damage, particularly from bleaching.**

If you're choosing one, start with K18. If you've tried K18 and want more intensive treatment, add Olaplex.

---

## What They Have in Common

Both address disulphide bond damage — the structural protein bonds broken by bleaching, chemical processing, or sustained high heat. Neither is a conditioner or moisture treatment. Neither is appropriate for unprocessed natural hair with no chemical or heat damage history — there's nothing to repair.

Both are available at Boots. Both are more expensive than standard protein treatments but address a different (deeper) level of damage.

---

## Where They Differ

| | K18 | Olaplex No. 3 |
|--|-----|--------------|
| Format | Leave-in (no rinse) | Rinse-out |
| Treatment time | 4 minutes | 30 min–overnight |
| Application | Over clean damp hair, then style normally | After shampoo, before conditioner, rinse |
| Surface effect | Smoothing and slip | Less surface effect |
| Intensity | Moderate | High (especially with No. 0) |
| Price (Boots) | ~£25 for 50ml | ~£28 for 100ml |
| Best for | Moderate damage, coily hair routine | Severe damage, bleached hair |

---

## Why K18 Works Better in a Coily Hair Routine

The leave-in format removes the rinse step. In a coily hair routine, every additional step with water or rinse is another manipulation event on fragile wet hair. K18 applied for four minutes and left in allows the peptide to work while the routine continues — style over the top as normal.

Olaplex No. 3 requires:
1. Applying to clean damp hair
2. Waiting thirty minutes to overnight
3. Rinsing out
4. Continuing with conditioner and styling

On straight hair this is straightforward. On very coily 4C hair, a thirty-minute wait followed by a rinse and restart adds significant time and manipulation to wash day.

---

## When Olaplex Is the Right Choice

**Severe bleach damage.** Full bleach on natural hair (going significantly lighter, highlights, or fashion colour on type 4) breaks disulphide bonds extensively. Olaplex No. 0 + No. 3 protocol — more intensive than K18 alone — is the stronger tool for this level of damage.

**As part of a salon colour treatment.** Olaplex No. 1 and No. 2 are salon products applied during the colour process. If you're getting bleach or significant colour at a salon, asking for Olaplex in-service is the right call and most natural hair salons offer it.

**When K18 hasn't been sufficient.** If four weeks of monthly K18 hasn't improved the hair's elasticity and strength, Olaplex's more intensive protocol may be needed.

---

## Can You Use Both?

Yes. The community-established combined protocol:
1. Olaplex No. 0 applied to damp hair, ten minutes
2. Olaplex No. 3 applied over No. 0, thirty minutes to overnight
3. Shampoo out
4. K18 applied to damp clean hair, four minutes, no rinse
5. Style normally

This is for hair with significant damage — not a routine monthly treatment. Monthly for severely damaged hair, then step down to one or the other as the hair improves.

## FAQs

**Which is better value for money?**

K18 at £25 for 50ml sounds expensive, but you use a pea-to-dime amount per session. At monthly use, 50ml lasts three to six months — roughly £4–8 per treatment. Olaplex No. 3 at £28 for 100ml is similar cost-per-treatment at comparable frequency.

**I have unprocessed 4C hair. Should I try either?**

No. Neither product addresses a problem unprocessed 4C hair has. A protein treatment (Aphogee Two-Minute) and a good deep conditioner (SheaMoisture Manuka Honey Masque) cost less and are the right tools for maintaining strong, healthy unprocessed 4C.

**Can these products be used on locs?**

K18 can be applied to locs — work through each loc and leave in without rinsing. Olaplex can be applied to locs but requires a thorough rinse, which is harder to achieve throughout mature locs. K18 is the more practical choice for locs.

**The salon wants to use Olaplex during my bleach session. Is it worth the add-on cost?**

Yes, for significant lightening on natural hair. Olaplex No. 1 and No. 2 applied during and after bleaching reduce the structural damage that would otherwise accumulate in the hair shaft. The in-salon add-on is more effective than the at-home No. 3 used after the fact — the damage is being addressed as it occurs rather than after.
