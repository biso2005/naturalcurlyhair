Newcastle upon Tyne and the wider North East of England have a smaller Afro hair salon offering than northern cities like Leeds or Manchester, reflecting a proportionally smaller Black British community. The provision is real but limited — mobile stylists are an important part of the picture here, and some residents travel to Leeds or Manchester for major services.

**Newcastle water:** moderately soft. Northumbrian Water serves Newcastle with water from upland Pennine and Northumbrian sources, which is naturally softer than Midlands or South East water. Mineral buildup is less of a concern than in harder-water areas, though a quarterly clarifying shampoo is still good practice.

---

## How to Find a Newcastle Afro Hair Salon

**Instagram.** Search "#NewcastleNaturalHair", "#NewcastleBraids", "#AfroHairNewcastle", "#NorthEastNaturalHair". Mobile stylists in the North East are well-represented on Instagram and often cover Sunderland, Durham, and Middlesbrough as well as Newcastle.

**Facebook groups.** Newcastle and North East Black community groups. In a smaller market, Facebook recommendations are particularly valuable.

**Google Maps.** "Afro hair salon Newcastle" or "natural hair Newcastle". The results set is smaller than in most of this guide's cities; read reviews carefully.

**Elswick and Benwell.** These areas have historically had the highest concentration of Afro hair businesses in Newcastle.

## Sunderland, Durham, and Teesside

Sunderland and Durham have very limited specialist provision. Middlesbrough has a slightly larger offering. For the wider North East, Newcastle and mobile stylists covering the region are the practical options.
