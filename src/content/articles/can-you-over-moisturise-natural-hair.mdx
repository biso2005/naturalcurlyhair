Yes, you can over-moisturise natural hair, and the result is called hygral fatigue — wait, no it isn't. That term is disputed and oversimplified. The accurate description: too much moisture without enough protein leaves hair feeling mushy, stretchy, and limp, because the hair strand has absorbed so much water it's lost structural integrity.

The fix is protein. Not a permanent fix to your whole routine — one protein treatment, then reassess.

## What over-moisturised hair feels like

- Strands stretch a long way before snapping, then break without much resistance
- Hair feels soft but limp rather than defined
- Curls fall flat quickly after washing
- Products absorb instantly but don't seem to do anything
- Hair feels mushy when wet

If your hair snaps immediately with almost no stretch, that's a different problem — under-moisturised or protein-overloaded hair.

## Why it happens

Most natural hair routines are moisture-forward by design: deep conditioners, leave-ins, creams, oils. That's correct for most people most of the time. But if you're adding moisture every wash day without any protein, the balance tips.

It can also happen seasonally. Summer heat and humidity push more moisture into the hair shaft continuously, so your routine may work fine in February and produce over-moisturised results by July.

## How to fix it

**Step 1: Do one protein treatment.** A light protein (like a rice water rinse or a conditioner with hydrolysed wheat protein) is enough to start. You don't need a hard protein treatment unless your hair is severely damaged.

**Step 2: Skip the deep conditioner on this wash.** Cleanse, protein treat, use a light leave-in, seal. No moisture layering.

**Step 3: Reassess next wash.** If your curls look more defined and the mushiness is gone, you were over-moisturised. If the snapping got worse, you overcorrected with protein.

## Preventing it

You don't need to count protein treatments or follow a fixed schedule. Pay attention to how your hair feels when wet:
- Lots of stretch → needs protein
- No stretch, snaps immediately → needs moisture
- Some stretch, then snaps cleanly → balanced

Most people need protein roughly once a month, more often in summer or after chemical services.

## A note on deep conditioning frequency

Deep conditioning two or three times a week is too frequent for most people. Once a week or once a fortnight is enough. If you're deep conditioning every wash and your hair feels mushy, reducing frequency is the first thing to try before adding products.
