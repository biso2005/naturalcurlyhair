**Co-wash is not a shampoo substitute. Shampoo is not too harsh to use on natural hair if it's the right shampoo.** Both have a job; neither does the other's job.

Co-wash: refreshes moisture and removes light surface buildup between wash days. Appropriate every two to five days for type 4 hair that needs frequent moisture without a full wash.

Shampoo: removes product buildup, sebum, and (with a chelating or clarifying formula) mineral deposits. Necessary at minimum fortnightly. A co-wash-only routine builds up on the scalp and hair over weeks.

---

## When Co-Wash Wins

**Between full wash days on type 4 hair:** type 4 coily hair needs moisture more frequently than it needs a full cleanse. A co-wash refreshes moisture and adds slip without stripping — so the hair stays moisturised through the week rather than going dry between washes.

**On high porosity hair:** high porosity hair loses moisture fast. Frequent gentle co-washing replaces moisture without the stripping effect of a sulphate-free shampoo used too often.

**For children's natural hair mid-week:** rinsing a child's hair with a co-wash mid-week after PE or outdoor play is faster and gentler than a full wash.

**When the hair is in a protective style:** rinsing the scalp with a co-wash every one to two weeks while in braids or twists keeps the scalp clean without disturbing the style.

---

## When Shampoo Wins

**Weekly or fortnightly full wash:** co-wash does not remove accumulated product buildup effectively. After two to three co-washes without a shampoo, the hair surface accumulates enough residue to reduce moisture uptake. A shampoo wash clears the slate.

**For the scalp:** the scalp produces sebum regardless of hair type. A co-wash does not adequately cleanse the scalp — it rinses rather than cleanses. Scalp health requires regular shampoo use.

**Monthly clarifying:** to remove heavy product buildup, a sulphate-free clarifying shampoo (As I Am Curl Clarity, Cantu Clarifying Shampoo) is needed. A co-wash cannot do this.

**Monthly chelating (hard water areas):** to remove mineral deposits from London, SE, East Anglia, East Midlands water, a chelating shampoo with EDTA (Malibu C Hard Water) is necessary. A co-wash cannot remove mineral deposits.

**For low porosity hair:** low porosity hair's flat cuticle makes buildup more likely. Co-washing as the primary cleansing method accelerates buildup on low porosity hair faster than on other hair types. Shampoo more frequently; co-wash less.

## The Practical Rotation

**Type 4 hair (most common):**
- Fortnightly: sulphate-free moisturising shampoo + deep conditioner
- Between washes: co-wash as needed (every 3–5 days)
- Monthly: clarifying shampoo replaces the fortnightly wash
- Monthly (hard water areas): chelating shampoo replaces or precedes the monthly clarifying wash

**Type 3 hair:**
- Weekly: sulphate-free shampoo
- Between washes: optional co-wash or rinse-only if hair needs refreshing
- Monthly: clarifying shampoo

**Low porosity hair:**
- Weekly or fortnightly: shampoo (shampoo more frequently than average)
- Co-wash: minimal or none — buildup risk is higher

## FAQs

**My hair is dry after shampooing. Does that mean I should switch to co-wash only?**

No — it means either the shampoo is too stripping (switch to a sulphate-free formula) or you're not deep conditioning after washing. Shampoo followed by deep conditioning should not leave the hair dry. A co-wash-only routine deferred this dryness but didn't fix it.

**How do I know if I have co-wash buildup?**

The hair feels coated or limp despite regular co-washing. Moisture products feel like they're sitting on top rather than absorbing. Definition gradually declines. One clarifying shampoo wash typically resolves it immediately — if the hair feels dramatically better after one shampoo, co-wash buildup was the issue.

**Can I use conditioner as a co-wash?**

Some conditioners work as a co-wash in a pinch. A dedicated co-washing product (As I Am Coconut CoWash, Cantu Complete Conditioning Co-Wash) is formulated for the scalp as well as the hair and rinses more cleanly than a standard rinse-out conditioner used as a co-wash.

**I co-wash daily. Is that too often?**

Daily co-washing is excessive for most natural hair — it can cause its own buildup, waterlogging on high porosity hair, and scalp issues from constant water exposure without adequate drying time. Every three to five days is the most frequent that most type 4 hair benefits from. If the hair needs daily refreshing, a spray bottle of water and leave-in is less intensive than a full co-wash.
