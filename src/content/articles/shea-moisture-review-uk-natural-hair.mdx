SheaMoisture is the most visible natural hair brand in the UK market — available at Boots, Superdrug, Sainsbury's, and increasingly in mainstream chemists. The brand's reputation was built on genuinely strong formulations from the original Sofi Tucker Shea Butter range. Some of those formulations are still excellent. Others have been diluted, reformulated, or extended into product lines that ride the brand name rather than earning it.

Here's the verdict by product.

---

## Worth Buying

**SheaMoisture Manuka Honey & Yogurt Hydrate + Repair Hair Masque** — the best product in the range and one of the best deep conditioners available in UK retail at any price point. Thick, genuinely penetrating, and the mild protein from the yogurt makes it suitable for most type 4 hair regardless of protein sensitivity. Fifteen to twenty minutes under a shower cap produces a noticeable result. Available at Boots and Superdrug. Around £12 for 326g.

**SheaMoisture Jamaican Black Castor Oil Strengthen & Restore Leave-In Conditioner** — despite the JBCO marketing (the oil itself isn't doing growth work), the formulation underneath is strong. Good slip, good moisture for 4B and 4C hair, works as a detangling base. The JBCO adds weight that helps with heavier curl types. Around £10 for 325ml.

**SheaMoisture Coconut & Hibiscus Curl Enhancing Smoothie** — the original cult product and still a strong curl cream for 3B–4A hair. The classic formulation (check the batch; some users report inconsistency) is reliably good for definition and moisture. Around £12 for 326g.

**SheaMoisture 100% Virgin Coconut Oil Daily Hydration Detangling Milk** — a lighter leave-in than the JBCO range. Better for fine-stranded type 4 or type 3 hair that finds the heavier SheaMoisture leave-ins weighty. Around £11 for 237ml.

## Skip These

**SheaMoisture Jamaican Black Castor Oil Strengthen, Grow & Restore Shampoo** — the JBCO addition doesn't change the cleansing performance. The shampoo is fine but not differentiated from other sulphate-free options. The "grow" claim is not supported by evidence. → [Castor oil and hair growth: what the evidence actually says](/products/castor-oil-hair-growth-evidence-uk/)

**SheaMoisture African Black Soap Balancing Shampoo (for some users)** — effective at removing buildup and helping with dandruff, but too drying for regular use on very dry type 4 hair. Appropriate monthly for oily scalp or seborrhoeic dermatitis; not appropriate as a weekly shampoo for moisture-depleted hair.

**SheaMoisture Curl & Shine Coconut & Hibiscus Conditioner** — the reformulated version underperforms the masque and the original formula. At the current formulation, it's a rinse-out conditioner that doesn't provide enough slip or conditioning to be worth the price versus the masque. Use the masque for deep conditioning; skip this for a routine rinse-out.

**SheaMoisture Jamaican Black Castor Oil Seal & Protect Hair & Scalp Oil** — not a useful product when a bottle of straight jojoba or argan oil achieves the same sealant function for the same or lower price without the JBCO marketing premium.

## The Glycerin Note

SheaMoisture products are generally higher in glycerin than comparable Cantu products. Glycerin appears near the top of the ingredient list across most of the styling range. This makes SheaMoisture products work well in UK summer conditions (high ambient humidity) but potentially problematic in winter when indoor air is dry. → [Glycerin in natural hair products: summer vs winter UK](/products/glycerin-natural-hair-winter-summer-uk/)

If you use SheaMoisture styling products in winter and find the hair frizzing or drying out faster than expected, glycerin is a likely cause. Either rotate to lower-glycerin products in winter or seal with oil over the SheaMoisture product before going outside.

---

## The Reformulation Issue

SheaMoisture was acquired by Unilever in 2017. Since then, some formulations have been adjusted. The most common community complaints are about the Coconut & Hibiscus Curl Smoothie (current formula thinner than original) and some of the leave-in conditioners. The masque and the JBCO leave-in have remained more consistent.

If a product you've used before and liked seems to be performing differently, a formulation change is possible. The ingredient list is the best check — compare with a batch you have from a year or two ago if possible.

## FAQs

**Is SheaMoisture worth the price?**

The masque is worth full retail price. The leave-in conditioners are competitive at their price point. The styling products are more variable — the Coconut & Hibiscus Smoothie is good, some of the newer range extensions less so. Look out for Boots offers on SheaMoisture — 3-for-2 on hair products runs regularly and brings the masque to a very good price per gram.

**Is SheaMoisture good for type 4C hair?**

The JBCO leave-in and the masque are the strongest options for 4C in the range. The styling smoothie is better suited to 4A and 4B. For very dense 4C that needs maximum moisture, the masque plus Cantu Leave-In as a daily leave-in is a better combination than SheaMoisture across the board.

**Has SheaMoisture changed since the Unilever acquisition?**

Formulation changes have happened in some products. The community consensus is that changes were most noticeable in the Curl & Shine range and less so in the JBCO and Manuka Honey ranges. The masque is consistently the most praised product in the current range.

**Is SheaMoisture cruelty-free?**

SheaMoisture is certified cruelty-free by Leaping Bunny. Unilever as a parent company is not universally certified, but the SheaMoisture brand itself maintains its cruelty-free certification.
