Porosity matters more visibly on 2B than on most other patterns because 2B already uses the lightest products of any textured hair type. The wrong porosity/product combination on 2B shows immediately: too-heavy products for low-porosity 2B flatten the wave entirely; too-light products for high-porosity 2B mean the moisture evaporates before the wave has set.

## What Porosity Means for 2B Hair

**Low porosity 2B:**
- Hair takes a long time to fully wet under running water
- Products sit on the surface rather than absorbing — hair can feel coated
- Buildup appears within a few washes
- Heat during deep conditioning makes a significant difference
- Hair feels moisturised right after washing then surprisingly dry within hours
- The flat S-wave can look "weighed down" even with the lightest products if they're not absorbing

**High porosity 2B:**
- Hair wets almost instantly
- Products absorb quickly but moisture doesn't stay — dry and frizzy by afternoon
- Ends consistently rough or dry
- More common after chemical treatments, bleaching, or heat styling
- Open cuticle catches dry indoor air unevenly — accelerates wave loss in UK winter

**Medium porosity 2B:** Most common. The broadest range of lightweight products works. The routine is more about technique and drying conditions than precise product formulation.

## Testing Porosity on 2B Hair

### The float test: skip it

2B waves trap air in the S-bend. That air keeps the strand floating even if the cuticle is fully open. The test is unreliable for any wave type; skip it.

### Spray bottle test

Spritz a small section of clean, dry 2B hair. Watch for 30 seconds:

- Water **beads and takes time to absorb** — low porosity
- Water **absorbs quickly**, section visibly darkens — high porosity
- Water **absorbs slowly** — medium porosity

Run this on clean hair after a clarifying or chelating wash. Mineral deposits in hard water areas mimic low porosity on 2B waves — the mineral layer absorbs the water before the hair does.

### Feel test

Run a clean, dry strand between fingers from tip to root:

- **Smooth, almost slippery** — likely low porosity
- **Slightly rough or catches** — likely high porosity
- **Very rough or breaks** — high porosity with damage

## Mixed Porosity: Common on 2B

Roots are lower porosity (newer growth); ends are higher porosity (older, more handled). If your 2B roots are moisturised after washing but ends are dry or frizzy — particularly if the ends start to frizz before the roots do — this is why.

Practical adjustment: concentrate conditioner and any light sealant at the ends. Apply leave-in more heavily from the mid-length down. Roots often need almost nothing.

## What to Do Based on Your Result

### Low porosity 2B

Getting moisture past the flat cuticle without adding weight to a pattern that weighs down easily.

- **Heat during deep conditioning.** A warm damp towel around a shower cap for 15–20 minutes. On 2B, this is essential — the conditioner won't absorb without heat, and the result is waves that look coated and flat rather than moisturised and defined.
- **Lightest possible liquid leave-in.** If there's any cream texture, it's too heavy for low-porosity 2B. The lightest water-based leave-in on the market.
- **Apply to soaking-wet hair.** The window where a low-porosity cuticle is slightly more receptive is when the hair is very wet. Don't let it partially air dry first.
- **Monthly clarify.** Buildup accumulates faster on low-porosity hair — products pile on the surface. Clarifying monthly keeps the wave surface clear.

### High porosity 2B

Moisture retention is the challenge in UK conditions — particularly in winter when the indoor air is dry.

- **Seal after leave-in.** A couple of drops of lightweight oil applied while the hair is still wet slows moisture evaporation from the wave. This step is optional for low and medium porosity but makes a real difference for high porosity in UK winter.
- **Slightly more weight than low porosity 2B.** High-porosity 2B can handle a marginally richer leave-in — still lightweight, but not the thinnest possible mist. The additional weight helps compensate for fast moisture loss.
- **Diffuse in winter.** High-porosity 2B loses moisture fastest in dry indoor air. Diffusing sets the wave before the indoor air can dry it out.
- **Protein when needed.** When hair feels mushy or stretches more than expected when wet, a protein treatment every six to eight weeks helps. Don't put it on a schedule — use it when the hair signals it.
- **Satin bonnet every night.** High-porosity hair loses moisture through cotton significantly faster than lower-porosity hair.

### Medium porosity 2B

The widest range of 2B-weight products works. Focus on the UK seasonal adjustment (low-glycerin products from October to March) and the drying technique. Medium-porosity 2B that's still struggling with definition is almost always a technique or drying conditions issue rather than a product weight issue.

## Hard Water and Porosity Testing on 2B

Mineral deposits from hard water make the spray bottle test read incorrectly on 2B — the mineral layer absorbs first, making medium or high-porosity 2B wave look low-porosity. Always chelate or clarify before testing.

## FAQs

**My 2B waves look fine on wash day but are flat by day two. Is that a porosity problem?**

Probably not porosity primarily — more likely night routine (pineapple + satin bonnet) or gel amount. High porosity can contribute if the product coat doesn't last overnight, but check the mechanical causes first.

**I've been avoiding heavy products for my 2B but my waves are still dry. What's happening?**

If you're using lightweight products and the waves still feel dry: either low porosity (heat during deep conditioning), hard water (chelate), or high porosity that needs a sealant step you're skipping.

**Does porosity change how often I should deep condition?**

Yes slightly. Low porosity 2B: monthly deep condition with heat, clarify monthly. High porosity 2B: every three to four weeks, with heat, plus protein every six to eight weeks when needed. Medium: every three to four weeks. None of these types need weekly deep conditioning.
