4A wash day is less complicated than 4B or 4C — the S-coil means less tangling, more natural clumping, and generally better product response. But UK-specific factors still apply: hard water across most of England that blocks conditioner, and indoor winter humidity swings that mess with product performance.

This is a wash day routine built for 4A hair in the UK.

---

## Before You Wash: Pre-Poo

Optional for 4A hair, more useful in winter. Apply oil or conditioner to dry hair in sections before washing — it softens the hair before the shampoo step and reduces the friction that causes tangles during washing.

Coconut oil, olive oil, or your regular conditioner all work. Apply section by section, leave 20–30 minutes, then wash as normal. You don't need to saturate the hair — coat the length, focus on the ends.

If your hair isn't particularly tangled before washing, skip the pre-poo and add that time elsewhere.

---

## Shampoo

### Weekly wash: gentle shampoo or co-wash

4A hair doesn't need strong cleansing every week. A sulphate-free shampoo or co-wash (cleansing conditioner) removes day-to-day scalp buildup without stripping. Apply to a wet scalp, work gently with fingertips, and let it rinse through the length.

**Signs you need a stronger wash:** product build-up making coils feel heavy or defined but stiff without gel; scalp itchiness that doesn't resolve; hair looking dull even after washing.

### Monthly: chelating shampoo

If you're in a hard water area — London, South East, East Anglia, Midlands — include a chelating shampoo once a month. This is the most commonly skipped step in UK 4A routines, and it explains why deep conditioning often underperforms.

Hard water minerals settle on the hair shaft with every wash. They're invisible and regular shampoos don't remove them — only a chelating shampoo does. Check the label for EDTA or phytic acid. Use monthly, then deep condition straight after.

## Deep Conditioning

Apply to soaking-wet hair, section by section. 4A hair typically responds well to moisture-focused conditioners — heavy protein treatments every week can tip 4A hair toward stiffness. A moisture conditioner weekly and a light protein treatment every four to six weeks is a reasonable starting framework.

**On heat:** a heat cap, steamer, or warm damp towel over a shower cap significantly improves results — particularly for low-porosity 4A hair where the cuticle resists moisture. Leave for 20–30 minutes minimum.

### Detangle during conditioning

4A hair tangles less dramatically than 4B or 4C, but detangling with conditioner in — before rinsing — is still the easier approach. The slip makes strands separate cleanly. Start at the ends, work upward in sections, fingers before comb if needed.

## Rinsing

Lukewarm or cool water. A cool final rinse helps coils tighten and can add noticeable shine.

If you're in a very hard water area, consider a distilled water pour-over as your last rinse. Your freshly-conditioned hair has just had minerals removed; finishing with hard tap water re-deposits some immediately. A distilled rinse is a small step that protects the work you've just done.

---

## Applying Products

Everything goes on while the hair is soaking wet. Not towel-dried damp. Soaking wet — water should still be dripping.

**Leave-in conditioner:** First layer. Apply section by section, rake or scrunch in.

**Oil:** Second layer (if using LOC method). For 4A hair, lighter oils — jojoba, argan, grapeseed — typically work better than castor oil or heavy butters unless the hair is significantly damaged or highly porous. Heavy oils can weigh 4A coils down and cause clumps to fall rather than hold their shape.

**Cream or gel:** Third layer. For wash-and-go, a hold gel defines coils and reduces frizz. For twist-outs, a curl cream gives a softer result. Apply and scrunch upward to encourage clumping.

### The LCO alternative

Some 4A naturals find LCO (Leave-in, Cream, Oil) better than LOC — cream before the sealing oil means the oil sits on top of more product and holds it in rather than sitting between the leave-in and the next layer. Try both over a few wash days and compare how the hair feels the next morning.

## Drying

Don't use a rough towel. Terry cloth creates friction at the coil and causes frizz before you've even started styling. Use a microfibre towel or old cotton t-shirt to gently squeeze excess water.

Air dry where possible. If you use a diffuser, use low heat with the hair scrunched upward into the diffuser cup rather than held downward. High heat on still-forming 4A coils breaks the clumping and causes frizz.

**Don't touch while drying.** 4A hair that's disturbed while the coil is setting produces frizz. Leave it alone until completely dry, then scrunch out any product cast with a small amount of oil on your palms.

---

## Protecting the Style

**At night:** a satin bonnet or satin-lined pillowcase prevents friction and moisture loss overnight. For loose styles, pineappling (gathering hair loosely at the very top of the head with a satin scrunchie) keeps the length from compressing.

**Refreshing between wash days:** spritz with water and scrunch to revive coils. If you're in a hard water area, use filtered or distilled water in your spritz bottle — tap water re-deposits minerals. A small amount of leave-in or gel can revive lost definition.

## Troubleshooting

**Coils fall apart as they dry:** Possibly insufficient hold product, or hair was touched while drying. Increase your gel amount and leave to dry completely undisturbed.

**Coils look defined when wet but frizzy when dry:** Usually a sealant or humidity issue. Check whether you're sealing after leave-in. In winter, reduce glycerin-heavy products — they can pull moisture out of hair in low-humidity indoor air.

**Conditioner doesn't seem to do anything:** Most likely hard water. Check your postcode, test your water, and add a monthly chelating wash.

**Wash day consistently takes longer than expected:** Usually the detangling step. Detangle with conditioner in — not after rinsing — and work in smaller sections. That alone typically cuts the time significantly.

## FAQs

**Can I co-wash 4A hair every week?**

Yes, if you're not using heavy products that require shampoo to remove. If you're using butters, styling waxes, or silicone serums, a gentle shampoo every couple of weeks prevents buildup that co-washing can't fully clear.

**Should I detangle 4A hair wet or dry?**

Wet with conditioner in. 4A hair doesn't tangle as dramatically as 4B or 4C, but dry detangling still causes unnecessary breakage. Conditioner in the hair is always easier.

**Why do my 4A coils look great on wash day but are frizzy the day after?**

The most common cause is disturbing the hair before it's fully dry. If you've already let it dry undisturbed and the frizz still appears, it's usually humidity-related — try reducing glycerin in your styler.

**My 4A hair is fine after washing but dry by mid-week. What helps?**

First, make sure you're sealing with an oil or cream immediately after leave-in on wash day. Second, refresh mid-week with a spritz and a small amount of leave-in. Third, check your bonnet — moisture loss overnight is significant if you're not protecting your hair.
