4A hair is the tightest curl type that still has a consistent, visible coil when dry. That's what sets it apart from 4B and 4C — where those types collapse into a dense, angular mass without product, 4A has recognisable S-coils roughly the width of a crochet needle. The coil is tight enough to cause significant shrinkage and dryness challenges, but loose enough to respond to lighter products than 4B or 4C typically can.

This guide is specifically for 4A hair in the UK: what the S-coil means in practice, why it dries differently, and a routine built around how 4A actually behaves — including the hard water problem that most UK-based 4A naturals are dealing with without knowing it.

---

## What Is 4A Hair?

4A hair forms tight S-coils — a consistent spiral shape that stays visible even when dry and without product. The coil diameter is small, roughly crochet-needle width, and the hair has a springy, bouncy quality when healthy. It's the most defined of the type 4 categories.

Key characteristics:

- **Consistent S-coil** — the defining feature; visible curl shape when dry
- **High shrinkage** — typically 50–75%, though less dramatic-looking than 4B or 4C because the coil retains its shape
- **Some natural clumping** — 4A hair tends to clump into curl groups more readily than 4B or 4C, which makes wash-and-go styles more achievable
- **Still prone to dryness** — the coil is tight enough to interrupt oil distribution from the scalp

Most people with 4A hair have some variation across their head. The crown tends to be tighter than the temples or nape. Treat your specific hair as the reference, not the category.

### 4A vs 4B and 3C

| Type | Pattern | When dry | Shrinkage |
|------|---------|----------|-----------|
| 3C | Corkscrew coils, pencil-width | Visible ringlets | Moderate |
| 4A | Tight S-coils, crochet-needle width | Visible coils | High |
| 4B | Z or sharp S, angular bends | Minimal curl visible | Very high |

The practical difference between 4A and 3C: 4A is drier and less likely to maintain definition without product. The practical difference between 4A and 4B: 4A has smoother, more consistent curves and tangles in a different way — less hooking at Z-angles, more looping and strand wrapping.

## What Makes 4A Hair Behave the Way It Does

### The S-coil and dryness

The same tight S-coil that gives 4A its distinctive shape is why the hair gets dry. Your scalp produces oils that travel down each hair strand — but at every bend in the coil, some of that oil stalls. On 4A hair with its consistent spiralling, the oil gets a reasonable distance down the strand, but never reaches the ends without help. This is structural, not a scalp health problem.

### Why products behave differently than on 4B or 4C

4A hair has a genuine advantage over 4B and 4C: it responds to lighter products. The S-coil shape creates natural space between strands in each curl group, which means lighter oils and water-based leave-ins can actually reach the inner strands without needing to be heavy enough to push through dense Z-pattern compaction.

This is why the 4B/4C advice to use castor oil and thick butters on everything doesn't always translate to 4A — it can weigh 4A coils down and cause frizz instead of definition. 4A hair generally does better with lighter sealants and mid-weight creams unless it's damaged or highly porous.

### Why 4A can look dry even when it isn't

4A coils shrink dramatically — hair that's at shoulder length wet can spring up to ear-length when dry. That visual compactness can look like dryness when the hair is actually retaining moisture well. Check by stretching a small section: if it stretches without snapping and feels pliable, the moisture is there regardless of how compact it looks.

## A Routine for 4A Hair in the UK

### Hard water first

If you're in London, the South East, East Anglia, or the Midlands, hard water is almost certainly affecting your 4A routine. Minerals in the water settle on the hair shaft with every wash. On 4A hair, this shows up as coils that lose definition faster than they should, conditioner that seems to do nothing, and a rough texture that product doesn't fix.

A chelating shampoo — different from a clarifying shampoo; check the label for EDTA or phytic acid — used once a month strips those minerals off. It's the reset that makes everything else work.

→ [4A hair and hard water UK](/4a/4a-hair-hard-water-uk/)

### Wash day

1. **Pre-poo (optional):** Oil or conditioner applied to dry hair before washing. More useful in winter when the hair is more brittle; optional in summer.
2. **Chelating or clarifying shampoo** (monthly): Scalp-focused, rinse through length.
3. **Gentle shampoo or co-wash** (other weeks): 4A hair doesn't need strong cleansing weekly.
4. **Deep conditioner:** Apply to soaking-wet hair. 4A hair typically responds well to moisture-focused conditioners without needing heavy protein every time.
5. **Detangle during conditioning:** Conditioner provides slip; detangle before rinsing rather than after.
6. **Cool rinse,** then style immediately while wet.

### Moisturising and sealing

Apply leave-in while hair is dripping wet. 4A hair generally does well with the LOC method (Leave-in, Oil, Cream), but a lighter oil — jojoba, argan, sweet almond — works better than castor oil for most 4A hair unless it's high porosity or significantly damaged.

→ [4A hair porosity](/4a/4a-hair-porosity/)

### Protective styling

4A hair handles protective styles well. Twists, braids, and buns all suit the S-coil shape. The same hairline caution applies as for all type 4 hair: tight installation at the edges causes traction damage over time.

## Styling 4A Hair

### Wash and go

4A is the most wash-and-go-friendly of the type 4 categories because the S-coil provides natural clumping that 4B and 4C hair lack. The approach that works best:

- Apply leave-in and gel or cream to soaking-wet hair, section by section
- Use the prayer hands method (pressing hair between palms) or raking through with fingers to define coils
- Scrunch upward to encourage clumping
- Let dry undisturbed — touching while damp causes frizz
- Scrunch out the cast (if gel was used) once fully dry

**UK-specific caveat:** In high humidity outdoors or low humidity indoors (winter central heating), 4A wash-and-gos can revert or go crunchy faster than expected. If your style collapses in British weather, the culprit is often a high-glycerin product pulling moisture in when you don't want it, or releasing it when you do. Reduce glycerin products in winter.

→ [Why your wash and go reverts in UK humidity](/hair-care/wash-and-go-reverting-uk-humidity/)

### Twist-outs and braid-outs

4A hair takes a twist-out well. The S-coil means twists stay set and separate into a defined pattern. Let dry fully before separating — untwisting damp produces frizz — and separate with oil on fingertips.

### Shrinkage

4A shrinkage is high but the coil retains its shape rather than compressing into an amorphous mass. Stretching options:

- **Banding:** Loose hair ties at intervals down damp sections; elongates without heat
- **Twist-outs or braid-outs:** Both stretch and define simultaneously
- **African threading:** Very effective; widely used in West African natural hair traditions

## Common 4A Problems

### Dryness

The most common complaint for 4A hair. The causes and solutions:

- **Hard water:** Check your postcode. In hard water areas, mineral film blocks conditioner — chelating wash monthly is the fix.
- **Products sitting on top:** Try lighter, water-based leave-ins and apply to wetter hair. Thick products on 4A that's already reasonably defined can weigh coils down rather than moisturising.
- **Missing a sealant:** If moisture disappears quickly, you may be applying leave-in without sealing. An oil layer after leave-in significantly extends moisture retention.

→ [Why is my 4A hair always dry?](/4a/4a-hair-always-dry-uk/)

### Product flaking

A common 4A-specific issue: gel meeting butters or heavy oils underneath can break down into white flakes. The fix is usually using less oil before the gel, or switching to a gel that's compatible with your specific product stack.

### Loss of definition in British weather

The UK's combination of high outdoor humidity and low indoor humidity creates a two-way problem: coils frizz outside, then dry out and crunch inside. Glycerin-heavy products are particularly sensitive to this swing. Reduce glycerin in your routine if definition isn't holding through a British day.

## Where to Find Products in the UK

4A hair responds well to a wider range of products than 4B or 4C — many mainstream curl products designed for "curly hair" actually work better on 4A than on tighter types. This means Boots, Superdrug, and Holland & Barrett carry usable options. Specialist Afro-Caribbean beauty supply shops in London, Birmingham, Manchester, and Leeds have the widest range if you want specifically formulated type 4 products.

## FAQs

**Is 4A hair the same as Afro hair?**

No — "Afro hair" covers types 3C through 4C. 4A is one specific pattern within that range.

**Why does my 4A hair look good when wet but not when dry?**

Shrinkage and definition loss as the coil compresses. A strong-hold styler applied to soaking-wet hair and left to dry undisturbed will hold more definition. Once the hair dries, scrunch out any stiffness with a small amount of oil.

**Can 4A hair have low porosity?**

Yes. Porosity is not determined by curl pattern. Low-porosity 4A is common, and building a routine designed for high porosity when yours is low will result in product buildup and no absorption improvement.

**How often should I wash 4A hair?**

Every five to ten days is a common starting point. More frequent than type 4B or 4C hair is fine if your scalp needs it — 4A hair handles washing more frequently without as much tangles cost, as long as you're conditioning thoroughly each time.
