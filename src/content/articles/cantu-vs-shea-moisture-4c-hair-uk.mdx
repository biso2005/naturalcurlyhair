**Cantu for everyday leave-in, winter styling, and budget.** **SheaMoisture for deep conditioning and high porosity 4C that needs maximum moisture.** The honest answer for most 4C hair in the UK is that the best routine uses both — different products for different jobs.

But if you're choosing one brand to start with, here's how to decide.

---

## Start With Cantu If:

**Budget is the primary constraint.** Cantu's core products (Leave-In Conditioning Repair Cream, Moisturizing Curl Activator) cost less per gram than comparable SheaMoisture products and are widely available at Boots, Superdrug, Sainsbury's, and Asda.

**You're buying in winter.** Cantu products are lower-glycerin than most of the SheaMoisture range. In UK winter with central heating dropping indoor humidity to 30–40%, lower-glycerin products perform more consistently. SheaMoisture's high-glycerin formulations can frizz or dry out in winter conditions. → [Glycerin in natural hair products: summer vs winter UK](/products/glycerin-natural-hair-winter-summer-uk/)

**Your 4C hair is medium porosity.** Cantu's moisture density suits medium porosity 4C well. The Leave-In provides enough moisture without needing the heavy density of the SheaMoisture JBCO range.

**You need a daily leave-in without buildup.** The Cantu Leave-In Conditioning Repair Cream is the strongest daily leave-in in either brand's range for 4C.

---

## Start With SheaMoisture If:

**Your 4C hair is high porosity.** High porosity 4C loses moisture rapidly. SheaMoisture's denser, higher-glycerin formulations provide more moisture per application — appropriate when the hair needs to absorb and hold as much as possible during each wash day.

**Deep conditioning is the priority.** The SheaMoisture Manuka Honey & Yogurt Hydrate + Repair Hair Masque is the best readily available deep conditioner in the UK for 4C hair at any price point. No Cantu product matches it for deep conditioning.

**You have bleached or chemically treated 4C.** The SheaMoisture JBCO Strengthen & Restore range is formulated with more strengthening and conditioning layering — appropriate for 4C that has additional damage.

## The Routine Most 4C Hair in the UK Ends Up On

Wash day: SheaMoisture Manuka Honey Masque as deep conditioner.
Leave-in: Cantu Leave-In Conditioning Repair Cream.
Styling: Cantu Curl Activator Cream + Eco Style or LA Looks gel for hold.
Scalp: light oil of choice.

This isn't brand loyalty — it's choosing the best product from each brand for the job it does best.

## FAQs

**Has SheaMoisture changed its formulas recently?**

Yes — some products in the SheaMoisture range have been reformulated since the Unilever acquisition in 2017. The Manuka Honey Masque has remained most consistent; the Curl & Shine range has changed most noticeably. If a product you used before isn't performing, check the ingredient list against a batch from a year or two ago. → [SheaMoisture UK honest review](/products/shea-moisture-review-uk-natural-hair/)

**Is Cantu a Black-owned brand?**

Cantu is not independently Black-owned — it's distributed by Softsheen-Carson (L'Oréal subsidiary). It was developed to serve the Black natural hair market and its community positioning reflects that, but ownership is at a large multinational level.

**Which is better for 4C low porosity hair?**

Low porosity 4C needs effective cleansing more than maximum moisture density. Start with Cantu — lower glycerin works better for low porosity in winter, and the Clarifying Shampoo is a good low porosity routine addition. The SheaMoisture masque is still useful monthly, but the high-glycerin SheaMoisture styling products build up on low porosity hair faster than Cantu.

**Can I use Cantu and SheaMoisture together in the same routine?**

Yes — they're both silicone-free and the ingredient profiles are compatible. The most common 4C combination (Cantu leave-in, SheaMoisture masque for deep conditioning) is deliberately cross-brand.
