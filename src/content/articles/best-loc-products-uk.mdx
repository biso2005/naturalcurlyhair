Locs need significantly fewer products than loose natural hair. The heavy leave-ins, thick butters, and layered sealing routines of loose hair become buildup problems in locs. The categories that matter are: a residue-free shampoo, a scalp oil, and a retwist product. Everything else is optional.

This guide covers products actually available in the UK, with honest notes on what's in each category and what to avoid.

---

## The Essential Three

### 1. Residue-free or clarifying shampoo

The most important product for locs. Regular moisturising shampoos leave conditioning residue inside the loc that accumulates over time and causes buildup, mildew risk, and loss of definition.

**What to look for:** "residue-free" on the label, or a clarifying shampoo. No heavy silicones, no heavy conditioning agents high in the ingredient list.

**UK options:**
- **Jamaican Mango & Lime Tingle Shampoo** — designed specifically for locs, widely used, available online and in Afro BSS
- **Dr Bronner's Pure-Castile Liquid Soap** — diluted in water, gentle, effectively clarifying, available at health food shops and Amazon
- **Cantu Apple Cider Vinegar Rinse & Shampoo** — available at Boots and Superdrug, mild chelating action alongside clarifying
- **Malibu C Hard Water Wellness Shampoo** — chelating and clarifying, good for hard water areas, available online

**What to avoid:** anything labelled "moisturising," "hydrating," or "for dry hair" as the primary function. These deposit conditioning agents that accumulate inside locs.

### 2. Scalp oil

Locs don't need oil along the length — this creates buildup. The scalp does need oil, applied directly between the locs to the scalp surface.

**What to look for:** lightweight oil that absorbs without creating surface film. Jojoba, sweet almond, or argan.

**UK options:**
- **Jojoba oil** — available in health food shops (Holland & Barrett), pharmacies, and Amazon. Closest to sebum in molecular composition, absorbs cleanly.
- **Jamaican Mango & Lime Loc'd In Conditioner** — a scalp-specific spray, not for the loc length
- **Dr. Miracle's Feel It Formula Hair & Scalp Oil** — warming scalp oil, widely available in BSS stores, good for circulation

**What to avoid:** castor oil directly on the scalp between locs — too viscous, accumulates at the root and creates buildup. Heavy butters on the scalp.

### 3. Retwist product

Applied only at the root during retwisting to hold the new growth in place while it knits into the loc.

**What to look for:** light hold, minimal residue. Avoid heavy waxes — they accumulate inside the loc over time and are almost impossible to remove.

**UK options:**
- **Jamaican Mango & Lime Locking Firm Wax** — light hold version, used sparingly at the root
- **Murray's Beeswax** — traditional, very widely available in BSS and Afro beauty shops. Use very sparingly — heavier waxes build up
- **Aloe vera gel** — the most residue-free option, provides hold without accumulation; diluted slightly for retwisting; available at Holland & Barrett, Boots online
- **Flaxseed gel** — similar to aloe vera, light film-forming hold, very low residue

**What to avoid:** any wax applied to the full length of the loc. Loc butter applied heavily — a small amount at the root only. "Loc retwisting cream" products with heavy conditioning agents.

## Optional: Light Frizz Product for the Exterior

For UK outdoor humidity frizz, a light film-forming product on the exterior of the loc helps keep surface hairs down.

**UK options:**
- **Flaxseed gel** — make at home (simmer flaxseeds in water, strain, cool) or buy; very light hold
- **Aunt Jackie's Don't Shrink Flaxseed Elongating Curling Gel** — available in BSS, light hold, low buildup risk
- **Cantu Moisturizing Curl Activator Cream** — used very lightly on the exterior; available at Boots and Superdrug

Apply only to the exterior surface, not into the loc. Light application — a small amount worked over the palm and smoothed over the loc.

## What Locs Don't Need

**Heavy deep conditioners inside the loc:** conditioning agents accumulate in the loc interior.

**Leave-in conditioner applied to the loc length:** same issue. Scalp only.

**Heavy oils along the length:** use scalp oil on the scalp; not on the loc length.

**Growth products or serums:** these don't affect how fast hair grows. They're marketing.

**Loc-specific shampoo that's actually just a moisturising shampoo with "loc" on the label:** check the ingredient list for heavy conditioning agents. If it's predominantly conditioning, it's not suitable for regular loc washing regardless of what the label says.

## Where to Buy in the UK

**BSS (Afro hair beauty supply stores):** London (Brixton, Peckham, Tottenham, Wood Green), Birmingham, Manchester, Leeds, Bristol, and most cities with significant Afro-Caribbean communities. Best prices on Jamaican Mango & Lime, Murray's, and other loc-specific products.

**Boots and Superdrug:** Cantu, SheaMoisture (limited loc-specific range), some Dr Bronner's.

**Holland & Barrett:** jojoba oil, aloe vera gel, sweet almond oil.

**Amazon UK:** broadest selection. Malibu C, Ion, less common US brands.

## FAQs

**Can I use a moisturising conditioner to co-wash my locs?**

Not advisable as a regular practice. Moisturising conditioner inside locs creates buildup. Some people use a rinse with diluted apple cider vinegar as a light between-wash refresh — this is lower-risk than conditioner co-washing.

**Do I need to buy specialist loc products or will regular natural hair products work?**

Regular natural hair products can work if they meet the residue-free criteria. The "loc" label on a product is marketing; what matters is the formulation. A Dr Bronner's diluted castile wash is a better loc shampoo than many products sold specifically for locs.

**My loctician uses a specific product I can't find online. What do I do?**

Ask them directly what it is and where to get it. Locticians often use professional-range products not widely stocked in retail. Some will sell products directly; others will order for you.
