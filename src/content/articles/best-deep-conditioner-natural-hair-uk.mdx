## Moisture Deep Conditioners

Most natural hair — particularly type 4 — needs a moisturising deep conditioner weekly or fortnightly. Protein treatments are for when the moisture alone isn't enough, not the default.

**The pick: SheaMoisture Manuka Honey & Yogurt Hydrate + Repair Hair Masque** — a rare product that genuinely does both: adds moisture and has mild protein from the yogurt. Suitable for most type 3 and 4 hair. The texture is thick enough to work through coily hair without sliding off. Available at Boots, Superdrug, and Sainsbury's. Around £12 for 326g.

**Runner-up: Camille Rose Algae Renew Deep Conditioning Mask** — lighter than the SheaMoisture, better for looser curl types (type 3, or fine-stranded type 4 that finds heavier masks weighty). Available at Boots and online. Around £18 for 240g.

**Skip: Herbal Essences Hello Hydration Deep Moisture Mask** — widely available and inexpensive, but formulated for Eurocentric hair types and doesn't have enough slip or penetration for type 4 coily hair. Fine for type 2, inappropriate as a routine deep conditioner for 4A–4C.

## Protein Deep Conditioners

Use a protein deep conditioner when the hair shows signs of protein deficiency: stretches too far before breaking, feels mushy when wet, looks limp and flat, has lost its curl definition. If uncertain, run the stretch test first. → [Protein vs moisture balance for natural hair](/hair-care/protein-vs-moisture-balance-natural-hair/)

**The pick: Aphogee Two-Minute Keratin Reconstructor** — the most accessible protein treatment in the UK and the most forgiving to use. "Two minutes" is accurate — it's a light protein treatment, not a hard protein treatment. Appropriate for monthly use on most hair types. Available at BSS and Amazon UK. Around £12 for 237ml.

**Runner-up: ORS Hair Mayo Protein Pack** — a heavier protein hit than Aphogee Two-Minute. Appropriate when the stretch test shows significant elasticity loss or the hair is very damaged. Use no more than monthly. Available at Superdrug and Boots. Around £9 for 227g.

**Skip: Aphogee Two-Step Protein Treatment** — the strongest protein treatment on the market. Not appropriate for home use without clear knowledge of the process. It hardens the hair during application and must be rinsed and followed immediately with a moisture treatment. Misuse causes breakage. The Two-Minute version handles 90% of protein needs; the Two-Step is for severe protein-deficient or bleached hair only.

## For Specific Situations

**After a chelating wash (monthly, hard water areas):**

Chelating removes minerals but temporarily depletes moisture. The deep conditioner applied immediately after a chelating wash is the most important one in the monthly cycle — make it a full moisturising deep condition with fifteen to twenty minutes of heat (shower cap + warm towel) rather than a quick condition and rinse.

**After a protective style removal:**

The hair coming out of a protective style after four to eight weeks has been starved of fresh moisture for that period. A thorough shampoo and a long deep condition (twenty to thirty minutes with heat) before doing anything else with the hair prevents the brittle post-style breakage that loses the length the style retained.

**For low porosity hair:**

Low porosity hair resists moisture uptake because the cuticle lies flat and doesn't open easily. Heat helps: a steam treatment or a deep conditioner applied under a plastic cap warmed with a hooded dryer or warm towel forces the cuticle to open and allows the conditioner to penetrate. A cold-applied deep conditioner on low porosity hair often just sits on the surface and rinses off without penetrating.

**For high porosity hair:**

High porosity hair absorbs moisture quickly but loses it just as fast. Thicker, heavier deep conditioners work better than lightweight ones — they coat the shaft more effectively and slow moisture loss. A protein treatment quarterly helps repair the damaged cuticle gaps that cause high porosity.

## How to Deep Condition Properly

The conditioner isn't doing much work in two minutes. Fifteen to twenty minutes minimum for a moisturising treatment; the instructions on protein treatments vary.

1. Shampoo first — a deep conditioner applied to buildup-covered hair can't penetrate
2. Apply the conditioner to sections, root to tip
3. Plastic cap or shower cap on
4. Heat: hooded dryer, warm towel, or steam — ten to twenty minutes
5. Rinse thoroughly — leaving conditioner in the hair is not the same as deep conditioning
6. Proceed to leave-in and style

## FAQs

**Can I deep condition every week?**

Yes for moisturising deep conditioners — weekly is appropriate for dry, high porosity, or type 4 hair. No for protein treatments — weekly protein use without balancing moisture causes protein overload, which makes the hair stiff and brittle.

**My deep conditioner makes the hair soft in the shower but the hair is dry within a day. What's happening?**

The conditioner is coating the hair surface but not penetrating it. Two likely causes: either the hair is low porosity (needs heat to open the cuticle for penetration) or there's product or mineral buildup blocking absorption. Clarify and chelate first, then try the deep condition again with heat.

**Is the expensive deep conditioner worth it over a cheaper one?**

Sometimes. The SheaMoisture Manuka Honey masque is mid-price and outperforms several products twice its price. Ingredient quality matters more than brand tier. Look for glycerin, aloe vera, shea butter, or rice water high in the ingredient list — these are markers of formulations that work for natural hair.

**My deep conditioner leaves the hair limp. What's wrong?**

Either it's too heavy for your hair's porosity and density (fine, low porosity hair needs a lighter formulation), or it contains a lot of silicones that coat the hair rather than conditioning it, or you're not clarifying regularly enough and the conditioner is sitting on buildup. Try the Camille Rose as a lighter alternative and clarify before the next wash.
