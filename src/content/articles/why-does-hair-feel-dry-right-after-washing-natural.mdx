Hair that feels dry immediately after washing — not as it air-dries, but while you're still in the shower or right after rinsing — is telling you something specific: the wash process removed moisture and your conditioner didn't replace it adequately. This is a product and technique problem, not a permanent feature of your hair.

## The most common causes

**Sulphate shampoo used too often.** Sulphate shampoos are effective cleansers, but used weekly on natural hair — which is already prone to dryness — they strip the natural oils (sebum) that help the hair feel moisturised. If you're shampooing with a sulphate-based product every wash day, reduce to every two to four weeks and use a co-wash or sulphate-free shampoo in between.

**Hard water.** In hard water areas, calcium and magnesium minerals bond with shampoo ingredients during washing and form a residue that coats the hair shaft. This coating actively blocks moisture from getting in, even when you condition afterwards. The result is hair that feels coated and rough after washing despite conditioning. This is one of the most common causes of dry-feeling hair in London, the East Midlands, and the Home Counties that people don't recognise. A chelating shampoo once a month removes the mineral layer. [Check your water hardness.](/tools/water-hardness/)

**Not enough conditioner, or not enough contact time.** A quick rinse-out conditioner applied for two minutes does very little for dense 4A–4C hair. Deep conditioning — a richer product, left on for 20–30 minutes under a plastic cap, sometimes with heat — is necessary for 4C hair in particular to feel moisturised after washing. If you're not deep conditioning, start there.

**Protein overload.** If you've recently used a protein-heavy product (a protein treatment, or a protein-rich conditioner), hair can feel dry and stiff rather than moisturised. The fix is a moisture-only deep conditioner. Protein makes hair feel dry even when it's technically not damaged.

**Water temperature.** Washing with very hot water opens the cuticle aggressively and strips more oils than warm water. A cool final rinse closes the cuticle and helps the hair feel smoother immediately after washing.

## Diagnosing which one

- Dry feeling only when shampooing, feels better after conditioning → shampoo is too stripping
- Dry feeling after both shampoo and conditioning, conditioner seems to do nothing → hard water mineral block, or protein overload
- Dry feeling despite long conditioning time → product may be too light for your hair's density or porosity
- Hair feels stiff and dry rather than soft and dry → protein, not moisture, is the issue

## What to try first

If you're not sure which cause applies, try this sequence on your next wash:

1. Chelating shampoo (removes mineral build-up — rules out hard water as the cause)
2. Deep conditioner with heat, 30 minutes
3. Cool final rinse
4. Leave-in applied to soaking-wet hair before any product

If your hair feels significantly better after this wash than usual, hard water and/or insufficient conditioning contact time were the issue. Adjust your routine from there.
