Crochet hair is a method of attaching extension hair to a braided cornrow base using a small latch hook tool — the hook loops pre-made extensions through the cornrow, securing them without thread or glue. It's one of the fastest protective styles available: a full head of crochet hair typically takes two to four hours, significantly less than box braids or wrapped faux locs.

---

## How Crochet Hair Works

**The base:** the natural hair is braided into flat cornrows first, usually in a pattern designed for the specific style being installed (straight back for a wavy look; circular pattern for full Afro styles; specific patterns for faux locs or braids).

**The installation:** extension hair — pre-looped or purchased separately — is hooked through the cornrow using a latch hook tool. The hook goes under the cornrow, catches the extension hair, and pulls it through, creating a secure attachment without thread or adhesive.

**The result:** the extension hair covers the cornrow base. From the outside, the style looks like the extension texture — wavy, curly, straight, loc, or braid patterns depending on the type of crochet hair used.

## Crochet Hair Types Available in the UK

**Faux locs:** pre-made loc extensions crocheted onto the base. Lower cost and faster than wrapped faux locs. Available in Afro hair BSS and online.

**Spring twists / passion twists:** pre-twisted extension hair that creates a defined, chunky twist look. Very popular in the UK. Available in Boots, Superdrug (limited), and extensively in BSS stores.

**Marley twists:** a looser, more textured twist extension. Natural-looking texture.

**Water wave / goddess wave:** pre-curled extensions that create a wavy, loose curl look. For natural hair wearers who want a longer curl pattern than their own.

**Box braid extensions:** pre-made braids crocheted in. Faster installation than individual box braids. The braids may look slightly different from hand-installed box braids.

**Afro kinky:** textured extension hair that blends with natural 4A–4C hair texture. Good for volume without definition.

## Cost in the UK

**Salon installation: £60–£150** depending on the style, the amount of extension hair, and the stylist. Significantly cheaper than box braids or wrapped faux locs because of the faster installation time.

**DIY:** crochet hair can be done at home. The latch hook tool costs £2–£5. The extension hair cost varies by type — spring twists and passion twists run £5–£12 per pack, and a full head uses three to six packs.

## Does Crochet Work on Natural Hair?

Yes, with considerations:

**The cornrow base is the critical step.** Crochet relies on well-braided cornrows underneath. If the cornrows are loose or poorly installed, the crochet extensions will pull out quickly. If you're doing the cornrows yourself, take time with them.

**Cornrow tension at the hairline.** The same traction risk as standard cornrows — the hairline is most vulnerable. Loose cornrows at the hairline, with the crochet extensions starting slightly behind the hairline rather than at it, reduces edge stress.

**Natural hair must be moisturised before installation.** The cornrows will be under the extensions for four to eight weeks. Moisturised natural hair going in means healthier natural hair coming out.

## Care While In

**Scalp moisture:** the cornrow base is covered by the extensions. A lightweight oil or moisture spray applied with a nozzle directly to the scalp through the extensions is the same as any protective style.

**Night protection:** a large satin bonnet if the extensions are short enough, or a satin pillowcase for longer styles. Spring twists and passion twists frizz at the surface with cotton friction.

**Scalp washing:** diluted shampoo applied to the scalp, rinsed thoroughly, dried fully. The extensions can be gently squeezed during rinsing.

**Hard water:** in London and hard water areas, the extension hair shows mineral dullness over time. An ACV rinse after washing every two weeks helps maintain the texture appearance.

---

## Duration

**Crochet faux locs:** four to six weeks.
**Spring twists / passion twists:** three to five weeks before significant frizzing.
**Water wave / goddess wave:** two to four weeks, depending on how much the curl loosens.

All crochet styles last less long than equivalent installation methods (wrapped faux locs last longer than crochet faux locs; hand-installed box braids last longer than crochet box braids) because the attachment point is a single hook-through rather than a continuous wrap. But the installation speed advantage is significant.

---

## Removal

Remove the extension hair first by unpicking the latch hook attachment — a crochet hook or your fingernail at the base releases the loop. Then the natural cornrow is unravelled with conditioner.

**Do not cut.** Extension hair on crochet installs sits very close to the cornrow base — cutting risks cutting the natural hair underneath.

## FAQs

**Can I do crochet hair on myself at home?**

Yes. The latch hook technique is learnable in one session. The main challenge is doing the cornrow base — if you can cornrow the back of your own hair, you can crochet the extensions. Many people do the cornrows themselves and have a friend or family member help with the harder-to-reach sections.

**Will crochet hair damage my edges?**

Only if the cornrows at the hairline are too tight. The crochet extensions themselves don't pull on the edge hair — the cornrow does. Ask for loose cornrows at the edges specifically.

**What's the best crochet style for natural hair?**

Afro kinky and spring twists blend best with natural hair textures (4A–4C) because the texture matches. Water wave and goddess wave create a looser pattern that contrasts with natural coils — some people want this contrast; others find it looks mismatched. Test with a smaller amount of extension hair before committing to a full install.

**Can I colour crochet extension hair?**

Some synthetic crochet hair can be dip-dyed with diluted fabric dye. Bleach and regular hair colour don't work on synthetic hair. Human hair crochet extensions (more expensive) can be coloured like natural hair. Check the packaging for whether the extension hair is synthetic or human.
