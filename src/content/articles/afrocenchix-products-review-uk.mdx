Afrocenchix is an independent Black-owned brand founded in London, making products specifically formulated for Afro-textured hair and manufactured in the UK. That origin story matters to the community that supports them, and it should — supporting a UK-made, independently Black-owned brand serves values that buying SheaMoisture or Cantu doesn't.

But this is a product review, so the editorial standard is the same as for any other brand: which products work, which don't, and whether the price is justified. The good news is that the core Afrocenchix products are genuinely good. The price point is higher than US brands available in the UK, and for the right products that's justified. For others, the price-to-performance ratio is less clear.

---

## Worth Buying

**Afrocenchix Swish Moisturising Shampoo** — the best product in the range and one of the better sulphate-free moisturising shampoos made specifically for Afro-textured hair in the UK market. Cleanses without stripping, has enough slip to begin detangling during the wash, and leaves the hair feeling clean rather than stripped or coated. Around £14 for 200ml — expensive per ml but the formulation justifies it. Available direct from afrocenchix.com and Boots.

**Afrocenchix Whipped Moisturising Hair Cream** — a medium-weight styling cream that works well for 4A and 4B hair as both a leave-in and a curl cream. Moisturises without the heavy glycerin load of many competing creams, which makes it more consistent in UK winter. Around £15 for 200ml.

**Afrocenchix Soar Scalp Oil** — a lightweight scalp oil designed for regular scalp treatment. The peppermint provides a pleasant cooling sensation (similar function to Mielle Rosemary), the carrier oils are light enough to absorb rather than coat. Around £14 for 100ml.

---

## Worth It Conditionally

**Afrocenchix Soothe Scalp Serum** — a targeted treatment for dry, flaky, or irritated scalps. Works well as a periodic treatment. At £16 for 50ml it's expensive for what is essentially a scalp oil with added active ingredients, but for people with persistent scalp sensitivity issues it has a more specific use case than a general scalp oil. If your scalp is fine, the Soar oil does the same job for less.

---

## The Price Question

Afrocenchix products are priced at £14–18 for 50–200ml — significantly more expensive per ml than Cantu or SheaMoisture, and comparable to or above Camille Rose and Mielle. The reasons are real: UK manufacturing costs more than overseas manufacturing, independent production at smaller scale costs more per unit, and the formulations use higher-quality base ingredients than most mass-market alternatives.

Whether that justifies the price depends on what you're optimising for. If you're choosing between the Afrocenchix Swish Shampoo and the As I Am Curl Clarity Shampoo purely on performance, the Swish is better — but not at twice the price per ml. If you're choosing on the basis of supporting a UK-independent Black-owned brand using UK manufacturing, the premium is the point.

Both reasons are valid. The editorial standard here is to be honest that the premium exists and what you're getting for it.

## FAQs

**Is Afrocenchix worth the premium over US brands?**

For the Swish shampoo and the Whipped cream, yes if formulation quality and brand values are both factors in your choice. For a pure cost-per-ml comparison, you can build an equivalent routine for less using Cantu and As I Am. Most readers will end up somewhere in between — using Afrocenchix for specific products where the formulation advantage is most clear (shampoo, scalp treatment) and US brands for the rest of the routine.

**Does Afrocenchix work on 4C hair?**

The Whipped cream is appropriate for 4A and 4B. For very dense 4C that needs maximum moisture, the Whipped cream is lighter than ideal as a standalone product — use it as a leave-in and add a heavier cream on top, or combine with a heavier sealant. The Swish shampoo works across all type 4 hair.

**Is Afrocenchix available in stores?**

Boots stocks Afrocenchix in some locations and online. The most reliable source is afrocenchix.com direct — they ship to UK addresses, offer subscription discounts, and the full range is available. Some independent natural hair stores in London stock it.

**Are Afrocenchix products free from potentially problematic ingredients?**

Yes. The range is free from sulphates, silicones, parabens, mineral oil, and artificial fragrance — a stricter clean formulation standard than most natural hair brands including several that market themselves as natural. The fragrance-free approach also makes the products appropriate for sensitive scalps.
