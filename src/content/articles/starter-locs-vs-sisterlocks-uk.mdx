Both are locs. The meaningful differences are size, cost, installation method, and who can install them. Sisterlocks is a proprietary system with a specific look. Traditional starter locs are more versatile and significantly cheaper. The right choice depends on the aesthetic you want and your budget.

---

## What Makes Sisterlocks Different

Sisterlocks were developed by Dr JoAnne Cornwell in the 1990s and are a trademarked system. The key features:

- **Very small, uniform locs** — much finer than standard starter locs
- **Installed with a specific interlocking tool** — not twists or coils
- **Must be installed by a trained Sisterlocks consultant** — the training and certification are required; someone without it can't install them correctly
- **Proprietary retightening method** — maintenance should also be done by a trained consultant, at least initially

The resulting locs are small enough to style like loose hair — worn out, in twists, braids, up-dos — which is a significant practical difference from thicker locs. They're also significantly more expensive to install and maintain.

---

## What Starter Locs Are

Starter locs is the general term for traditional locs begun with twists, coils, or interlocking — not the Sisterlocks proprietary system. They produce larger, thicker locs. The size varies based on section size and the stylist's approach.

Any trained loctician can install them. Home installation is also possible, particularly with two-strand twists. Maintenance is more flexible — many people retwist their own locs at home with good results.

## Comparing the Two

| | Starter locs | Sisterlocks |
|--|-------------|-------------|
| Loc size | Medium to large | Very small, fine |
| Installation | Twists, coils, interlocking | Proprietary tool, certified consultant only |
| Installation cost (UK) | £80–£250 | £350–£700+ |
| Maintenance | DIY possible | Consultant recommended, especially early |
| Maturation timeline | 12–18 months typical | 18–24+ months (smaller sections take longer) |
| Styling versatility | Medium | High — size allows loose-hair-like styling |
| Hard water risk | Same as all locs | Higher — smaller sections, more surface area |

## Cost in the UK

Sisterlocks installation in the UK ranges from approximately £350 to over £700 depending on hair length and density. Retightening sessions run £60–£150 every four to six weeks.

Traditional starter locs: installation £80–£250. Maintenance at home is possible from month two onwards with good results, reducing ongoing cost significantly.

Sisterlocks cost is often the deciding factor. It's a substantial financial commitment on top of the time commitment.

---

## Finding a Sisterlocks Consultant in the UK

Sisterlocks International maintains a consultant directory at sisterlocks.com. UK consultants are concentrated in London, Birmingham, and Manchester. Outside these cities, options are limited — some people travel for installation and less-frequent maintenance.

When booking, verify the consultant is listed on the Sisterlocks International website, not just someone who has watched tutorials. The technique is specific and errors in the installation pattern affect the long-term health of the locs.

## Which to Choose

**Choose Sisterlocks if:**
- You want very fine, uniform locs
- Styling versatility matters — you want to wear them loose, in updos, in braids
- Your budget allows for the installation and regular maintenance cost
- You're in a city with access to a certified consultant
- You understand the 18–24+ month maturation timeline

**Choose traditional starter locs if:**
- You want a shorter path to the locs look (12–18 months vs 18–24+)
- Budget is a significant consideration
- You're interested in DIY maintenance
- Loc size is not the primary concern
- You want flexibility in how they're installed and maintained

## FAQs

**Can I convert Sisterlocks to traditional locs later?**

Not cleanly. The sections in Sisterlocks are very small and the interlocking method creates a specific internal structure. You can't add adjacent locs together easily after years of separate locking. The commitment is to the size.

**Can I get Sisterlocks if I have type 3 hair?**

Yes, though type 3 hair takes longer to loc than type 4 in any method. In Sisterlocks with the smaller sections, type 3 can take significantly longer — sometimes three years or more to full maturity. Discuss this with a consultant for your specific hair type.

**My friend has Sisterlocks and I tried to copy the installation at home. Is that okay?**

Not advisable. The Sisterlocks pattern — the specific locking method and grid of the interlocking — requires training to execute correctly. Incorrect installation can cause locs to thin or break at the installation points. Invest in the proper installation or choose traditional starter locs.

**Are Sisterlocks better for professional settings?**

Both traditional locs and Sisterlocks are protected hairstyles under the Equality Act 2010. Whether Sisterlocks "look more professional" in a given workplace is a function of the workplace's discriminatory norms, not the legitimacy of the style. Neither requires justification.
