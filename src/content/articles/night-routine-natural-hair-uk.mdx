The night routine for natural hair takes four minutes. It is not complicated.

---

## The Routine

**Step 1: Pineapple or secure the hair (30 seconds)**

Gather the hair loosely at the top of the head with a satin scrunchie — the "pineapple" — or loosely section it into two or three large twists. This keeps the hair up and off the pillow and reduces overnight tangling.

For locs or braids: a loose pineapple, or leave them down if the bonnet fits.
For shorter hair: leave loose; the bonnet does the work.

**Step 2: Spritz if needed (30 seconds)**

If the hair feels dry — run a hand over it. If it feels rough or brittle, a light water mist or a diluted leave-in spray refreshes the moisture. If it feels fine, skip it. Don't over-moisturise hair that doesn't need it.

In UK winter, central heating draws moisture from the hair overnight. A light spritz before bed reduces the dryness that accumulates by morning.

**Step 3: Satin bonnet (10 seconds)**

On. Done.

If you don't have a bonnet: a satin pillowcase. Less effective (the hair moves around more) but significantly better than cotton.

**Total: approximately four minutes.**

---

## Why This Matters

Cotton pillowcases absorb moisture from the hair and create friction on the hair shaft throughout the night — approximately seven to eight hours of contact, every night. This friction:

- Frizzes the curl or coil pattern
- Removes moisture the routine just put in
- Contributes to breakage at the ends and roots that contact the pillow

Satin doesn't absorb moisture and creates significantly less friction. The hair in a satin environment overnight comes out in the morning much closer to how it went in.

This is the single highest-impact habit in natural hair care. More so than any product.

## Adjustments by Style

**Wash-and-go:** pineapple or large loose twists before bed, satin bonnet. The pineapple preserves the definition gathered at the top of the head so it doesn't compress against the pillow.

**Twist or braid-out (setting overnight):** leave the twists or braids in as the set. Bonnet over the top. No pineapple needed — the twists or braids are already the style.

**Protective style (braids, box braids, faux locs):** large satin bonnet if the style fits, or gather with a satin scrunchie and satin pillowcase for longer styles.

**Locs:** satin bonnet or satin pillowcase. Locs against cotton create lint accumulation over time, not just frizz.

---

## In UK Winter

Central heating runs overnight in most UK homes. The bedroom can reach 30–40% relative humidity — dry enough to draw moisture from the hair through the night even with a bonnet.

**Additional step for winter:** a very light oil (jojoba, a drop of argan) smoothed over the hair before the bonnet. This seals surface moisture and slows the rate of overnight evaporation in dry indoor air.

## FAQs

**The bonnet keeps falling off. What do I do?**

A bonnet with a tighter elastic, or a satin-lined sleep cap (which sits more like a beanie hat and doesn't slip as easily). Also try the pineapple first — the gathered hair at the top of the head creates a better structure for the bonnet to sit on.

**Can I sleep without a bonnet if I use a satin pillowcase?**

Yes. The satin pillowcase does most of the work — it reduces friction and doesn't absorb moisture. A bonnet is more effective (the hair doesn't move around), but a satin pillowcase is a good substitute.

**My hair is very short (TWA or close crop). Do I need a bonnet?**

Yes, if you have natural coily hair at any length. Even very short natural hair benefits from satin rather than cotton overnight — the coils are still susceptible to friction frizz and moisture loss.

**I share a bed with someone and feel self-conscious about the bonnet. Are there less obvious alternatives?**

A satin-lined bonnet or beanie that looks more like casual nightwear. A satin pillowcase as the primary method. A silk head wrap tied loosely, which looks more like a scarf than a traditional bonnet. All work better than cotton.
