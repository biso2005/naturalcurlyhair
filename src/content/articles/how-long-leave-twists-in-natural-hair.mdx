Two different questions get asked as one: how long to leave twists in as a protective style, and how long to leave twists in before unravelling for a twist-out. The answers are very different.

**As a protective style:** one to two weeks, maximum.
**As a twist-out base:** overnight to two nights.

---

## As a Protective Style

Two-strand twists worn as a protective style — left in, not unravelled — protect the hair from manipulation and keep it moisturised under a satin bonnet. The limits:

**One week:** good for most hair types. The scalp needs moisture and some access for washing by this point.

**Two weeks:** the maximum most people can go while keeping the style looking presentable and the scalp healthy. Beyond two weeks:
- The scalp becomes harder to clean properly
- New growth at the root creates frizz and distortion at the twist base
- The twist ends may start to mat, especially for very coily hair

**What lets you go longer:** consistent scalp moisture every two to three days, scalp washing at week one (a diluted shampoo through the twists, rinsed and dried), and a satin bonnet every night.

**When to take them down sooner:** if the scalp is significantly itchy before the week is up, or if individual twists are unravelling and tangling with each other. Don't force the timeline past the scalp's tolerance.

---

## As a Twist-Out Base

Twists being used to set a twist-out pattern need to be:
1. Applied on freshly washed, damp hair
2. Left until completely dry throughout — not just dry on the outside
3. Then unravelled

**Minimum time:** if you wash and twist in the evening and the hair is fully dry by morning, overnight is sufficient. For thick or dense hair, or in UK winter when indoor air is warm and dry from central heating, overnight may not be enough.

**In UK winter:** heat from central heating dries hair faster indoors. Check the thickest twists in the morning by squeezing — if they still feel cool inside, they need more time. Let them dry longer before unravelling or spend 10–15 minutes diffusing on low heat.

**In UK summer:** the house is less heated; humidity is slightly higher. Twists may take longer to dry. If you need the style the next morning, wash and twist earlier in the day to allow more drying time.

**What happens if you unravel too soon:** the twist-out pattern collapses within an hour. The hair was set in the shape but hadn't dried in that position — the moisture still in the hair allows it to revert immediately when the twist is removed.

---

## Leaving Twists in Too Long

Twists that are left in beyond the point the scalp can tolerate accumulate:
- Sebum at the root
- Product residue
- In hard water areas, mineral deposits

The twist base starts to mat with new growth. Removal becomes harder and causes more breakage.

Twists that have matted at the root may be difficult to separate without cutting. This is the main risk of leaving them in too long — not the style itself but the damage during removal.

## FAQs

**My twists are nice and I don't want to take them out at two weeks. Can I wash them and keep them in longer?**

Yes, but with monitoring. Wash the scalp at two weeks, dry fully, re-moisturise. If the root looks good and the scalp is healthy after washing, you can go another week. At three weeks, most hair types have enough new growth and matting risk that removal is appropriate.

**I left my twists in for three weeks and now they're partially matted at the root. What do I do?**

Do not pull. Saturate the matted area with conditioner, leave for a few minutes, and work through very gently with fingers. Work from the outer edges of the mat inward. For severe matting, a loctician or natural hair stylist can help with safe removal. Some hair loss at the mat is likely — that's normal for this situation.

**Can I refresh twists at one week to extend them to two?**

Yes. Spritz lightly with water and a small amount of leave-in on any twists that look dry or frizzy, then re-twist those specific sections. Don't do the whole head unless needed — over-wetting mid-week can cause the base of the twists to loosen.

**Can I leave a twist-out in longer than two nights before unravelling?**

Two nights is the maximum before the pattern starts to unravel inside the twist or the hair dries out to the point where the twist-out won't look right. If you need to delay, keep the twists in as a protective style and unravel when you want the twist-out.
