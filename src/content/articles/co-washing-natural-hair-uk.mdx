Co-washing — washing hair with conditioner only, skipping shampoo — works well for type 4 high-porosity hair that strips easily. It works poorly for fine-stranded wavy hair, low-porosity hair, and anyone with product buildup problems or scalp issues. If you've tried it and your hair felt like wet cotton or your scalp got itchy within a week, it wasn't for you.

---

## What Co-Washing Does

Conditioner has enough surfactant activity to remove sweat, everyday debris, and light product. It doesn't remove heavy product buildup, mineral deposits from hard water, or scalp sebum effectively. The result is clean-ish hair that retains more moisture than after a shampoo wash — and a scalp that needs occasional clarifying to reset.

The appeal for natural hair: sulphate shampoos strip the oils and moisture that coily and curly hair can't easily replace. Co-washing avoids that stripping. The risk: buildup accumulates faster, and without regular clarifying it affects curl definition and product absorption.

---

## Who Benefits From Co-Washing

**Type 4 hair (4A, 4B, 4C):** densely coiled hair that dries out quickly and strips noticeably with regular shampoo use. Co-washing between proper shampoo washes — or replacing most shampoo washes — works well here. The hair is thick enough to not get weighed down by conditioner, and the moisture retention is the priority.

**High-porosity hair:** the cuticle is open and loses moisture fast. Stripping with shampoo every wash day creates a cycle of dryness and breakage. Co-washing preserves moisture between proper clarifying washes.

**Very dry or damaged hair:** hair recovering from chemical treatment, heat damage, or extended neglect. The moisture maintenance from co-washing supports recovery. Clarify monthly; co-wash between.

---

## Who Co-Washing Doesn't Work For

**Fine-stranded wavy hair (2A, 2B, 2C):** fine hair gets weighed down by conditioner easily. Co-washing on type 2 hair often results in flat, greasy, definition-free waves within a day. The solution for fine wavy hair is a gentle, lightweight shampoo — not co-washing.

**Low-porosity hair:** the cuticle is tight and already resistant to absorption. Adding conditioner as the primary cleanser means more product sitting on the hair shaft surface rather than penetrating it. The result is buildup without the moisture benefit.

**Anyone with active scalp issues:** seborrhoeic dermatitis, dandruff, or any scalp condition that benefits from regular cleansing. Co-washing is not a substitute for the scalp cleanse shampoo provides. If your scalp needs regular washing, use shampoo regularly.

**Anyone in a hard water area without chelating:** hard water accelerates the buildup problem. If you're in London, the South East, or the Midlands, co-washing without monthly chelating will degrade your hair's condition within two to three months regardless of curl type.

## How to Co-Wash Correctly

If co-washing is right for your hair type:

1. **Use a conditioner designed for co-washing**, or a very slip-heavy rinse-out conditioner. Not a deep conditioner — too heavy to rinse fully.
2. **Apply generously to soaking-wet hair,** section by section, massaging into the scalp as well as the lengths.
3. **Work through with fingers,** detangling as you go.
4. **Rinse thoroughly.** Under-rinsed conditioner is the most common cause of the "limp and greasy" feeling people attribute to co-washing not working. Rinse longer than you think necessary.
5. **Follow with leave-in and seal** as normal.

---

## The Clarifying Schedule

Co-washing without clarifying creates buildup. The schedule:

- **Type 4, low product use, soft water area:** clarify every four to six weeks with a gentle shampoo
- **Type 4, higher product use or hard water area:** clarify monthly with a chelating shampoo (not just a clarifying one — chelating removes minerals; clarifying removes product only)
- **Any type using co-washing:** if hair suddenly feels waxy, heavy, or products stop absorbing, clarify immediately regardless of schedule

## FAQs

**I co-washed and my hair felt greasy and limp. Did I do it wrong?**

Either the conditioner is too heavy for your hair type, you didn't rinse thoroughly enough, or co-washing isn't right for your hair. Try rinsing for twice as long. If the result is the same, switch to a lightweight shampoo.

**Can I co-wash every wash day?**

For type 4 hair with minimal product use and soft water, yes. For everyone else, alternate with shampoo or clarify every four to six weeks at minimum.

**The conditioner I co-wash with says "rinse out" — is that the right product?**

Yes, a rinse-out conditioner is correct. Some people use a specific "co-wash" product, but a generous, slip-heavy rinse-out conditioner works equivalently. Don't use a deep conditioner for co-washing — it's too concentrated and thick to rinse fully.

**I live in London. Can I still co-wash?**

Yes, but chelate monthly. London water is very hard. Mineral buildup from hard water happens faster when you're not using shampoo to help clear it. Monthly chelating with a product containing EDTA or phytic acid keeps it manageable.
